Lesson:

Herbs and spices are the heartbeat of global cuisine. They carry history, geography, and memory in every pinch. Fresh herbs burst with oils that perfume your kitchen; dried ones concentrate intensity and warmth. Knowing when to introduce them matters: hearty herbs like rosemary, bay, and thyme thrive in stews and long cooking, slowly releasing oils into fats.
