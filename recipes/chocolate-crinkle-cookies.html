Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

These cookies are a holiday staple for a reason. They are essentially a fudgy brownie in cookie form—soft and chewy on the inside with a crisp, snowy powdered sugar coating on the outside. The "crackle" effect created as they bake makes them look as good as they taste.

Recommended Tools

  • Stand Mixer or Hand Mixer (Essential for mixing the thick, sticky batter)
  • Small Cookie Scoop (Crucial for handling sticky dough and getting even sizes)
  • Baking Sheets (Light-colored pans prevent the bottoms from burning)
  • Cooling Rack (To keep the bottoms crisp)

Ingredients

  • ½ cup corn oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 4 oz unsweetened chocolate, melted
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (sifted)
  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar (powdered sugar) for rolling

Directions

  1. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a large bowl or stand mixer, mix the oil, melted chocolate, and granulated sugar together until well combined.
  2. Add Eggs: Blend in the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  3. Combine Dry: In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  4. Make Dough: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined. The dough will be very soft and sticky—this is normal.
  5. Chill: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight. Do not skip this step, or the cookies will spread too much.
  6. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  7. Roll: Scoop the chilled dough into 1-inch balls. Drop the balls directly into a bowl of confectioners’ sugar and roll them until generously coated (you want a thick white layer).
  8. Bake: Place on the prepared cookie sheet spaced 2 inches apart. Bake for 10–12 minutes. The cookies should be set on the edges but still slightly soft in the center.
  9. Cool: Let them cool on the baking sheet for 2–3 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Notes

  • Chill Time: Chilling the dough overnight yields a deeper chocolate flavor and significantly better crinkles.
  • Don't Be Shy: Roll the dough generously in powdered sugar. If the coating is too thin, it will dissolve into the cookie while baking.
  • Texture: For extra soft cookies, slightly underbake them. They will firm up as they cool.
  • Flavor Boost: Add 1–2 teaspoons of espresso powder to the wet ingredients to intensify the chocolate flavor without making it taste like coffee.
  • Sticky Dough: If the dough becomes too sticky while rolling, pop the bowl back into the fridge for 10–15 minutes.
