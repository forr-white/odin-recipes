Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Submitted by Forrest White/Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

Ingredients

  • ½ cup corn oil
  • 4 oz unsweetened chocolate, melted
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 cups sifted flour
  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar (for rolling)

Directions

  1. Mix oil, melted chocolate, and sugar together.
  2. Blend in the eggs one at a time until well mixed.
  3. Add vanilla.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients and mix until combined.
  6. Chill dough (typically at least 1 hour or overnight).
  7. Roll dough into balls and coat generously in confectioners’ sugar.
  8. Place on a cookie sheet and bake at 350°F until set (usually 10–12 minutes).

Notes

  • Chill the dough overnight for deeper flavor and better crinkles.
  • Roll dough generously in powdered sugar—thin coating will disappear while baking.
  • For extra soft cookies, slightly underbake; edges should be set but centers still soft.
  • Add 1–2 teaspoons espresso powder to intensify the chocolate flavor.
  • If dough becomes sticky while rolling, pop it back into the fridge for a few minutes.
  • Use a cookie scoop for evenly sized cookies and more consistent baking.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: Per cookie, makes 36
