Vegetarian Cornbread Stuffing

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • American • Holiday

Stuffing is often the hero of the holiday table, and this vegetarian version ensures everyone can enjoy it. It starts with a homemade, slightly sweet cornbread that is dried out to absorb the savory vegetable broth and aromatics without turning into mush. The result is a perfect balance of the sweetness from the corn and the earthiness of sage and thyme.

  • 9-inch Round Cake Pan or Cast Iron Skillet (For the cornbread)
  • 9x13 Baking Dish (For the final stuffing)
  • Large Mixing Bowl

Ingredients

The Cornbread Base

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • ⅔ cup white sugar
  • 3½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg

The Stuffing Mix

  • 1 pan prepared cornbread (from above), cooled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons butter (or olive oil for vegan)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 ribs celery, diced
  • 3–4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1½–2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten (binder)
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Optional Add-ins

  • 1 cup sautéed mushrooms
  • ½ cup toasted pecans
  • ½ cup dried cranberries

Directions

Part 1: The Cornbread

  1. Make Batter: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan or skillet. Whisk flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Stir in milk, oil, and 1 egg until just combined (don't overmix).
  2. Bake: Pour batter into the pan and bake for 20–25 minutes until golden and a toothpick comes out clean. Let cool completely.
  3. Dry Out (Crucial): Cut the cooled cornbread into 1-inch cubes. Spread them on a baking sheet and bake at 300°F (150°C) for 15–20 minutes until the edges feel dry and toasted. Let cool. (This prevents soggy stuffing).

Part 2: Assembly

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9×13-inch baking dish.
  2. Sauté Veggies: Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and celery. Cook 8–10 minutes until soft. Add garlic, sage, thyme, parsley, pepper, and salt. Cook 1 minute more until fragrant.
  3. Combine: Place dried cornbread cubes in a very large bowl. Add the vegetable mixture and any optional add-ins (pecans, cranberries, etc). Toss gently.
  4. Hydrate: Drizzle 1½ cups of vegetable broth over the mixture, tossing gently. The bread should be moist but not disintegrating. If it feels dry, add more broth. Taste and adjust salt/pepper.
  5. Bind: Whisk the 2 eggs in a small bowl, pour over the mixture, and fold gently to combine.
  6. Bake: Transfer to the baking dish. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 10–15 minutes until the top is golden and crisp.
  7. Rest: Let rest for 10 minutes before serving to allow the stuffing to set.

Notes

  • Make Ahead: This is a great Thanksgiving prep dish. Bake the cornbread 2 days before. Cube and dry it 1 day before. Assemble the stuffing the morning of, and bake before dinner.
  • Vegan Option: Use oil instead of butter for sautéing. Omit the eggs in the stuffing step; add a little extra broth or a "flax egg" (2 tbsp ground flaxseed + 6 tbsp water) to help bind it.
  • Texture: Be gentle when mixing the broth into the bread. You want distinct cubes of cornbread in the final dish, not a bowl of mush.
