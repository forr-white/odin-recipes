Vegetarian Cornbread Stuffing

sides • American

Ingredients

Cornbread Base

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • ⅔ cup white sugar
  • 3 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg

Stuffing

  • 1 pan prepared cornbread (9-inch round), cooled and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons butter (or olive oil for vegan)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 ribs celery, diced
  • 3–4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½–1 teaspoon salt, to taste
  • 1½–2 cups vegetable broth, added as needed
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten (omit for vegan; see Notes)
  • Optional add-ins: 1 cup sautéed mushrooms, ½ cup toasted pecans, ½ cup dried cranberries, or 1 cup roasted butternut squash cubes

Directions

  1. Make the cornbread: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Lightly grease a 9-inch round cake pan or cast-iron skillet with butter or oil.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder, and salt until evenly combined.
  3. Create a small well in the center of the dry ingredients. Pour in the milk, vegetable oil, and egg. Whisk gently until just combined; a few small lumps are okay.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let the cornbread cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then remove and cool completely.
  5. Dry the cornbread: Once cooled, cut the cornbread into 1-inch cubes. Spread the cubes on a baking sheet and bake at 300°F (150°C) for 15–20 minutes, stirring once, until the edges feel dry but not hard. Let cool. (This step can be done a day ahead.)
  6. Prep the oven for stuffing: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9×13-inch baking dish.
  7. Sauté the vegetables: In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the diced onion and celery and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent, 8–10 minutes.
  8. Add the minced garlic, sage, thyme, parsley, black pepper, and ½ teaspoon salt. Cook for 1–2 minutes more, until fragrant. Remove from the heat.
  9. Combine the stuffing: Place the dried cornbread cubes in a large mixing bowl. Add the sautéed vegetables and any optional add-ins (mushrooms, pecans, cranberries, squash). Toss gently to combine.
  10. Drizzle 1½ cups of vegetable broth evenly over the mixture, tossing gently so the cornbread absorbs the liquid without turning mushy. Add more broth a little at a time if the mixture still feels dry. Taste and adjust seasoning with extra salt or pepper as needed.
  11. Whisk the eggs in a small bowl, then pour over the cornbread mixture and gently fold to combine, trying not to break up the cubes too much.
  12. Bake: Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it into an even layer. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  13. Remove the foil and continue baking for 10–15 minutes, or until the top is lightly golden and the stuffing is set in the center.
  14. Let the stuffing rest for 5–10 minutes before serving so it can firm up slightly and slice or scoop more cleanly.

Notes

  1. Vegan option: Use olive oil instead of butter and replace the eggs with 2–3 tablespoons ground flaxseed mixed with 6 tablespoons water (let sit 5–10 minutes to thicken).
  2. Make ahead: Cornbread can be baked and cooled up to 2 days in advance, then cubed and dried. You can assemble the stuffing up to the point before baking, cover, and refrigerate for up to 1 day. Add a splash of extra broth before baking if it looks dry.
  3. Texture control: Add broth gradually. The cornbread should be moist and just starting to clump together, not soggy. If it looks overly wet, bake uncovered a bit longer to dry the top.
  4. Flavor variations: Try adding finely chopped fresh sage, rosemary, or thyme, or folding in roasted vegetables like butternut squash or Brussels sprouts for extra flavor and color.
