Ground Beef Tostadas with Refried Beans

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican

This recipe features a "Picadillo" style filling—ground beef cooked with tender diced potatoes, onions, and tomatoes. When layered over a crunchy corn tortilla spread with creamy refried beans and topped with cool lettuce and salsa, it creates the perfect balance of texture and temperature.

Ingredients

Beef & Potato Filling

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 large russet potato, peeled and diced into small cubes (approx. ½-inch)
  • ½ small onion, diced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 Roma tomato, diced
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Assembly & Toppings

  • 12 tostada shells (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups refried beans (canned or homemade)
  • Shredded lettuce (Iceberg or Romaine)
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Sour cream or Mexican Crema
  • Jalapeño Salsa or Salsa Verde Taquera
  • Crumbling cheese (Cotija or Queso Fresco), optional

Directions

  1. Cook Potatoes: In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced potatoes. Sauté for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are golden on the outside and mostly soft on the inside.
  2. Brown Beef: Make a well in the middle of the potatoes (push them to the edges). Add the ground beef to the center. Break it up with a spoon and cook until browned.
  3. Add Aromatics: Once the beef is browned, add the diced onion, minced garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together (incorporating the potatoes) and cook for 2–3 minutes until the onions soften.
  4. Finish Filling: Stir in the diced Roma tomato. Reduce heat to low, cover the skillet, and let it steam for 2 minutes to fully soften the potatoes and meld the flavors. Turn off heat.
  5. Prep Beans: While the beef rests, heat the refried beans in a small saucepan. Tip: Stir in a splash of water or milk to make them easier to spread.
  6. Assemble: Spread a thin, even layer of warm refried beans onto a tostada shell. This acts as the "glue" for the meat.
  7. Top: Spoon the beef and potato mixture over the beans. Top generously with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, sour cream, salsa, and cheese if using. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Potato Size: Cut the potatoes small (¼ to ½ inch cubes). If they are too large, they won't cook through by the time the beef is done.
  • Preventing Breakage: Tostada shells are fragile. Be gentle when spreading the beans. Using warm beans makes them spread much smoother than cold beans.
  • Refried Beans: If using canned beans, fry them in a little bacon grease or oil for a few minutes to improve the flavor before serving.
