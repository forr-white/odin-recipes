Crispy Garlic Smashed Potatoes

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • American

Smashed potatoes offer the best of both worlds: the fluffy, creamy interior of mashed potatoes and the shatteringly crisp edges of roasted french fries. By boiling them first and then smashing them flat, you increase the surface area that touches the hot pan, resulting in maximum crunch.

  • Large Rimmed Baking Sheet (Essential for high-heat roasting)
  • Large Stock Pot (For boiling the potatoes)
  • Heavy Glass or Jar (The flat bottom is perfect for smashing the potatoes)

Ingredients

The Potatoes

  • 2 lbs small or baby Yukon Gold potatoes (uniform size is best)
  • Salt for boiling water
  • Olive oil spray (for the pan)

The Garlic Butter Topping

  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C). Grease a large baking sheet generously with non-stick olive oil spray or rub with oil.
  2. Boil: Place potatoes in a large stockpot and cover with cold water by about an inch. Liberally salt the water. Bring to a boil and cook for 20–25 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  3. Steam Dry (Crucial Step): Drain the potatoes in a colander. Let them sit in the colander for 5 minutes to let the steam escape. (Removing excess moisture now ensures they get crispy later).
  4. Smash: Arrange the potatoes on the prepared baking sheet. Using a potato masher or the flat bottom of a heavy glass, gently press down on each potato to flatten it until it is about ½-inch thick. Try to keep them in one piece, but messy edges are fine (they get crispy!).
  5. Season: In a small bowl, mix the melted butter, minced garlic, and chopped parsley. Brush or spoon this mixture generously over each smashed potato. Season liberally with salt and pepper.
  6. Roast: Bake for 10–15 minutes, or until the edges are deep golden brown and crispy.
  7. Finish: Remove from oven, sprinkle the Parmesan cheese over the top, and return to the oven for 2–3 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Serve hot.

Notes

  • Potato Choice: Use small, waxy potatoes like Baby Yukon Golds or Red Potatoes. Large potatoes don't smash well (they crumble), and Russets tend to fall apart completely.
  • The Glass Trick: If the potatoes stick to the bottom of your glass when smashing, spray the bottom of the glass with a little cooking spray or place a small piece of parchment paper between the glass and the potato.
  • Garlic Watch: 450°F is hot! Keep an eye on the garlic. If it starts to burn before the potatoes are crispy, tent the pan loosely with foil.
