Chow Mein

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • Chinese

True Chow Mein is all about texture: the snap of the bean sprouts, the crunch of the cabbage, and noodles that are slightly crispy on the outside but soft on the inside. This recipe uses classic Cantonese techniques to achieve that signature "Wok Hei" (breath of the wok) flavor right at home.

Ingredients

The Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon Chinese cooking wine (Shaoxing wine)
  • 2 tablespoons Dark Soy Sauce
  • 1½ teaspoons Oyster Sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • ½ teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch

The Stir Fry

  • 8–10 oz ramen noodles or chow mein noodles
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 green onions, sliced in 1-inch segments
  • 1 large rib of celery, sliced diagonally
  • 1 carrot, peeled and julienned
  • 3 cups green cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons water (for steaming noodles in the wok)

Directions

Prepare the Sauce

  1. Mix: In a small bowl, whisk together the Chinese cooking wine, dark soy sauce, ground white pepper, toasted sesame oil, and oyster sauce.
  2. Thicken: Whisk in the cornstarch until completely dissolved. Set aside.

Stir Fry

  1. Boil Noodles: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the noodles and cook according to the package directions (usually 2–3 minutes). Drain the noodles and rinse thoroughly with cold water to stop the cooking and remove excess starch.
  2. Season the Wok (Longyau): Add vegetable oil to a wok, swirl it around to coat the sides, and heat over medium-high. Heat until the oil begins to smoke slightly. Pour this oil out carefully and wipe the wok with a paper towel. (This creates a non-stick surface).
  3. Sear Veggies: Add fresh vegetable oil and heat back over medium-high heat. Once the oil is super hot, add the green onions, celery, carrots, and cabbage. Stir fry vigorously for about 3 minutes until vegetables have color and are soft but still retain a crunch. Remove vegetables from the wok and set aside.
  4. Fry Garlic: Add a little more oil to the wok and heat over medium-high. Add the chopped garlic and fry, swirling constantly, until golden brown (be careful not to burn it).
  5. Combine: Immediately add the cooked vegetables back to the wok. Add the noodles and toss everything together over high heat for 1 minute.
  6. Sauce & Steam: Pour in the sauce mixture and the 2 tablespoons of water. Stir fry for another 30 seconds to coat the noodles evenly.
  7. Finish: Add the bean sprouts and stir fry for a final 30 seconds (you want them to stay crisp). Remove from heat.
  8. Serve: Transfer to a bowl and garnish with extra sliced green onions if desired.

Notes

  • Noodles: Fresh Chow Mein noodles or Yakisoba noodles work best. If using dried ramen bricks, discard the seasoning packet.
  • High Heat: The secret to this dish is speed and heat. Have all your vegetables chopped and sauce mixed before you turn on the stove.
  • Dark Soy Sauce: This ingredient provides the rich caramel color. If you only have regular soy sauce, the flavor will be similar, but the noodles will be much paler.
