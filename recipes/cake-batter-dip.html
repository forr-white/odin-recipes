Cake Batter Dip

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

Remember fighting over who got to lick the spoon when Mom made a birthday cake? This dip captures that exact nostalgia in a safer, fluffier form. It’s essentially a deconstructed cheesecake batter mixed with Funfetti flavor. It’s perfect for kids' parties or a sweet movie night snack.

Recommended Tools

  • Stand Mixer or Hand Mixer (Essential for whipping the heavy cream to peaks)
  • Mixing Bowl (Deep enough to prevent splattering)
  • Rubber Spatula (For folding in the sprinkles gently)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ¼ cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1½ teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups confetti cake mix (dry, do not add eggs/oil)
  • ½ cup sour cream (adds tang and balances the sweetness)
  • ¼ cup rainbow sprinkles, plus more for finishing
  • Vanilla wafer cookies, animal crackers, or graham crackers for serving

Directions

  1. Whip Cream: In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the whisk attachment, whip the heavy cream to soft peaks, about 3 minutes.
  2. Sweeten: Add the confectioners’ sugar and vanilla extract. Continue to whip to medium peaks, about 1 minute more.
  3. Combine: Add the dry cake mix and mix on low speed until well combined. Add the sour cream and ¼ cup sprinkles, and mix gently until the dip is thick and smooth.
  4. Serve: Transfer to a serving bowl. Garnish with extra sprinkles and serve immediately with vanilla wafer cookies for dipping.

Notes

  • Food Safety Tip: Since raw flour can contain bacteria, it is recommended to "heat treat" the cake mix before using. Spread the dry mix on a baking sheet and bake at 350°F for 5 minutes, then let cool completely before adding to the dip.
  • Serve Fresh: It is recommended not to make the dip more than 1 hour ahead, because the sprinkles can bleed and discolor the dip over time.
  • Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 day.
  • Dippers: Aside from Nilla Wafers, this is great with pretzels (for a salty/sweet combo), strawberries, or Teddy Grahams.
