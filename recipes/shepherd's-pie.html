Shepherd's Pie

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • British / American

This is the ultimate meat-and-potatoes comfort meal. While purists will note that "Shepherd's Pie" is traditionally made with lamb (and beef versions are called "Cottage Pie"), this recipe uses the American standard of savory ground beef. It features a rich, double-gravy filling and a crispy broiled potato crust.

Ingredients

The Topping

  • 3 cups Mashed Potatoes (warm)

The Filling

  • 1 lb ground beef (85/15 or 90/10 lean)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ¾ cup yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1 rib celery, finely diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)

The Gravy Base

  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • ½ cup chicken or beef broth
  • 1 cup Brown Gravy (prepared)
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions

1. Prepare the Filling

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Preheat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the diced onions and celery. Sauté for 3 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Brown Beef: Increase heat to high. Add the ground beef. Cook, crumbling with a spoon, until browned and cooked through. Drain excess grease from the pan.
  3. Make Roux: Reduce heat to medium-high. Sprinkle the flour over the meat and toss to coat. Add the tomato paste and dried thyme. Cook for 1–2 minutes to toast the flour and paste.
  4. Deglaze & Simmer: Pour in the chicken broth to deglaze the pan, using a silicone spatula to scrape up any flavorful brown bits stuck to the bottom. Add half of the prepared Brown Gravy.
  5. Add Veggies: Stir in the frozen mixed vegetables. Cook until heated through.
  6. Adjust: Season with salt and pepper. If the mixture is too dry, stir in the remaining brown gravy until it reaches your desired sauciness. Remove from heat.

2. Assemble & Broil

  1. Layer: Transfer the meat mixture to a lightly greased 9-inch square pan or 7x11 casserole dish. Spread it into an even layer.
  2. Top: Carefully spread the warm mashed potatoes over the meat. Tip: Dollop spoonfuls all over first, then connect them gently to avoid mixing the potatoes with the gravy.
  3. Texture: Use a fork to "rake" lines or peaks into the surface of the potatoes. These ridges will catch the heat and become crispy.
  4. Broil: Set the oven rack about 5 inches away from the broiler element. Turn broiler to High (550°F). Broil for about 5 minutes. Watch closely after 2 minutes—it will go from golden brown to burned very quickly!
  5. Serve: Let stand for 5 minutes before serving to allow the filling to set slightly.

Notes

  • Bake vs. Broil: If you prepared the filling or potatoes ahead of time and they are cold, don't just broil. Bake the pie at 350°F (175°C) for 20 minutes to heat it through, then broil for 3 minutes to brown the top.
  • Cheese Option: For an extra rich crust, sprinkle ½ cup of shredded cheddar cheese or parmesan over the potatoes before broiling.
  • Lamb Variation: To make a true "Shepherd's Pie," simply swap the ground beef for ground lamb.
