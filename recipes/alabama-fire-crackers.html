Alabama Fire Crackers

snacks • Southern American

Crispy, salty, and packed with a slow-building heat, these Alabama Fire Crackers are the perfect party snack or game-day nibble. Make them ahead, let the flavors soak in overnight, and watch them disappear as soon as you set them out.

Ingredients

  • 1⅔ cups vegetable oil
  • 2 (1 ounce) envelopes ranch dressing mix
  • 3 tablespoons crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 (16.5 ounce) package saltine crackers

Directions

  1. Mix the seasoning oil: Add the vegetable oil, ranch dressing mix, crushed red pepper flakes, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper to a 2-gallon resealable plastic bag.
  2. Combine well: Seal the bag and squeeze/massage with your hands until the oil and spices are fully combined and no clumps of seasoning remain.
  3. Add the crackers: Place the crackers into the bag, seal, and gently turn and rotate the bag to coat all the crackers in the seasoned oil.
  4. Let the flavor soak in: Let the bag sit for about 1 hour, then turn and rotate again. Repeat several times until the crackers are evenly coated. Allow the bag to sit 8 hours or overnight so the crackers fully absorb the seasoning.
  5. Serve: Remove the crackers from the bag and serve as a snack, appetizer, or party mix.

Notes

  • Letting the crackers sit overnight gives the best flavor and crunch — the oil fully absorbs and the heat mellows into the perfect kick.
  • If you prefer a milder version, reduce the crushed red pepper flakes to 1–2 tablespoons.
  • For extra heat, add ½–1 teaspoon cayenne pepper or use spicy ranch seasoning.
  • Use regular saltines if you want a lighter texture; multigrain adds a heartier crunch.
  • Flip or rotate the bag several times during soaking to ensure every cracker gets evenly coated.
  • Store in an airtight container for up to one week — the flavor continues to develop over time.
  • These make great party snacks, holiday gifts, or additions to charcuterie boards.
