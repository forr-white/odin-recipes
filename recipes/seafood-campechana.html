Seafood Campechana

Submitted by Cook Anything Kitchen

Mexican • Seafood • Appetizer

Ingredients – Seafood

  • 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled, deveined, tails removed
  • 1 pound jumbo lump crabmeat, shells removed

Ingredients – Sauce

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 5 large garlic cloves, finely diced
  • 1/2 medium red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large celery stalks (about 1/2 cup), finely chopped
  • 1 medium cucumber (about 1/2 cup), unpeeled, finely chopped
  • 2 large jalapeños, seeded, finely diced
  • 1 small bunch cilantro (about 1/4 cup), finely chopped
  • 1 cup Heinz tomato ketchup
  • 1/2 cup sweet chili sauce
  • 1/2 cup Stubb’s Moppin’ Sauce Bar-B-Q Baste
  • 5 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Tabasco
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 medium avocados (about 1 cup), pitted, peeled, chopped, divided

Directions

  1. Cook the shrimp: Boil shrimp in salted water for about 5 minutes. Strain and cool in the refrigerator.
  2. Sauté the garlic: In a large skillet, heat olive oil and sauté garlic on medium-high for 1 minute. Set aside.
  3. Prep the vegetables: In a mixing bowl, combine onion, celery, cucumber, jalapeños, and cilantro. Mix and set aside.
  4. Make the sauce: In a separate bowl, whisk together ketchup, sweet chili sauce, Bar-B-Q baste, lemon juice, Tabasco, salt, and pepper. Stir in the sautéed garlic and all of the olive oil.
  5. Add avocado: Stir 1/2 cup of the chopped avocado into the sauce mixture.
  6. Combine: Add the sauce mixture to the vegetables and mix well.
  7. Fold in seafood: Gently fold in the chilled shrimp and crabmeat.
  8. Finish: Garnish with the remaining 1/2 cup avocado. Serve chilled with tortilla chips.

Notes

  • Chill at least 30 minutes before serving for best flavor.
  • For a spicier version, keep jalapeño seeds in or add extra Tabasco.
  • Great served with tortilla chips, saltines, or over shredded lettuce.
