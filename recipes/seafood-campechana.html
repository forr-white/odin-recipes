Seafood Campechana

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

appetizer • Mexican • Seafood

If you've ever had the famous Campechana at Goode Co. Seafood in Houston, you know this flavor profile. Unlike a standard shrimp cocktail, this version uses a "secret" combination of sweet chili sauce, ketchup, and vinegar-based BBQ sauce (Moppin' sauce) to create a smoky, sweet, and spicy base. Loaded with lump crab and shrimp, it is the ultimate party dip.

Ingredients

The Seafood

  • 1 lb medium shrimp, peeled, deveined, tails removed
  • 1 lb jumbo lump crabmeat, shells removed (picked over carefully)

The "Secret" Sauce Base

  • 1 cup Heinz tomato ketchup
  • ½ cup sweet chili sauce (e.g., Mae Ploy)
  • ½ cup Stubb’s Moppin’ Sauce (or other thin, vinegar-based BBQ sauce)
  • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 5 large garlic cloves, finely diced
  • 5 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Tabasco (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper

The Fresh Mix

  • 2 large avocados, pitted, peeled, and chopped (divided)
  • ½ medium red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large celery stalks, finely chopped (about ½ cup)
  • 1 medium cucumber, unpeeled, finely chopped (about ½ cup)
  • 2 large jalapeños, seeded and finely diced
  • 1 small bunch cilantro, finely chopped (about ¼ cup)

Directions

  1. Poach Shrimp: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add the shrimp and cook for just 2–3 minutes, until pink and opaque. Do not overcook. Immediately drain and plunge into an ice water bath to stop cooking. Drain again and chop into bite-sized pieces if they are large.
  2. Infuse Garlic Oil: In a large skillet, heat the ¼ cup olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced garlic and sauté for exactly 1 minute (do not burn). Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  3. Mix the Liquids: In a medium bowl, whisk together the ketchup, sweet chili sauce, Stubb’s Moppin’ Sauce, lemon juice, Tabasco, salt, and pepper. Stir in the sautéed garlic and all of the oil from the skillet.
  4. Combine Veggies: In a very large mixing bowl, combine the red onion, celery, cucumber, jalapeños, and cilantro. Pour the sauce mixture over the vegetables and mix well.
  5. Fold: Gently fold in the cooled shrimp. Carefully fold in the lump crabmeat (try to keep the lumps intact). Finally, gently fold in half of the chopped avocado.
  6. Chill & Serve: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let flavors meld (this is crucial). Before serving, top with the remaining fresh avocado. Serve with sturdy tortilla chips or saltines.

Notes

  • Stubb's Moppin' Sauce: This specific sauce is thin, vinegary, and spicy. If you can't find it, do not use thick brown BBQ sauce. Instead, mix a little apple cider vinegar with spices and a dash of liquid smoke, or find a "Carolina Style" vinegar BBQ sauce.
  • The Crab: Jumbo lump crab is expensive but worth it for the texture. If on a budget, use "special" or "claw" meat, but the texture will be more shredded.
  • Make Ahead: You can make the sauce and veggie mixture a day in advance. Fold in the seafood and avocado shortly before serving to keep them fresh.
