Goulash

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American Comfort Food

Not to be confused with the traditional Hungarian stew of the same name, this is American Goulash (sometimes called "American Chop Suey" or "Slumgullion"). It is the ultimate one-pot comfort meal: tender macaroni cooked directly in a rich, cheesy, tomato-beef sauce. It’s hearty, nostalgic, and feeds a crowd.

Recommended Tools

  • Dutch Oven or Deep Skillet (Essential: Needs to be large enough to hold the liquid and the expanding pasta)
  • Wooden Spoon (For breaking up the beef and stirring the thick sauce)
  • Cheese Grater (Freshly grated cheddar melts much smoother than pre-bagged)

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 1 Tbsp. tomato paste
  • 1 (15-oz.) can diced tomatoes (undrained)
  • 1 (15-oz.) can tomato sauce
  • 1¼ cups low-sodium beef broth
  • 1 tsp. Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp. paprika (sweet or smoked)
  • 1½ cups elbow macaroni (dry)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for serving

Directions

  1. Sauté Aromatics: In a large deep skillet or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened (about 7 minutes). Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute more.
  2. Brown Beef: Add the ground beef. Season generously with salt and pepper. Cook, breaking up the meat with a wooden spoon, until it is browned and no longer pink (about 6 minutes). Drain excess fat if necessary.
  3. Build Sauce: Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1 minute to remove the raw flavor. Pour in the diced tomatoes (with juices), tomato sauce, and beef broth. Add the Italian seasoning and paprika. Stir well.
  4. Simmer Pasta: Stir in the dry elbow macaroni. Bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking, until the pasta is tender (about 10–15 minutes).
  5. Finish: Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and creamy. Taste and season with more salt and pepper if needed. Remove from heat.
  6. Serve: Top with fresh chopped parsley and serve warm.

Notes

  • One Pot Magic: Cooking the pasta directly in the sauce releases starch, which naturally thickens the mixture into a rich gravy. Do not cook the pasta separately!
  • Cheese: Sharp cheddar provides the best flavor contrast to the sweet tomato sauce.
  • Add-ins: For extra nutrition, you can stir in diced bell peppers or corn kernels along with the onions.
  • Leftovers: Pasta absorbs liquid as it sits. When reheating leftovers, add a splash of water or broth to loosen it up.
