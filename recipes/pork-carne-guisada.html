Pork Carne Guisada

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Tex-Mex

"Carne Guisada" literally translates to "stewed meat." While often made with beef, this pork version is a staple of South Texas and Northern Mexican cooking. The secret here is the flour-thickened gravy enriched with beer and spices, which coats the tender pork and potatoes. It's best served simply: in a bowl with a hot flour tortilla to scoop up the sauce.

Ingredients

The Stew Solids

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder (butt), cut into 1½-inch cubes
  • 2 large red potatoes, unpeeled and chopped into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded, deveined, and finely chopped

The Gravy Base

  • 1 bottle (12 oz) beer (Amber or Lager works best)
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon red wine vinegar
  • Dash of liquid smoke (optional)

Spices

  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 2–3 teaspoons crushed red pepper flakes (adjust to heat preference)
  • 1½ teaspoons ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 bay leaf

Directions

  1. Make the Gravy Base: In the insert of your slow cooker, pour in the beer. Add the flour and whisk vigorously until dissolved and no lumps remain. Whisk in the tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, vinegar, liquid smoke, garlic, and all the dry spices (chili powder, red pepper flakes, cumin, paprika, salt).
  2. Combine: Add the cubed pork, chopped potatoes, onions, and jalapeno to the liquid. Stir well to coat everything in the sauce base. Add the bay leaf on top.
  3. Slow Cook: Cover and cook on Low for 7–9 hours. The pork should be fork-tender and falling apart, and the sauce should be thick.
  4. Finish: Remove and discard the bay leaf. If there is excess fat on the surface, skim it off with a spoon. Use two forks to gently shred the pork (leave some chunks for texture).
  5. Serve: Serve hot in bowls with warm flour tortillas, or use as a filling for tacos.

Notes

  • Browning Option: For deeper flavor, you can sear the pork cubes in a skillet with a little oil before adding them to the slow cooker, but this recipe works well as a "dump and go" meal too.
  • Beer Choice: A Mexican lager (like Modelo or Dos Equis) or a darker amber ale (like Shiner Bock) adds the best depth to the gravy.
  • Consistency: If the stew is too thin at the end, mash a few of the cooked potatoes against the side of the pot and stir them in—the potato starch will thicken the gravy instantly.
