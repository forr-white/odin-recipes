Southwest Chicken Salad

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

lunch • American Southwest

This takes standard chicken salad and gives it a Tex-Mex upgrade. Instead of celery and grapes, we use smoky cumin, crispy bacon, and fresh cilantro. It's creamy, crunchy, and packed with protein. It works beautifully scooped up with tortilla chips, stuffed into a pita, or served over a bed of greens.

Ingredients

The Salad Base

  • 4 medium boneless skinless chicken breasts (approx. 24 oz), cooked and cooled
  • ½ cup cooked bacon, chopped (about 4–5 strips)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced (red adds nice color contrast)
  • 1 tomato, seeded and diced
  • ½ cup red onion, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 whole avocado, diced

Creamy Lime Dressing

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • ½ teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder

Directions

  1. Shred Chicken: Shred the cooked, cooled chicken. (Tip: Place warm chicken breasts in a stand mixer with the paddle attachment on low speed for 30 seconds to shred instantly). Place in a large bowl.
  2. Prep Veggies: Dice the bell pepper, tomato, onion, cilantro, and bacon. Add these to the bowl with the chicken.
  3. Make Dressing: In a small separate bowl or jar, whisk together the mayonnaise, lime juice, cumin, salt, and garlic powder until smooth.
  4. Combine: Pour the dressing over the chicken and vegetable mixture. Toss well to coat everything evenly.
  5. The Avocado: Gently fold in the diced avocado last. You want the cubes to stay intact rather than mashing into guacamole.
  6. Serve: Serve immediately with crackers, on bread, or over lettuce.

Notes

  • Avocado Tip: Pick an avocado that is slightly firm so it holds its shape. If you are making this for meal prep (to eat later in the week), do not add the avocado to the main bowl. Add fresh avocado to your serving right before you eat to prevent browning.
  • Shortcut: A rotisserie chicken works perfectly for this recipe if you are short on time.
  • Storage: Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days (1–2 days if avocado is already mixed in).
  • Cilantro Haters: If you have the gene that makes cilantro taste like soap, swap it for flat-leaf parsley or green onions.
