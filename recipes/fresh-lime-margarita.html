Fresh Lime Margarita

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • Mexican

Put down the neon green bottle of sour mix. A real margarita needs only three main components: tequila, orange liqueur, and fresh citrus. This recipe balances tart lime with a splash of lemon for brightness, creating a clean, crisp cocktail that is dangerously easy to drink.

Recommended Tools

  • Citrus Juicer (Essential—bottled lime juice will ruin this drink)
  • Small Pitcher (For mixing a batch)
  • Margarita Glasses (Though a rocks glass works just fine)

Ingredients

  • ½ cup Tequila (Blanco or Silver recommended)
  • ¼ cup Triple Sec or Cointreau
  • ¼ cup fresh lime juice (approx. 2 limes)
  • ¼ cup fresh lemon juice (approx. 1 large lemon)
  • 2 tablespoons superfine sugar or agave nectar
  • 1⅓ cups crushed ice
  • For Garnish: 4 lime wedges, 1 tablespoon coarse kosher salt

Directions

  1. Rim the Glass: Pour the kosher salt onto a small plate. Run a lime wedge around the rims of 4 glasses. Dip the rims into the salt to coat. Fill the glasses with fresh ice.
  2. Mix: In a pitcher, combine the tequila, Triple Sec, lime juice, lemon juice, and sugar (or agave).
  3. Dissolve: Stir vigorously until the sugar is completely dissolved.
  4. Serve: Pour the mixture over the crushed ice in the prepared glasses. Garnish with a fresh lime wedge.

Notes

  • Tequila Choice: Use 100% Blue Agave tequila. "Mixto" tequilas (which aren't 100% agave) are the main culprit for hangovers.
  • Sweetness: If using agave nectar instead of sugar, stir well as it is thicker. Adjust the amount to taste depending on how sour your limes are.
  • Watermelon Twist: Add ¾ cup of cran-watermelon juice and increase the tequila to ¾ cup for a refreshing fruity variation.
  • Spicy Option: Muddle a slice of jalapeño in the bottom of the pitcher before adding the liquids for a spicy kick.
