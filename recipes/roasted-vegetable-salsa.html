Roasted Vegetable Salsa

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Mexican

Ingredients

  • 2 large tomatoes, halved
  • 1 red bell pepper, halved and seeded
  • 1 poblano pepper, halved and seeded
  • 1 small red onion, quartered
  • 3 cloves garlic, unpeeled
  • 1 jalapeño (optional, for heat)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 4 tbsp lime juice (juice from 2 limes)
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Place tomatoes, bell pepper, poblano, onion, garlic, and jalapeño (if using) on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast for 25-30 minutes, until vegetables are charred and soft. Let cool slightly.
  4. Peel the garlic and transfer all roasted vegetables to a blender or food processor. Add cilantro, lime juice, cumin, salt, and pepper. Pulse until slightly chunky or smooth, depending on preference.
  5. Adjust seasoning and set aside.

Notes

  • For smoky depth: Add one chipotle pepper in adobo or 1–2 teaspoons of the adobo sauce when blending.
  • For thicker salsa: Drain excess liquid from the roasted vegetables before blending.
  • Texture control: Pulse in short bursts to keep the salsa chunky, or blend longer for a smooth finish.
  • Heat adjustment: Remove jalapeño seeds for mild heat, use the whole pepper for medium, or add a serrano for extra spice.
  • Make-ahead tip: Flavor improves after chilling 4–6 hours; keeps 5–7 days in the refrigerator.
  • Serving ideas: Excellent with tacos, grilled meats, roasted potatoes, or breakfast eggs.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 6 servings (about 1/4 cup each)

Fat: 4 g

Carbs: 6 g
