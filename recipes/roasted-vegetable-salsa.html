Roasted Vegetable Salsa

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Mexican

In Mexico, roasting ingredients until the skins are charred is called "tatemar." This technique adds a complex, smoky depth that fresh salsa simply can't match. By roasting the tomatoes, peppers, and garlic together, the sugars caramelize and the flavors concentrate, creating a rich salsa that tastes like it simmered all day.

Ingredients

The Roasting Pan

  • 2 large tomatoes, halved
  • 1 red bell pepper, halved and seeded
  • 1 poblano pepper, halved and seeded
  • 1 small red onion, peeled and quartered
  • 3 cloves garlic, unpeeled (leave skin on to prevent burning)
  • 1 jalapeño (optional, for heat)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

The Blender Finish

  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped (leaves and tender stems)
  • 4 tbsp fresh lime juice (approx. 2 limes)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • Salt to taste

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Season: Arrange the tomatoes, bell pepper, poblano, onion, garlic cloves (still in skins), and jalapeño on a large rimmed baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle generously with salt and pepper. Toss to coat.
  3. Roast: Roast for 25–30 minutes. You want the vegetables to be soft and the skins to have distinct charred, black spots.
  4. Cool & Peel Garlic: Remove from oven and let cool for 5–10 minutes. Squeeze the soft, roasted garlic out of its papery skin. Discard the skin.
  5. Blend: Transfer all roasted vegetables (including any juices on the pan) and the squeezed garlic to a food processor. Add the fresh cilantro, lime juice, and cumin.
  6. Pulse: Pulse 5–10 times for a chunky salsa, or blend continuously for a smoother restaurant-style consistency.
  7. Finish: Taste and adjust with more salt or lime juice if needed. Serve warm or chilled.

Notes

  • Garlic Tip: Leaving the skin on the garlic while roasting steams the clove inside, making it sweet and spreadable, rather than burning it bitter.
  • Chipotle Twist: For an even smokier flavor, add 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce to the blender.
  • Consistency: If the salsa is too watery (tomatoes vary in water content), drain some of the liquid from the pan before adding the veggies to the blender.
  • Make Ahead: This salsa tastes even better the next day after the flavors have melded. It keeps for 5–7 days in the fridge.
