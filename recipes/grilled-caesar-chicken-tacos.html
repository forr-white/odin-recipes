Grilled Caesar Chicken Tacos

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Fusion (Tex-Mex + American)

This recipe utilizes the viral "smash taco" technique—spreading raw meat directly onto the tortilla and cooking it face-down. This creates an incredibly crispy, caramelized chicken layer that bonds to the tortilla. Topped with cold, crunchy romaine and rich dressing, it’s a perfect hot-and-cold fusion dinner.

Ingredients

Base

  • 10 small flour tortillas (taco size)
  • 1 lb ground chicken
  • 1–2 tbsp taco seasoning or Greek seasoning (depending on desired flavor profile)
  • 1–2 cups chopped romaine lettuce
  • ½–1 cup Caesar dressing (homemade or high-quality bottled)
  • Shredded Parmesan cheese

Optional Add-Ins

  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup pico de gallo
  • Avocado slices

Directions

  1. Prep Tortillas: Lay all 10 tortillas flat on a clean work surface (like a cutting board or counter).
  2. Spread Chicken: Divide the raw ground chicken evenly among the tortillas (about a generous tablespoon each). Using a spoon or fingers, spread the meat into a thin, even layer all the way to the very edges of the tortilla. Note: It shrinks as it cooks, so go to the edge!
  3. Season: Sprinkle your seasoning of choice (Taco or Greek) evenly over the raw chicken layer.
  4. Cook Meat-Side Down: Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium-high heat with a little oil spray. Place the tortillas chicken-side down onto the hot surface.
  5. Sear: Cook for 4–6 minutes without moving them. The chicken should be fully cooked, browned, and crispy. The tortilla might puff up slightly.
  6. Crisp Tortilla: Flip the tacos over. Cook the tortilla side for 1–2 minutes just to warm it up and make it slightly crisp.
  7. Assemble: Remove from heat. Immediately top with the cold chopped romaine, a generous drizzle of Caesar dressing, and parmesan cheese. Fold and eat immediately.

Notes

  • Spreading Technique: Spreading the raw chicken directly on the tortillas keeps the meat juicy and prevents it from falling out later. The meat acts as a glue.
  • Temperature Contrast: These are best enjoyed immediately. The magic is the contrast between the hot, salty chicken and the cold, crisp salad topping.
  • Seasoning: Taco seasoning gives it a Mex-Italian fusion vibe. Greek seasoning (oregano, garlic, lemon pepper) leans more into the classic Caesar profile.
