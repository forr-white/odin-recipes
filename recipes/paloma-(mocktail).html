Paloma (Mocktail)

Submitted by Forrest White

drinks • Mexican

While the Margarita gets all the fame, the Paloma is arguably the more refreshing choice for a hot day. This alcohol-free version retains the sophisticated bitterness of fresh grapefruit and the zip of lime, making it a complex drink that doesn't feel like "just juice." It's bubbly, hydrating, and perfectly balanced.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz fresh grapefruit juice (pink or ruby red is best)
  • ½ oz fresh lime juice
  • ¼ oz agave nectar or simple syrup (adjust to sweetness preference)
  • 2 oz sparkling water (or club soda)
  • Coarse sea salt (for the rim)
  • Ice cubes
  • Grapefruit wedge or lime slice (for garnish)

Directions

  1. Rim the Glass: Pour some coarse salt onto a small plate. Rub a lime wedge around the rim of your highball glass to moisten it. Dip the rim into the salt to coat.
  2. Ice: Fill the prepared glass to the top with fresh ice cubes.
  3. Mix Base: In a shaker or a separate small glass, combine the grapefruit juice, lime juice, and agave nectar. Stir or shake vigorously until the agave is completely dissolved.
  4. Assemble: Pour the juice mixture over the ice. Top with the sparkling water.
  5. Garnish: Give it a gentle stir to combine (don't stir too hard or you'll lose the bubbles). Garnish with a fresh wedge of grapefruit or a wheel of lime.

Notes

  • Soda Swap: For a sweeter, "soda shop" version, replace the sparkling water and agave with a Mexican grapefruit soda like Jarritos or Squirt.
  • Make it Spicy: Add a slice of jalapeño to the shaker for a spicy kick that pairs beautifully with the grapefruit.
  • Spirit-Free Spirits: To mimic the burn and flavor of tequila, add a shot of a non-alcoholic alternative like Ritual or Lyre’s Agave Spirit.
