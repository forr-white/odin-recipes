Cheddar Jalapeño Bread

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

bread • Southwestern American

This isn't your average sandwich bread. Loaded with sharp cheddar and spicy jalapeños, this loaf has a tender crumb and a crispy, cheesy crust. The "crisping" step at the end ensures the sides aren't soggy, making it sturdy enough for the ultimate grilled cheese.

  • Wire Cooling Rack (Essential for crisping the crust)

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp instant yeast (less than a ¼-oz package)
  • 4 cups (500 g) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1 Tbsp Diamond Crystal or 1¾ tsp Morton kosher salt, plus more for egg wash
  • ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for greasing pan
  • 1¾ cups warm water (105–115°F)
  • 3 medium jalapeños, finely chopped (seeds removed for less heat if desired)
  • 6 oz extra-sharp cheddar, coarsely grated (about 1½ cups)
  • 2 oz Parmesan, finely grated (about ½ cup plus 1 Tbsp)
  • 1 large egg (for wash)

Directions

  1. Proof Yeast: Stir together 1 tsp instant yeast and 1 Tbsp warm water in a small bowl; let mixture stand until foamy, about 5 minutes. (If it doesn't foam, discard and start over with new yeast).
  2. Mix Dough: In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the dough hook, combine the flour, salt, olive oil, yeast mixture, and remaining 1¾ cups warm water. Beat on low speed until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead: Scrape down sides of bowl, then increase speed to medium-high and beat 3 minutes more.
  4. Add Mix-ins: Add the chopped jalapeños, and all but one big pinch of the cheddar and Parmesan (reserve those pinches for the topping). Beat until combined.
  5. First Rise: Scrape down sides of bowl, gathering dough into a ball in the center. Sprinkle the top lightly with flour. Cover with a clean kitchen towel (not terry cloth) and let rise in a warm, draft-free place until doubled in bulk, 1–1½ hours. (Alternatively, let dough rise in refrigerator 8–12 hours).
  6. Shape: Turn dough onto a very well-floured surface. With floured hands, gently pat it into a roughly 11x8" rectangle. Fold the dough in thirds (like a letter), pressing along the seam of each fold to seal.
  7. Second Rise: Place dough, seam side down, in an oiled 9x5" loaf pan. Cover and let rise until dough completely fills the pan and rises slightly above the rim, about 45 minutes to 1 hour.
  8. Preheat: Halfway through the second rise, preheat oven to 400°F (200°C) with the rack in the middle position.
  9. Top and Bake: Beat the egg with a pinch of salt. Brush the top of the loaf with the egg wash, then sprinkle with the reserved cheese. Bake until golden brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom, 50 minutes to 1 hour.
  10. Crisp the Crust: Let cool slightly, then run a knife around the edge to loosen. Tip the bread out onto a baking sheet lined with a wire rack. Place the loaf on its side on the rack and return to the oven for 10 minutes to crisp the bottom and sides.
  11. Cool: Remove from oven, set upright, and let cool completely on the rack before slicing (at least 1½ hours).

Notes

  • Cooling is Key: It is tempting to slice warm bread, but slicing too early releases steam and makes the texture gummy. Wait the full 1.5 hours!
  • Sticky Dough: This is a hydration-rich dough. Do not be alarmed if it is stickier than standard bread dough; use plenty of flour on your hands when shaping.
  • Heat Level: For a milder bread, remove the seeds and white ribs from the jalapeños. For extra spice, leave them in or add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the flour.
