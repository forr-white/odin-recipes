Slow-Braised Spiced Boneless Leg of Lamb

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mediterranean

This isn't your average Sunday roast. Heavily influenced by North African flavors, this leg of lamb is rubbed with a fragrant paste of cumin, turmeric, and cinnamon, then braised in a rich tomato broth. The cooking method—starting at high heat to jumpstart the cooking, then dropping low while sealed tight—results in meat so tender you can carve it with a spoon.

Ingredients

The Lamb & Rub

  • 4–5 lb boneless leg of lamb, rolled and tied
  • 6 whole garlic cloves, peeled
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Optional: 1 tbsp Dijon mustard or lemon zest

The Braising Liquid

  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken or beef broth
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2–3 carrots, cut into large chunks
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (for the pan)

Directions

1. Prepare the Lamb

  1. Preheat: Preheat the oven to 450°F (232°C). Pat the lamb dry with paper towels.
  2. Stud with Garlic: Using a sharp paring knife, make 6 deep slits (about 1–2 inches deep) all around the lamb. Insert one whole peeled garlic clove deep into each slit.
  3. Make Paste: In a small bowl, combine the salt, pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, turmeric, cinnamon, and 3 tablespoons of olive oil. Mix to form a loose paste.
  4. Season: Rub the spice paste all over the lamb, massaging it into the crevices.

2. Assemble & Braise

  1. Bed of Veggies: Drizzle 1 tablespoon of oil in the bottom of a large roasting pan. Spread the onion quarters and carrot chunks across the bottom. Pour the broth around the vegetables.
  2. Layer: Place the seasoned lamb on top of the vegetables. Pour the can of crushed tomatoes directly over the lamb, spreading it to coat the meat fully.
  3. Seal: Lay a sheet of parchment paper over the lamb (this prevents the acidic tomatoes from eating through the foil). Cover the pan tightly with 2–3 layers of heavy-duty aluminum foil. The seal must be airtight.
  4. The "Blast": Roast at 450°F for 1 hour. This brings the liquid to a boil and starts the browning process.
  5. The "Slow Cook": Reduce oven temperature to 350°F (175°C). Continue roasting for an additional 3 to 4 hours.
    • 3 hours: Sliceable but very tender.
    • 4 hours: Fall-apart tender (shreddable).
  6. Rest & Serve: Remove from the oven and carefully peel back the foil (watch out for steam). Let the lamb rest in the juices for 20 minutes before serving. Serve the meat with the pan juices and softened vegetables.

Notes

  • Why Parchment? Acidic foods like tomatoes can react with aluminum foil during long cooking times, creating small holes in the foil and a metallic taste in the food. The parchment paper acts as a barrier.
  • Serving Suggestions: This dish has a lot of rich sauce. Serve it over mashed potatoes, polenta, or couscous to soak up the juices.
  • Make Ahead: The lamb tastes even better the next day. You can season the raw lamb and refrigerate it overnight before cooking for deeper flavor penetration.
