Slow-Braised Spiced Boneless Leg of Lamb

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mediterranean

Ingredients

For the Lamb

  • 4–5 lb boneless leg of lamb, rolled and tied
  • 6 whole garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Optional: 1 tbsp Dijon mustard or 1 tbsp lemon zest

For the Pan

  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2–3 carrots, cut into large chunks
  • 2 cups chicken or beef broth
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (for vegetables)
  • Parchment paper (to cover the lamb before foiling)
  • Heavy-duty aluminum foil (2–3 layers to seal the pan)

Directions

Prepare the Lamb

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F (232°C). Pat the boneless leg of lamb dry with paper towels.
  2. Using a sharp paring knife, make 6 deep slits (about 1–2 inches) all around the lamb. Insert one whole garlic clove into each slit.
  3. In a bowl, combine the kosher salt, black pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, turmeric, and cinnamon. Add 3 tablespoons olive oil (and Dijon mustard or lemon zest, if using) to form a paste.
  4. Rub the spice paste all over the lamb, coating every side thoroughly.

Assemble & Cook

  1. Spread the onion and carrots across the bottom of a large roasting pan. Pour the broth around the vegetables.
  2. Place the seasoned lamb on top of the vegetables.
  3. Pour the full 28 ounces of crushed tomatoes over the lamb, spreading them to fully coat the meat.
  4. Lay a sheet of parchment paper over the lamb, tucking edges down to prevent sticking.
  5. Cover the pan tightly with heavy-duty foil, then add 1–2 additional layers to fully seal in moisture.
  6. Roast at 450°F for 1 hour.
  7. Reduce oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue roasting an additional 3–4 hours (3 hours for sliceable lamb, 4 hours for fall-apart tender).
  8. Remove from the oven and carefully peel back the foil and parchment. Let the lamb rest for 20 minutes before slicing or shredding.
  9. Optional: Make a thicker sauce by simmering the tomato and vegetable juices with a splash of broth or wine until slightly reduced.

Notes

  • For maximum flavor, you can season the lamb and refrigerate it overnight before cooking.
  • Using parchment paper under the foil prevents sticking and preserves the spice rub.
  • The longer cook time (4 hours total) will produce an ultra-tender, shreddable texture.
