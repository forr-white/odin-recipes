Creole Succotash from Treme

Adapted from Treme / New Orleans Tradition

dinner • Creole

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 2 ribs celery, chopped
  • 1 pound fresh or frozen okra, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 pound fresh or frozen lima beans
  • 1 pound fresh or frozen corn kernels
  • 1 pound smoked pork sausage, cut into ¾-inch slices
  • 1 pound boneless smoked ham, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Pinch of dried thyme
  • ½ to 1 cup water (reserved, if needed)
  • 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Hot cooked rice for serving

Directions

  1. In a large heavy-bottomed saucepan or Dutch oven over medium heat, melt the butter.
  2. Add the tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, and celery. Stir well to coat in the fat.
  3. Cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender (10 to 15 minutes).
  4. Stir in the okra, lima beans, corn, sausage, ham, bay leaves, and thyme.
  5. Cook, still uncovered, until vegetables are soft (about 1 hour), stirring occasionally.
  6. If the mixture seems dry toward the end of cooking, stir in water a little at a time to moisten, then cover the pan and continue cooking.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the shrimp. Cook, stirring often, until the shrimp are cooked through and flavors have blended (about 15 minutes longer).
  8. Remove from heat. Mound rice on large plates, spoon the succotash over the rice, and serve.

Notes

  • The Trinity: This recipe relies on the "Holy Trinity" of Cajun cooking (onions, bell peppers, celery) for its base flavor.
  • Okra Texture: Cooking the okra for a long period in the tomato base helps mitigate the "slime" texture often associated with it.
  • Yield: This is a large-batch recipe, perfect for feeding a crowd or for meal prepping leftovers.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 1 Bowl (1 ½ w/ Rice), about 8 servings

Fat: 18 g

Carbohydrates: 45 g

Protein: 34 g
