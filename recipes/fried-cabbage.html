Fried Cabbage

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • Southern American

This isn't your healthy steamed cabbage. This is Southern-style fried cabbage—sweet, savory, and cooked down in plenty of bacon grease until tender. It balances the tangy bite of apple cider vinegar with the richness of brown sugar. It is the perfect side dish for pork chops, cornbread, or black-eyed peas.

  • Dutch Oven or Large Pot (Raw cabbage has huge volume; you need a big pot to start)
  • Chef's Knife (For chopping the bacon and cabbage)
  • Wooden Spoon (For stirring the cabbage as it wilts)

Ingredients

  • 12 oz bacon, diced raw
  • 1 head green cabbage, cored and chopped into 1-inch squares
  • 1 large white onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Salt (optional, taste first)

Directions

  1. Render Bacon: Add the chopped bacon to a large pot or Dutch oven. Cook over medium heat until the fat has rendered and the bacon is extra crispy.
  2. Save Grease: Remove the cooked bacon bits from the pot with a slotted spoon and set aside. Leave the bacon grease in the pot.
  3. Wilt Cabbage: Add the chopped cabbage to the hot grease. Cook over medium-high heat for about 10 minutes, stirring every minute or so. The cabbage will shrink significantly.
  4. Add Onion: Add the diced onion and continue cooking for 5 more minutes, still stirring frequently to prevent burning.
  5. Season: Add the Worcestershire sauce, apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, and brown sugar. Stir well to combine.
  6. Simmer: Reduce the heat to medium-low. Continue cooking for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cabbage is completely tender and has absorbed the flavors.
  7. Finish: Stir the crispy bacon back into the cabbage. Taste and add generous amounts of black pepper. Add salt only if needed (the bacon usually provides enough). Serve warm.

Notes

  • Volume Warning: When you first put the cabbage in, it might look like it won't fit. Don't worry—it loses water rapidly and will shrink down by half.
  • Spicy Kick: If you like heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or Cajun seasoning along with the onions.
  • Main Dish: To turn this into a full meal, slice up smoked sausage or kielbasa and fry it along with the cabbage.
  • Salt Check: Be careful with added salt. Between the bacon, the grease, and the Worcestershire sauce, the dish is naturally salty.
