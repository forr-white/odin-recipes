Divinity Cookies

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

Ingredients

  • 2 ⅔ cup sugar
  • ⅔ cup corn syrup
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 egg whites
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • ⅔ cup chopped pecans
  • 20 pecan halves, to decorate

Directions

  1. Line baking sheet with parchment paper and then set aside.
  2. Add water, corn syrup and sugar to a saucepan. Mix to combine.
  3. Place saucepan over medium heat and stir the mixture continuously. Use a candy thermometer to monitor the temperature. When the mixture just starts to boil, stop stirring and let it boil. Do not stir again.
  4. Whip egg whites in the bowl of your stand mixer at medium to high speed until they form stiff peaks. Set them aside until the syrup mixture is prepared.
  5. Apply nonstick spray to two spoons. Set aside for later use.
  6. When the syrup mixture hits 250F degrees (hard ball stage), take the saucepan off the heat source.
  7. Switch on your mixer with the egg whites and set to medium speed.
  8. Very slowly and carefully, pour the hot syrup into the beaten egg whites in a steady stream. It should take about 5 minutes. Make sure the mixer is on before you begin to pour.
  9. After pouring all of the hot sugar mixture and making sure it’s well combined with the egg whites, add the vanilla. Increase the speed to high. Continue beating until the mixture loses its shine and the bowl is no longer hot to the touch.
  10. Take the bowl off the stand mixer and add the chopped pecans. Gently fold them into the mixture.
  11. Take a spoonful of the mixture and use the other spoon to transfer and drop little mounds onto the baking sheet. Repeat until you've portioned out the entire mixture into 20 mounds.
  12. Add a pecan half on top of each mound and press into place.
  13. Let your batch of divinity cookies sit for approximately 30 minutes to firm up,
