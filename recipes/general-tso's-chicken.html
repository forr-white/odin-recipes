General Tso's Chicken

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Chinese American

This is arguably the king of Chinese-American takeout. It features crispy chunks of dark meat chicken tossed in a glossy, sticky sauce that perfectly balances savory soy, sweet sugar, tangy vinegar, and spicy chilies. Making it at home ensures the chicken stays crispy and you can control the heat level.

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch knob ginger, minced
  • Pinch of salt
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • Neutral oil for frying (vegetable, canola, or peanut)

For the Sauce

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1.5 tablespoons vinegar (rice vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (mixed with the vinegar to create a slurry)
  • ½ cup chicken stock
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2-inch knob ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Chinese chili flakes
  • 5 dried red chili peppers (whole)

Garnish

  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Thinly sliced green onions

Directions

Prepare the Chicken

  1. Marinate: Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces and place in a large mixing bowl. Add the minced ginger, minced garlic, Shaoxing wine, and soy sauce. Toss well and let marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight in the fridge).
  2. Heat Oil: Add about 2 inches of oil to a heavy pot or wok. Attach a thermometer and bring the temperature to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Dredge: Whisk together the all-purpose flour and cornstarch in a shallow bowl. Toss the marinated chicken pieces in the flour mixture until well coated, pressing it in slightly. Shake off excess flour.
  4. Fry: Fry the chicken in batches for 3–4 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Do not overcrowd the pot or the temperature will drop. Transfer cooked chicken to a cooling rack and season immediately with a pinch of salt.

Make the Sauce & Serve

  1. Prep Slurry: In a small bowl, whisk together the 1 tablespoon cornstarch and the vinegar until smooth (no lumps). Set aside.
  2. Aromatics: Heat a wok or clean skillet over medium-high heat with a splash of oil. When the oil is shimmering, add the minced garlic, ginger, and dried red chili peppers. Stir-fry vigorously for 30 seconds until fragrant and the garlic is barely toasted (do not burn).
  3. Liquid Base: Add the Shaoxing wine to deglaze the pan. Then add the Chinese chili flakes, sugar, chicken stock, and soy sauce. Stir together and bring to a vigorous boil.
  4. Thicken: Give the cornstarch/vinegar slurry a quick stir and pour it into the boiling sauce. Stir constantly until the sauce thickens into a glossy glaze (about 30 seconds).
  5. Toss: Remove from heat. Immediately toss the fried chicken into the sauce. Mix well until every piece is coated.
  6. Serve: Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately over white rice.

Notes

  • Shaoxing Wine Substitute: If you cannot find Chinese Shaoxing wine, dry sherry is the best substitute. In a pinch, you can use chicken broth, though the flavor profile will change slightly.
  • Spiciness: The whole dried chilies are mostly for aroma and visual appeal (unless you eat them!). The heat primarily comes from the chili flakes, so adjust those to your taste.
  • Crispy Tip: For extra crispy chicken, you can "double fry." Fry the chicken once at 350°F to cook it through, let it cool, and then fry again at 400°F for 1 minute to crisp the shell.
