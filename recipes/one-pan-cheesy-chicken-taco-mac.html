One-Pan Cheesy Chicken Taco Mac

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Tex-Mex

Think of this as the ultimate homemade upgrade to the boxed stuff. It combines the zesty flavors of chicken tacos with the comfort of creamy mac and cheese. The best part? The pasta cooks directly in the sauce, releasing starches that naturally thicken the dish—and leaving you with only one pan to clean.

Recommended Tools

  • Large Skillet with Lid (Essential: You need a tight-fitting lid to steam the pasta properly)
  • Box Grater (Freshly shredded cheese melts much smoother than pre-bagged)
  • Wooden Spoon

Ingredients

Homemade Taco Seasoning

  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika (optional, for depth)
  • ¼–½ tsp red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)

The Base

  • 1 lb chicken breast, cut into ½-inch cubes
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic (jarred "jarlic" or fresh)
  • 1 can (10 oz) mild Rotel (diced tomatoes and green chilies)
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1½ cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups elbow macaroni (uncooked)

The Cheesy Finish

  • ½ cup milk
  • 1½ cups freshly shredded Colby Jack cheese
  • Optional: ½ cup extra cheese for broiling topping

Instructions

  1. Sauté Chicken: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the cubed chicken, diced onion, minced garlic, and the entire spice blend. Sauté for 5–7 minutes until the chicken is browned and the onions are soft.
  2. Combine: Pour in the Rotel (undrained), tomato sauce, chicken broth, and the uncooked elbow noodles. Stir well to ensure the noodles are mostly submerged in the liquid.
  3. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover with a tight-fitting lid. Cook for 12 minutes. Tip: Stir once halfway through to prevent noodles from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  4. Melt: Remove the lid. The liquid should be mostly absorbed, and pasta should be tender. Stir in the milk and 1½ cups of Colby Jack cheese. Stir constantly until the cheese melts into a creamy sauce.
  5. Broil (Optional): For a crispy top, sprinkle the extra ½ cup of cheese over the skillet. Place under the oven broiler for 1–2 minutes until bubbly and golden.

Notes

  • Fresh Cheese Matters: Pre-shredded bagged cheese contains anti-caking agents (cellulose) that prevent it from melting smoothly. For the creamiest sauce, grate your own block of Colby Jack.
  • Vegetable Boost: Add a can of drained black beans or a cup of frozen corn in Step 2 to bulk up the meal.
  • Leftovers: Pasta absorbs moisture as it sits. When reheating leftovers, add a splash of milk or broth to loosen the sauce back up.
