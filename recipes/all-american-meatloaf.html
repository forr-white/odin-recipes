All-American Meatloaf

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American

Nothing says comfort food quite like a classic meatloaf dinner. This All-American version features a juicy, tender loaf topped with a sweet and tangy glaze, served alongside creamy homemade mashed potatoes and crisp garlic butter green beans. It’s the perfect Sunday supper that provides great leftovers for the week ahead.

Ingredients

For the Meatloaf:

  • 2 lbs ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¼ cup ketchup (plus extra for topping)
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp paprika

For the Glaze:

  • ½ cup ketchup
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp mustard

For the Mashed Potatoes:

  • 3 lbs potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • ½ cup milk
  • ¼ cup butter
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

For the Green Beans:

  • 1 lb fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt & pepper

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground meat, breadcrumbs, chopped onion, garlic, milk, eggs, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, and paprika. Mix until just combined.
  3. Shape mixture into a loaf and place in a greased 9x5-inch loaf pan or on a baking sheet.
  4. Mix ketchup, brown sugar, and mustard.
  5. Spread evenly over the top of the meatloaf.
  6. Bake for 60–70 minutes, or until internal temperature reaches 160°F (71°C). Let rest 10 minutes before slicing.
  7. Boil potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15–20 minutes.
  8. Drain and mash with butter and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  9. In a skillet, melt butter over medium heat.
  10. Add garlic and sauté 1 minute.
  11. Add green beans, salt, and pepper, sauté until tender-crisp, about 5–7 minutes.
  12. Slice meatloaf and serve with a generous scoop of mashed potatoes and green beans on the side.

Notes

  • Don't Overmix: When combining the meatloaf ingredients, use a light hand. Overmixing compresses the meat fibers and results in a dense, tough loaf.
  • Resting is Key: Letting the meatloaf rest for 10 minutes before slicing allows the juices to redistribute so they don't run out onto the cutting board.
  • Make Ahead: You can mix and shape the meatloaf the day before; simply cover it tightly in the fridge and bake it when you are ready for dinner.
