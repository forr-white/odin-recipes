Keema Aloo

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Indian

Keema Aloo is a beloved staple of Indian and Pakistani home cooking. "Keema" refers to minced meat and "Aloo" to potatoes. This dish is savory, hearty, and aromatic. While it uses traditional whole spices, you can easily achieve a similar flavor using the common alternatives listed in our notes below.

Ingredients

The Whole Spices

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 6 black peppercorns
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 2 black cardamom pods (See Notes for substitute)
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Aromatics & Meat

  • 1 red onion, halved and sliced thin
  • 1 tsp grated garlic
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • ¾ tsp salt

The Spice Blend & Simmer

  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri chile powder (See Notes for substitute)
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 2 vine-ripened tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 Yukon Gold potato, peeled and cut into ½-inch cubes
  • ¼ cup whole milk yogurt
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1 long green chile, halved (optional)
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped

Directions

  1. Bloom: Heat oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add peppercorns, cardamom pods, and cinnamon. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Brown: Add onion and cook until deeply browned (7–10 minutes). Add garlic and ginger for 30 seconds, then add beef and salt. Increase heat to medium-high and cook, breaking the meat into tiny pieces, until the pan is dry and the meat is sizzling (7–12 minutes).
  3. Spice: Add coriander, chile powder, turmeric, and cumin. Cook for 1 minute to toast the spices.
  4. Simmer: Add tomatoes, potato, yogurt, water, and green chile. Bring to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook until potatoes are tender and tomatoes have broken down (12–18 minutes).
  5. Finish: Stir in cilantro. Remove whole spices before serving if desired. Serve with naan or rice.

Notes

  • Prep is Key: Indian cooking moves fast once the pan is hot. Have all your spices measured and vegetables chopped before you turn on the stove.
  • Substitutions: If you cannot find Kashmiri chile powder, substitute with 1.5 tsp paprika mixed with 0.5 tsp cayenne. If a long green chile is unavailable, use half of a serrano.
  • Serving Suggestions: This dish is traditionally served with roti, naan, or basmati rice. It also makes a fantastic filling for samosas or wraps.

Substitution Guide

Hard-to-Find Ingredient Best Alternative
Kashmiri Chile Powder 3 parts Smoked Paprika + 1 part Cayenne Pepper
Black Cardamom 1 extra Green Cardamom + a tiny pinch of Smoked Paprika
Long Green Chile ½ a Serrano Pepper or 1 small Jalapeño
Shaoxing Wine / Whole Spices Omit and add ½ tsp of "Garam Masala" blend at the end

*Kashmiri powder is prized for its bright red color and mild heat. The paprika/cayenne mix mimics this beautifully.
