Parmesan Crusted Chicken Thighs

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

main-course • Italian-American

Chicken breasts often dry out before a crust can get truly crispy, but thighs are the perfect solution. Their higher fat content keeps them incredibly juicy even at high oven temperatures. This recipe uses a blend of Panko for crunch and Parmesan for savory depth, creating a texture that rivals fried chicken without the mess of hot oil.

Ingredients

The Meat & Binder

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Salt and black pepper (for initial seasoning)

The Parmesan Crust

  • ¾ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup Panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp salt

Directions

  1. Preheat: Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper or, ideally, place a wire rack over the baking sheet to allow heat to circulate underneath.
  2. Dry & Season: Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels. Moisture is the enemy of a good crust. Lightly season all sides directly with salt and black pepper.
  3. Mix Crust: In a shallow bowl, combine the Parmesan cheese, panko breadcrumbs, garlic powder, onion powder, Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, black pepper, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Dredge: Dip each chicken thigh completely into the beaten egg, allowing excess to drip off. Immediately press the chicken firmly into the Parmesan mixture, turning to coat every nook and cranny.
  5. Bake: Arrange the chicken on the prepared pan, ensuring they aren't touching. Bake uncovered for 25–30 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until the crust is deep golden and the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  6. Broil (Optional): For extra crunch, switch the oven to broil for the final 2–3 minutes. Watch closely to prevent the cheese from burning.
  7. Finish: Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving (this keeps the juices inside). Garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges.

Notes

  • The Wire Rack Secret: If you bake the chicken directly on parchment paper, the bottom breading will steam and become soft. Using a wire rack ensures 360-degree crispiness.
  • Air Fryer Instructions: Preheat air fryer to 380°F. Cook for 18–22 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  • Cheese Choice: Use freshly grated Parmesan if possible. The pre-shredded stuff in bags contains anti-caking agents that prevent it from melting and crisping properly.
