Parmesan Crusted Chicken Thighs

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

main-course • Italian-American

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs boneless/skinless chicken thighs
  • ¾ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs, beaten

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven.
    Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper or place a wire rack over a baking sheet.
  2. Prepare the chicken.
    Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels. Lightly season all sides with salt and black pepper.
  3. Make the Parmesan crust.
    In a shallow bowl, combine Parmesan cheese, panko breadcrumbs, garlic powder, onion powder, Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, black pepper, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Fully coat the chicken.
    Dip each chicken thigh completely into the beaten egg, coating all sides and allowing excess to drip off. Press firmly into the Parmesan mixture, turning and pressing to fully coat the entire piece of chicken.
  5. Bake.
    Arrange chicken on the prepared pan with space between pieces. Bake uncovered for 25–30 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until the crust is golden and the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  6. Optional broil.
    For extra crispiness, broil for 2–3 minutes, flipping once if needed, watching closely to avoid burning.
  7. Rest and serve.
    Let rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges if desired.

Notes

  • Full coating: This recipe is designed to fully coat 2 lbs of boneless, skinless chicken thighs on all sides using egg as the binder.
  • Extra crisp tip: Use a wire rack and flip halfway through baking to ensure even browning on all sides.
  • Air fryer method: Cook at 380°F for 18–22 minutes, flipping once, until deeply golden and cooked through.
  • Flavor variations: Add lemon zest, red pepper flakes, or swap Parmesan for Pecorino Romano for a sharper flavor.
  • Storage: Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or air fryer to restore crispness.
