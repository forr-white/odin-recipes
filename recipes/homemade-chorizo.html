Homemade Chorizo

Submitted by Cesar Reyes

dinner • Mexican

This is a recipe for Mexican-style fresh chorizo, which is raw and crumbly, distinct from the cured, sliceable Spanish variety. It relies on a robust blend of dried chili powders and a splash of vinegar to achieve its signature deep red color and tangy, savory flavor profile. It is incredibly versatile for breakfast, tacos, or cheese dips.

Ingredients

The Meat & Liquids

  • 1 lb ground pork (fatty pork is best, aim for 80/20)
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tbsp water

The Spice Blend

  • 6 tsp paprika (sweet/regular, not smoked)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp Ancho chili powder
  • 1 tsp New Mexico ground chili powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ tsp dried oregano (Mexican oregano preferred)
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger

Directions

  1. Mix Spices: In a small bowl, combine all the dry ingredients (paprika through ginger). Whisk to ensure there are no clumps.
  2. Combine: Place the ground pork in a large bowl. Sprinkle the seasoning mix evenly over the meat. Pour in the white vinegar and water. The liquid helps hydrate the dried spices and distributes the flavor into the meat.
  3. Mix: Using gloved hands (to prevent staining your skin) or a sturdy spoon, mix everything until the pork is thoroughly and evenly coated. Do not overwork the meat into a paste; just mix until combined.
  4. Marinate (Important): While you can cook it immediately, covering and refrigerating the meat for at least 2 hours (or overnight) allows the vinegar and spices to cure the meat slightly, resulting in a deeper, more authentic flavor.
  5. Cook: To serve, heat a skillet over medium heat. Add the chorizo (no extra oil is usually needed if the pork is fatty). Cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and crispy, about 8–10 minutes.

Notes

  • Usage Ideas: This chorizo is perfect for Chorizo con Huevos (scrambled with eggs) or Papas con Chorizo (fried with diced potatoes).
  • Spice Level: The heat comes primarily from the Ancho and New Mexico chili powders. Adjust these up or down depending on your preference.
  • Storage: The raw mixture freezes beautifully. Divide into 1/4 lb portions and freeze in airtight bags for up to 2 months.
