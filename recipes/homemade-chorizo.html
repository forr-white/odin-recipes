Homemade Chorizo

Submitted by Cesar Reyes

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 6 tsp paprika (not smoked)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp ancho chili powder
  • 1 tsp New Mexico ground chili powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp oregano
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tbsp water

Directions

  1. In a small bowl combine all dry seasonings.
  2. Add the ground pork to a large bowl. Sprinkle the seasoning mix over the meat.
  3. Pour in the white vinegar and water. These will help the spices absorb and give the chorizo that signature tangy flavor.
  4. Using gloved hands, or a spoon, mix everything until the pork is evenly coated with spices. Don't over mix, just make sure it's well combined.
  5. Marinading the meat is optional but recommended. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for deeper flavor.
  6. Heat a skillet over medium heat, add the chorizo, and cook until browned and fully cooked through.

Notes

  • To store, divide into portions and refrigerate for up to 3 days, or freeze up to 2 months.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 4 Servings (About 1/4 lb each)

Calories: 295

Fat: 24 g

Carbohydrates: 2 g

Protein: 18 g
