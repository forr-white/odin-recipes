Pinto Beans

sides • Southern American

These aren't just plain beans—this is a hearty meal in a bowl. Heavily influenced by Louisiana cooking, this recipe starts with the "Holy Trinity" (onion, celery, bell pepper) and is packed with three different types of meat: ground beef, smoked sausage, and spicy Andouille. It eats like a chili but with the creamy texture of slow-cooked pintos.

Ingredients

The Base

  • 1 lb dried pinto beans
  • 5 cups chicken broth (plus water if needed to cover)

The Meats

  • 1 lb ground meat (beef or pork)
  • 1 lb smoked sausage, sliced into rounds
  • 1 link Andouille sausage, sliced into rounds

Vegetables & Aromatics

  • 1 sweet onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 rib celery, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

Seasoning

  • 1 tablespoon Creole or Cajun seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Soak: Sort through the dried beans to remove any small stones or debris. Rinse them well. Place in a large bowl and cover with water by 2 inches. Soak overnight (8–12 hours). They should double in size. Drain and rinse before cooking.
  2. Brown Meat: In a skillet over medium-high heat, brown the ground meat until fully cooked. Drain the excess grease.
  3. Assemble: Add the drained beans, cooked ground meat, sliced smoked sausage, and Andouille to a large slow cooker.
  4. Add Veggies & Spice: Add the chopped onion, bell pepper, celery, and garlic. Top with the Creole seasoning, onion powder, garlic powder, dried thyme, and bay leaves.
  5. Liquid: Pour in the chicken broth. Stir well to combine. Ensure the liquid covers the ingredients (add a little water if necessary).
  6. Cook: Cover and cook on Low for 8 hours. The beans should be tender and creamy.
  7. Serve: Discard the bay leaves. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Serve hot over steamed white rice.

Notes

  • Quick Soak Method: Forgot to soak overnight? Boil the beans and water for 2 minutes on the stove, turn off the heat, cover, and let sit for 1 hour. Drain and proceed.
  • Thickness: If you prefer a creamier, thicker broth, take a potato masher and mash some of the beans directly in the pot right before serving.
  • Salt: Wait until the end to add extra salt. The broth, sausages, and Creole seasoning all contain salt, so taste first!
