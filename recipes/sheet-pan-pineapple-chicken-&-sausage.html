Sheet Pan Pineapple Chicken & Sausage

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American

This recipe is the ultimate low-effort, high-reward weeknight dinner. It combines the savory saltiness of smoked sausage with the caramelized sweetness of roasted pineapple. It essentially tastes like a deconstructed sweet and sour chicken, but because it is roasted rather than fried, it is significantly healthier and easier to clean up.

Ingredients

The Sheet Pan Base

  • 1½–2 lbs chicken breasts or thighs, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 12–16 oz smoked sausage (like Kielbasa or Andouille), sliced into coins
  • 2–3 green bell peppers, chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped into chunks
  • 1–2 cups pineapple chunks (fresh or canned, drained well)
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil

The Seasoning Blend

  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning (or Cajun seasoning for heat)

Homemade Sweet & Sour Glaze

  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • ¼ cup rice vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • ⅓ cup sugar (brown or white)
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Thickener: 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp cold water

Directions

  1. Preheat: Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Line a large rimmed baking sheet with foil or parchment paper for easy cleanup.
  2. Season: Place the chicken, sausage, peppers, and onions directly onto the baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle the seasoning blend over everything. Toss with your hands or tongs until well coated.
  3. Arrange: Spread the mixture into a single even layer. Scatter the pineapple chunks over the top (this keeps them from getting mushy on the bottom).
  4. Bake: Bake for 25–30 minutes, flipping the mixture halfway through cooking, until the chicken is cooked through (165°F) and veggies are tender. Optional: Broil for the last 2 minutes to caramelize the edges.
  5. Make Sauce: While the pan is in the oven, combine the pineapple juice, vinegar, sugar, ketchup, and soy sauce in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  6. Thicken: Whisk the cornstarch and cold water in a small cup to make a slurry. Pour this into the simmering sauce. Whisk constantly for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens and turns glossy.
  7. Serve: Drizzle the sauce over the hot sheet pan mixture immediately, or serve it on the side for dipping. Serve over white rice.

Notes

  • Chicken Choice: Chicken thighs are more forgiving and stay juicier at high heat than breasts. If using breasts, be careful not to cut the chunks too small or they will dry out.
  • Color: Using a mix of red, yellow, and green bell peppers makes for a much more visually appealing presentation.
  • Make it Spicy: If you like heat, use Andouille sausage, add red pepper flakes to the seasoning blend, or squirt some Sriracha into the glaze.
