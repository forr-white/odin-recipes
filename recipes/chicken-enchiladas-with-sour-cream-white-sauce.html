Chicken Enchiladas with Sour Cream White Sauce

Submitted by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican

If you are looking for a break from traditional red sauce, these white chicken enchiladas are the answer. Tender flour tortillas are stuffed with seasoned chicken and plenty of cheese, then baked in a luxurious, tangy white sauce made from sour cream, chicken broth, and mild green chilies. It’s rich, comforting, and perfect for family dinner.

  • 9×13-inch Baking Dish (The standard size for 8 enchiladas)

  • Whisk (Essential for a smooth, lump-free sauce)
  • Cheese Grater (Freshly grated Monterey Jack melts much smoother than pre-shredded)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked, shredded chicken (rotisserie chicken works great)
  • 8 small flour tortillas (6-inch size)
  • 2 cups shredded Monterey Jack cheese (divided)
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 (4 oz) can diced green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Directions

  1. Combine Filling: In a large mixing bowl, combine the shredded chicken and 1 cup of the shredded Monterey Jack cheese. Set aside.
  2. Season: Add garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the chicken mixture. Stir to distribute the seasonings evenly.
  3. Roll: Lay a tortilla flat and spoon about ¼ cup of the chicken mixture into the center. Roll the tortilla tightly and place it seam-side down in a greased 9×13-inch baking dish.
  4. Repeat: Continue filling and rolling each tortilla until all 8 are arranged in the dish.
  5. Make Roux: Melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Stir in the flour and whisk constantly for about 1 minute until it forms a paste (roux).
  6. Make Base Sauce: Gradually whisk in the chicken broth, stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Cook for 3–4 minutes until the sauce thickens and bubbles.
  7. Finish Sauce: Remove the pan from heat. Stir in the sour cream and diced green chilies until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. (Removing from heat is crucial to prevent the sour cream from curdling).
  8. Assemble: Pour the prepared sour cream sauce evenly over the enchiladas. Sprinkle the remaining 1 cup of shredded Monterey Jack cheese over the top.
  9. Bake: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake uncovered for 20–25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Serve: Sprinkle with fresh cilantro before serving for added flavor and presentation.

Notes

  • Curdling Warning: Do not boil the sauce once the sour cream has been added. High heat can cause dairy to separate. Always remove the pan from heat before stirring in the sour cream.
  • Tortillas: Flour tortillas are traditional for this creamy "Tex-Mex" style dish as they hold up well to the heavy sauce. If using corn tortillas, warm them first to prevent cracking.
  • Make Ahead: You can assemble the enchiladas up to the baking point, cover, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Add 10 minutes to the baking time if cooking from cold.
