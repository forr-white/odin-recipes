Cucumber Lime Agua Fresca

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • Mexican

Ingredients

  • 2 medium cucumbers (regular or English)
  • ⅓ cup lime juice
  • ¼ cup honey, agave syrup, or sweetener of choice
  • 6 cups cold water
  • to taste extra sweetener (optional)

Directions

  1. Prep the Cucumbers: Partially peel the cucumbers to leave strips of skin (this adds color without bitterness). Cut them into large chunks and place them in a blender.
  2. Combine: Add the lime juice, honey (or agave), and the cold water to the blender with the cucumbers.
  3. Blend: Blend on high speed until the mixture is completely smooth. Taste the mixture and add more sweetener if you prefer a sweeter drink.
  4. Strain (Optional): For a smooth drink with little to no pulp, pour the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve into a pitcher. If using a high-powered blender, the pulp may be fine enough to skip this step.
  5. Serve: Pour into glasses over plenty of ice. Garnish with a cucumber slice or lime wheel if desired.
  6. Store: Keep in the refrigerator for 2 to 3 days in a tightly sealed jar or bottle. The solids will settle, so be sure to shake or stir well before serving.

Notes

  • Peeling: If using regular slicing cucumbers (which have thick, waxy skins), peel them completely to avoid a bitter aftertaste. If using English or Persian cucumbers, leaving the skin on is fine.
  • Sweetener: Agave syrup dissolves easier in cold water than honey. If using honey, you may want to dissolve it in a splash of warm water before adding it to the blender.
  • Fizz Upgrade: For a sparkling version, fill the glass halfway with the agua fresca mix and top with sparkling water or club soda.
