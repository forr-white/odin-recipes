Sweet Pepper Sausage Pasta

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian

This is the kind of reliable, crowd-pleasing dinner that comes together in about 30 minutes. The natural sweetness of the red and yellow bell peppers cuts through the richness of the salty sausage and acidic tomatoes, creating a perfectly balanced sauce without needing added sugar.

Ingredients

The Base

  • 12 oz penne pasta (or rigatoni)
  • 1 lb smoked sausage or sweet Italian sausage, sliced into coins
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (divided)

The Sauce

  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional: Parmesan cheese for serving

Directions

  1. Brown Sausage: Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced sausage and cook until browned on both sides. Remove sausage with a slotted spoon and set aside. (Tip: Leave the rendered fat in the pan—it adds huge flavor to the veggies).
  2. Sauté Veggies: In the same skillet (add the remaining olive oil if the pan looks dry), add the chopped onion and sliced bell peppers. Sauté for 5–7 minutes until the onions are translucent and peppers are soft. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Simmer Sauce: Pour in the crushed tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 10–15 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  4. Boil Pasta: Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook penne pasta according to package directions until al dente. Before draining, reserve ¼ cup of the starchy pasta water.
  5. Combine: Add the cooked sausage back into the tomato sauce. Add the drained pasta to the skillet.
  6. Finish: Toss everything together. If the sauce seems too thick or dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh chopped parsley and a generous dusting of Parmesan cheese. Serve hot.

Notes

  • Spicy Variation: Use spicy Italian sausage or Andouille, and add ½ teaspoon of red pepper flakes when you add the garlic.
  • Creamy Version: Stir in ¼ cup of heavy cream or mascarpone cheese right at the end for a "pink sauce" variation.
  • Sausage Type: If using raw Italian sausage links (instead of smoked), slice them carefully or remove the casings and crumble the meat in step 1.
