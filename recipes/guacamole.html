Guacamole

Submitted by Cook Anything Kitchen

appetizers • Tex-Mex

Great guacamole relies on two things: perfectly ripe avocados and the right balance of acid and salt. This recipe uses a technique where you mix the aromatics (onion, pepper, cilantro) with the lime juice first. This marinates the onions slightly, taking away their raw bite, and ensures the flavor is distributed evenly before you even start mashing.

  • Chef's Knife (For dicing the jalapeno and cilantro finely)
  • Molcajete (Traditional stone mortar) or Large Mixing Bowl
  • Potato Masher (Optional, if you prefer a smoother texture than a fork provides)

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-large ripe avocados
  • 2 tablespoons minced white onion (about ⅛ large onion)
  • 1½ tablespoons minced, seeded jalapeño (about ½ medium pepper)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • Juice of ½ lime (approx. 1 tbsp)
  • ¼ teaspoon coarse kosher salt, plus more to taste

Directions

  1. Make the Base: In the bottom of a medium bowl (or mortar), combine the minced onion, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, and salt. Stir well and let sit for a minute. This creates a flavorful "dressing" for the avocado.
  2. Prep Avocados: Cut the avocados in half lengthwise and remove the pits. Using a knife, carefully score the flesh of each half in a grid pattern (cross-hatch) without cutting through the skin.
  3. Scoop & Coat: Run a spoon between the avocado flesh and the skin to scoop the chunks cleanly into the bowl with the onion mixture. Gently toss the chunks to coat them immediately in the lime juice mixture (this helps prevent browning).
  4. Mash: Taste the mixture. Using a fork (or potato masher), mash the chunks to your desired consistency. Some prefer it chunky; others prefer it smooth. Add more salt or lime if needed. Serve immediately with tortilla chips.

Notes

  • Picking Avocados: A ripe avocado should yield slightly to gentle pressure (like a ripe peach) but shouldn't feel mushy. If the little stem cap pops off easily and is green underneath, it's good. If it's brown underneath, it's overripe.
  • Storage: Air is the enemy. To store leftovers, place plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the guacamole, pressing out all air bubbles, before sealing the container lid. This prevents oxidation (browning).
  • Add-ins: For a more substantial dip, stir in ¼ cup diced Roma tomatoes after mashing.
