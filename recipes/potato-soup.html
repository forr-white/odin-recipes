Creamy Potato & Kielbasa Soup

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • American

This isn't just a side soup—it's a full meal. While classic potato soup is delicious, adding savory, smoky kielbasa sausage elevates it to main-course status. It is rich, cheesy, and packed with veggies, making it the perfect antidote to a cold winter night.

  • Potato Masher (Optional, for thickening the soup)

Ingredients

The Base

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 lb kielbasa sausage, cut into ½-inch rounds or half-moons
  • 1½ lbs potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet), peeled and cut into cubes
  • ½ cup yellow onion, finely diced
  • ½ cup carrots, peeled and finely diced
  • ½ cup celery, finely sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced

The Broth

  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper

The Creamy Finish

  • 2 cups milk (whole milk is best for texture)
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley

Directions

  1. Brown Sausage: Heat the olive oil in a Dutch oven or large soup pot over medium-high heat. Add the sliced kielbasa and cook for 5–6 minutes, stirring frequently until browned. Remove the sausage with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the flavored oil in the pot.
  2. Sauté Veggies: Add the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic to the pot. Sauté for about 2–3 minutes until the onions begin to soften and become fragrant.
  3. Simmer: Pour in the chicken stock and add the cubed potatoes. Bring the mixture to a boil, then immediately reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook for 15 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  4. Make it Creamy: Turn the heat down to LOW. (Crucial Step: Do not let the soup boil once milk is added, or it may curdle). Stir in the milk, cooked sausage, cheddar cheese, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.
  5. Melt: Stir constantly gently for 5–10 minutes until the cheeses have melted completely and the soup is hot.
  6. Serve: Remove from heat. Stir in the chopped parsley and serve immediately.

Notes

  • Thickening Trick: If you prefer a thicker soup, take a potato masher and gently mash some of the potatoes directly in the pot before adding the cheese. The released starch will naturally thicken the broth.
  • Cheese Tip: Always shred your own cheese from a block. Pre-shredded cheese contains anti-caking agents that can make your soup grainy instead of smooth.
  • Potatoes: Yukon Gold potatoes hold their shape better, while Russets will break down more and create a thicker soup. Choose based on your texture preference!
