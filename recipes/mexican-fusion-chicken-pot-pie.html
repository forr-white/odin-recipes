Mexican Fusion Chicken Pot Pie

Submitted by Forrest White / Cook Anything Kitchen

Main Dish • Cornbread bottom, puff pastry top, roasted veggies, bacon & poblanos

Ingredients

Cornbread Bottom (Medium Layer)

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 2 tbsp honey (optional, for a touch of sweetness)

Chicken & Bacon

  • 1½–2 lbs chicken thighs, cooked and shredded
  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon, diced
  • Reserved bacon drippings for the roux (about 3 tbsp)

Roasted Veggies

  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 ribs celery, diced
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1½ cups corn (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 large poblano pepper, seeded and diced
  • 1 small zucchini, diced (optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Creamy Mexican-Style Filling

  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp bacon drippings (or an extra tbsp butter if needed)
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2½ cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3 oz cream cheese
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp Mexican oregano (or regular oregano)
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp kosher salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1–2 tsp lime juice (to taste)
  • ½ cup crumbled cotija or queso fresco (stirred into filling)

Top Crust

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp water

Finishing Garnishes

  • Crumbled cotija, for topping
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime zest
  • Mexican crema, for drizzling

Directions

  1. Pre-bake the cornbread bottom
    Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Grease a 9×13-inch baking dish. In a medium bowl, whisk together the cornmeal, flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the buttermilk, egg, melted butter, and honey (if using). Stir until just combined, then spread the batter evenly into the baking dish in a thin, even layer (about ½-inch thick). Bake for 12–14 minutes, just until set but not deeply browned. Reduce oven temperature to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roast the vegetables
    Increase the oven to 425°F (220°C) while the cornbread cools slightly. On a large sheet pan, toss the carrots, celery, onion, corn, poblano, and optional zucchini with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread into an even layer and roast for 20–22 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the vegetables are lightly caramelized. Remove from the oven and set aside.
  3. Cook the bacon
    In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the diced bacon until crisp. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the bacon to a paper towel-lined plate. Reserve at least 3 tablespoons of the bacon drippings in the skillet for the roux, discarding any extra or saving for another use.
  4. Make the creamy Mexican-style gravy
    With the skillet over medium heat, add the butter to the reserved bacon drippings. Once melted, whisk in the flour to form a roux and cook for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly. Slowly whisk in the chicken broth until smooth and slightly thickened, then add the heavy cream and cream cheese. Stir until the cream cheese is fully melted and the sauce is smooth. Season with cumin, smoked paprika, Mexican oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. Add 1–2 teaspoons of lime juice to brighten the flavor. Stir in the shredded chicken, roasted vegetables, crispy bacon, and ½ cup crumbled cotija. Simmer for 2–3 minutes until the filling is thick and creamy. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Assemble the pot pie
    Pour the hot chicken and roasted veggie filling evenly over the pre-baked cornbread layer in the 9×13-inch baking dish. Gently spread and smooth the top so the filling is level. Lay the puff pastry sheet over the filling, trimming or gently stretching so it covers the entire surface. Press the edges lightly against the sides of the dish to seal.
  6. Egg wash and vent
    In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and water to make an egg wash. Brush the top of the puff pastry with the egg wash for a glossy, golden finish. Use a sharp knife to cut 4–5 small slits in the pastry to allow steam to escape during baking.
  7. Bake
    Bake the assembled pot pie at 375°F (190°C) for 30–35 minutes, or until the puff pastry is deeply golden brown and the filling is bubbling around the edges. If the top browns too quickly, tent loosely with foil for the last 5–10 minutes.
  8. Finish and garnish
    Remove the pot pie from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes to allow the filling to set slightly. Sprinkle the top with extra crumbled cotija, chopped cilantro, and a bit of lime zest. Drizzle with Mexican crema just before serving.

Notes

  • For extra depth, you can roast the poblano separately until blistered, peel off the skin, then dice and add it to the filling.
  • If you prefer a slightly sweeter cornbread base, increase the honey to 3 tbsp.
  • To lighten the filling, replace half of the heavy cream with evaporated milk or whole milk.
  • Cotija inside the filling and on top gives a great salty contrast to the creamy sauce.
  • Leftovers reheat well in the oven at 350°F (175°C) until warmed through and the top is crisp again.
