Mexican Fusion Chicken Pot Pie

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

Main Dish • Tex-Mex

Why choose between cornbread and puff pastry when you can have both? This fusion pot pie features a **double-crust innovation**: a thin, honey-kissed cornbread base to soak up the juices and a light, shattery puff pastry lid. Inside, the traditional peas and carrots are swapped for roasted poblanos, corn, and a smoky cumin-infused cream sauce.

Ingredients

The Hybrid Crusts

  • Bottom: 1 cup cornmeal, 1 cup flour, 1 tbsp baking powder, ½ tsp salt, 1 cup buttermilk, 1 egg, 2 tbsp melted butter, 2 tbsp honey.
  • Top: 1 sheet puff pastry (thawed), 1 egg (for wash).

Chicken & Roasted Veggies

  • 1½–2 lbs chicken thighs, cooked and shredded
  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon, diced
  • Veggies: 2 carrots, 2 celery ribs, 1 yellow onion, 1½ cups corn, 1 large poblano pepper (all diced).

Mexican Cream Filling

  • 3 tbsp butter + 3 tbsp bacon drippings
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2½ cups chicken broth + 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3 oz cream cheese
  • ½ cup crumbled Cotija or Queso Fresco
  • Spices: 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp Mexican oregano, ½ tsp garlic powder, ½ tsp onion powder, salt & pepper.

Directions

  1. The Base: Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Whisk cornbread ingredients until just combined. Spread into a greased 9x13 dish and bake for 12–14 minutes. Remove and lower oven to 375°F.
  2. The Roast: Toss all the vegetables with oil and salt/pepper on a sheet pan. Roast at 425°F for 20 minutes until caramelized.
  3. The Gravy: Fry diced bacon until crisp; set bacon aside and keep 3 tbsp drippings in the pan. Add butter and flour to make a roux (cook 2 mins). Whisk in broth, heavy cream, and cream cheese until smooth.
  4. The Mix: Add the spices, lime juice, shredded chicken, roasted veggies, bacon, and Cotija to the sauce. Simmer for 2 minutes.
  5. Assemble: Pour the filling over the cornbread. Drape the puff pastry sheet over the top, sealing the edges against the dish. Brush with egg wash and cut steam vents.
  6. The Bake: Bake at 375°F for 30–35 minutes until the pastry is deeply golden. Let rest 10 minutes before serving.

Notes

  • Texture Control: Pre-baking the cornbread is essential. It creates a "barrier" that stays bready rather than turning into mush once the filling is added.
  • Make Ahead: You can roast the veggies and cook the chicken a day in advance to speed up the assembly process.
  • The Garnish: Don't skip the lime zest at the end—it cuts through the richness of the heavy cream and bacon drippings perfectly.
