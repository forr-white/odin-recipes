Pepper Steak Stir Fry

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Chinese American

Pepper Steak is a takeout staple for a reason. It combines the rich, savory umami of soy sauce with the fresh crunch of bell peppers and the subtle heat of ginger. This homemade version uses flank steak, which is lean yet incredibly flavorful, and cooks fast enough for a busy weeknight dinner.

Ingredients

The Stir Fry

  • 2 lbs flank steak, sliced into thin strips against the grain
  • 1 large red bell pepper, cut into thin strips
  • 1 large green bell pepper, cut into thin strips
  • 1 tbsp olive, avocado, or vegetable oil

The Sauce

  • ⅓ cup soy sauce (low sodium recommended)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, pressed or minced
  • ¼ cup water (optional, if sauce is too thick)

Directions

  1. Make Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, brown sugar, cornstarch, grated ginger, and pressed garlic. Set aside.
  2. Sear Veggies: Preheat a wok or large skillet with half the oil over medium heat. Add the sliced red and green bell peppers. Cook until just tender-crisp and slightly golden (about 3 minutes). Remove peppers from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sear Beef (In Batches): Turn the heat up to high. Add the remaining oil. Add the beef strips in a single layer. Do not crowd the pan, or the beef will steam instead of brown. Cook for 2–3 minutes until browned. If necessary, cook in two batches.
  4. Combine: Once the beef is browned, add the cooked peppers back into the pan.
  5. Thicken: Whisk the sauce one last time (cornstarch settles) and pour it over the steak and veggies. Toss constantly for 2–4 minutes. The sauce will bubble and thicken into a glossy glaze coating the meat.
  6. Serve: Remove from heat immediately to prevent the vegetables from getting soggy. Serve over steamed white rice or noodles.

Notes

  • Slicing Tip: Flank steak can be tough if cut incorrectly. Always find the direction of the muscle fibers ("the grain") and slice perpendicular to them. Freezing the meat for 20 minutes before cutting makes it easier to get thin slices.
  • Onions: If you like onions, slice half a yellow onion into thick wedges and cook them along with the peppers in step 2.
  • Heat: For a spicy kick, add a teaspoon of crushed red pepper flakes or Sriracha to the sauce mixture.
