Cajun Hollandaise

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Cajun French Fusion

Ingredients

  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted and warm
  • 1–1½ tsp Cajun seasoning (homemade or store-bought; adjust to heat level)
  • ¼ tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder
  • Pinch cayenne pepper (optional for extra heat)
  • ¼ tsp salt (adjust depending on seasoning blend)
  • 2–3 tsp hot sauce of choice (Crystal or Louisiana recommended)
  • Optional: squeeze of orange juice for a subtle Creole vibe

Directions

  1. Heat a small pot of water: Bring 1–2 inches of water to a gentle simmer.
  2. Whisk the yolks: In a heatproof bowl, whisk egg yolks and lemon juice until slightly thickened.
  3. Set over the steam: Place bowl over (not in) the simmering water. Continue whisking constantly so the eggs don’t scramble.
  4. Slowly stream in butter: While whisking, drizzle in the melted butter a little at a time until thick, glossy, and fully emulsified.
  5. Season it: Add Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, garlic powder, cayenne (if using), salt, and hot sauce. Adjust to taste.
  6. Serve immediately: Hollandaise is best fresh. Keep warm (not hot) if serving later.

Notes

  • For a true Creole-style hollandaise, swap the Cajun seasoning for Creole seasoning, which has more herbs (like oregano, thyme, and basil) and slightly less heat.
  • Add a splash of Worcestershire sauce and a tiny pinch of Creole mustard to create the classic tang found in Creole sauces.
  • Finish the sauce with a small squeeze of orange juice instead of lemon for a brighter, more citrus-forward Creole flavor profile.
