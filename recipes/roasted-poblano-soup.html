Roasted Poblano Soup

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • Mexican

Unlike a chunky chowder, this is a smooth, velvety soup that puts the flavor of the roasted poblano front and center. It starts with a simple roux to provide body, then blends the roasted peppers with rich stock. It packs a bit of heat, which is perfectly balanced by a drizzle of cool Mexican Crema and a splash of vinegar-based hot sauce.

Ingredients

The Soup Base

  • 5 fresh poblano peppers
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock

Seasoning

  • 2 teaspoons Mexican oregano (optional)
  • Pinch of cumin (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon salt (plus more to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Garnishes (Crucial for Balance)

  • Mexican Crema (or heavy cream)
  • Cilantro stems, finely chopped
  • Hot sauce (vinegar-based like Valentina or Cholula)

Directions

  1. Roast Peppers: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the rinsed poblanos on a baking sheet. Roast for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the skins are blistered and darkened.
  2. Steam & Peel: Remove peppers from the oven. Cover them loosely with foil or a kitchen towel for 5 minutes (this traps steam and loosens the skins). Peel off the skins, remove the seeds and stems, and roughly chop the flesh.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 5–7 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Make Roux: Sprinkle the flour over the onions and garlic. Stir well to cook off the raw flour taste (about 1 minute).
  5. Simmer: Slowly pour in the stock while stirring to prevent lumps. Add the oregano, cumin, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 7–8 minutes to thicken slightly.
  6. Combine: Add the chopped roasted poblanos to the saucepan. Simmer for a few minutes to ensure everything is the same temperature.
  7. Blend: Carefully transfer the hot mixture to a blender. (Safety Tip: Remove the center cap of the blender lid and cover with a folded towel to let steam escape). Blend until completely smooth.
  8. Finish: Return the soup to the pot. Simmer for 5–10 minutes. Taste and adjust salt.
  9. Serve: Ladle into bowls. Garnish generously with a swirl of Mexican Crema, chopped cilantro, and a few dashes of hot sauce for acidity.

Notes

  • Managing Heat: This soup can be spicy! If you prefer a milder version, roast all 5 peppers but only blend in 3 to start. Taste the soup, and add the remaining peppers only if you want more heat.
  • The Acid Factor: Because this is a savory, earthy soup, it needs acid to wake it up. If you don't have hot sauce, a squeeze of fresh lime juice is a perfect substitute.
  • Texture: I prefer adding the cream as a garnish rather than mixing it into the pot; it looks beautiful and keeps the flavor of the peppers pure.
