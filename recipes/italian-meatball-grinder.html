Italian Meatball Grinder

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sandwich • Italian-American

In New England, they call it a "Grinder" because the crusty bread requires a bit of grinding with your teeth. This version levels up the classic meatball sub by using a garlic-herb butter to toast the rolls first. It creates a waterproof barrier for the bread, ensuring your sandwich stays crisp even under a heavy blanket of marinara and melted mozzarella.

Ingredients

  • 12–15 cooked meatballs (Use our Classic Italian Meatballs)
  • 4 Italian sub rolls or hoagie rolls
  • 2–3 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped (optional)

Garlic Butter for Toasting

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp Italian seasoning
  • Pinch of salt

Directions

  1. Prepare Garlic Butter: In a small bowl, whisk together the melted butter, garlic powder, onion powder, Italian seasoning, and salt.
  2. Toast Rolls: Split the sub rolls open. Place them face-up on a baking sheet and brush the insides generously with the garlic butter. Broil for 1–2 minutes until the edges are golden brown. Watch carefully so they don't burn!
  3. Warm Meatballs: In a medium saucepan, warm the marinara sauce over medium heat. Add your cooked meatballs to the sauce and toss gently to coat. Let them simmer for 2–3 minutes until heated through.
  4. Assemble: Spoon a little extra sauce onto the bottom of each toasted roll. Place 3–4 meatballs inside each sandwich. Spoon another layer of sauce over the meatballs.
  5. Add Cheese: Sprinkle each grinder with grated Parmesan, then top with a heavy layer of shredded mozzarella.
  6. Melt & Finish: Return the pan to the oven and broil for 1–3 minutes until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and starting to show brown spots.
  7. Garnish: Remove from the oven and sprinkle with fresh chopped basil or parsley. Serve immediately while the cheese is hot and gooey.

Notes

  • Cheese Tip: For the ultimate "cheese pull," add a slice of Provolone cheese under the meatballs before adding the mozzarella.
  • Veggies: Sautéed bell peppers and onions make this "Staten Island style" and add a nice sweetness to the savory sauce.
  • Prep Ahead: You can make the meatballs and sauce ahead of time. Simply assemble and broil when you're ready to eat.
  • Bread: A "soft" hoagie roll works, but a crusty Italian baguette-style roll holds up better to the moisture of the sauce.
