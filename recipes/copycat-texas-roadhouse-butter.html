Copycat Texas Roadhouse Butter

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • American

Let's be honest: the steak is great, but the real reason we go to Texas Roadhouse is for the rolls and that incredible cinnamon honey butter. It is light, fluffy, sweet, and dangerously addictive. This copycat recipe whips up in minutes and brings that restaurant magic to your own kitchen table.

Recommended Tools

  • Hand Mixer or Stand Mixer (Essential for whipping air into the butter)
  • Mixing Bowl (Deep enough to prevent powdered sugar clouds)
  • Small Ramekins (For serving just like the restaurant)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • ½ cup confectioners' sugar (powdered sugar)
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt (add slightly more if using unsalted butter)

Directions

  1. Combine: Place the softened butter, confectioners’ sugar, honey, cinnamon, and salt in a deep mixing bowl.
  2. Start Slow: Start mixing on low speed to incorporate the powdered sugar without making a mess.
  3. Whip: Increase speed to medium-high and beat for 2–3 minutes. You want the butter to become pale, airy, and slightly fluffy.
  4. Serve: Scrape into a serving bowl and serve immediately at room temperature.

Notes

  • Room Temp is Key: The butter must be completely soft (but not melted) to whip properly. If it's too cold, you'll get lumps; too hot, and it will be greasy.
  • Not Just for Rolls: This butter is incredible on sweet potatoes, pancakes, waffles, French toast, or warm banana bread.
  • Storage: Store in the refrigerator, but let it sit on the counter for 30 minutes before serving to soften up again.
