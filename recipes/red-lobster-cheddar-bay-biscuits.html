Red Lobster Cheddar Bay Biscuits

bread • American

These are arguably the most famous bread basket items in America. Known as "drop biscuits," they require no rolling, cutting, or kneading. The secret to that signature flavor is the generous amount of sharp cheddar inside and the garlic-herb butter brushed on top the second they come out of the oven.

Ingredients

The Biscuit Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for a subtle kick)
  • 1 cup buttermilk (cold)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1½ cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese

The Garlic Butter Topping

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley (dried works in a pinch)
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C). Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, garlic powder, salt, and cayenne pepper.
  3. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a separate large glass measuring cup or bowl, whisk together the cold buttermilk and the melted butter. (Note: The butter may form small clumps as it hits the cold milk—this is fine and helps create flaky pockets).
  4. Combine: Pour the wet mixture over the dry ingredients. Stir using a rubber spatula just until moist. Do not overmix. Gently fold in the shredded cheddar cheese.
  5. Scoop: Using a ¼-cup measuring cup or a large ice cream scoop, drop mounds of batter onto the prepared baking sheet. Leave about 2 inches of space between them.
  6. Bake: Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until the tops are golden brown.
  7. The Finish: While biscuits bake, whisk together the topping ingredients (melted butter, parsley, garlic powder). As soon as the biscuits come out of the oven, brush the tops generously with the garlic butter mixture. Serve hot.

Notes

  • Cheese: For the best melting factor, buy a block of sharp cheddar and shred it yourself. Pre-shredded bagged cheese contains cellulose which prevents it from melting smoothly.
  • No Buttermilk? You can make a substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar to 1 cup of regular milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using.
  • Texture: Be careful not to overwork the dough in Step 4. Stir only until the flour streaks disappear. Overworking the gluten will result in hard, tough biscuits.
