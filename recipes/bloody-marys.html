Bloody Marys

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • American

The Bloody Mary is the undisputed queen of brunch cocktails. Savory, spicy, and endlessly customizable, it’s practically a meal in a glass. While the mix provides the flavor, the construction—from the salty rim to the elaborate garnish—is what makes it an experience.

Ingredients

  • Bloody Mary Mix (Homemade is best!)
  • 2 oz Vodka (per drink)
  • 1 tbsp Celery Salt (for rimming)
  • 1 tbsp Kosher Salt (for rimming)
  • Ice (plenty of it)
  • Garnishes: Lemon/lime wedges, celery stalks, olives, bacon, pickles, etc.

Directions

  1. Prepare the Rim: On a small plate, mix equal parts celery salt and kosher salt. Dip the rim of your glass into a shallow amount of water (or run a lime wedge around the rim), then dip the wet rim into the salt mix and twist to coat.
  2. Ice: Fill an 8-ounce glass or highball glass all the way to the top with ice.
  3. Pour: Add 2 ounces of vodka over the ice.
  4. Top: Fill the rest of the glass with your Bloody Mary Mix. Stir gently with a long spoon to combine.
  5. Garnish: Go wild! Add a celery stalk, a skewer of olives and pickles, a wedge of lemon or lime, and even a strip of bacon or cooked shrimp if desired.

Notes

  • Bloody Maria: Not a vodka fan? Swap the vodka for Tequila to make a Bloody Maria. The agave flavor pairs beautifully with the spicy tomato mix.
  • Red Snapper: Swap the vodka for Gin to make a Red Snapper cocktail.
  • The Rim: Don't skip the celery salt rim! It adds an essential aromatic crunch to every sip.
  • Chill: Ensure your mix is very cold before assembling to prevent the ice from melting too quickly and diluting the drink.
