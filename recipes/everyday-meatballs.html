Everyday Meatballs

Submitted by Ina Garten

dinner • Italian

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground meat (I use a mix of beef and pork)
  • 2/3 cup fresh bread crumbs or 1/2 cup panko
  • 1/3 cup milk or water
  • 2 tablespoon finely chopped parsley
  • 2 tablespoons finely grated Parmesan or Romano cheese (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons coarse or kosher salt, divided
  • Pinches of red pepper flakes or few grinds of black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced, divided
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 28-ounce can of tomato puree or crushed tomatoes

Directions

  1. Place meat, crumbs, milk or water, parsley, cheese (if using), 1 teaspoon salt, pepper, onion powder, eggs and half
  2. of your minced garlic in a large bowl. I like to mix all of this together with a fork, which does a good job of breaking up
  3. the eggs and chunks of meat. Form mixture into 1 1/2 to 2-inch meatballs and arrange on a plate. I like to let them
  4. set in the fridge for a bit — 30 minutes, if you can spare it — which helps them keep their shape.
  5. In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium heat. Add remaining garlic and some pepper flakes and let
  6. sizzle until garlic is golden, 30 seconds to 1 minute. Add tomatoes (beware the splatter!) and season with remaining
  7. salt. Let mixture simmer over low heat for about 10 minutes (with a thicker puree) or 20 (for crushed tomatoes,
  8. which are usually more watery), stirring occasionally.
  9. With stove on the lowest heat possible to maintain a gentle simmer, add meatballs to sauce one by one, and cover
  10. with a lid. It will be hard but please don’t touch or move them for at least 20 minutes of the 25-minute cooking time,
  11. so that they have a chance to keep their shape. Meatballs should be fully cooked through at 25 minutes, but it
  12. cannot hurt to cut one in half to verify.

Notes

  • Eat however makes you happy:
  • — as-is.
  • — with spaghetti: I’ll cook it very al dente, a generous minute shy of done, reserve a little pasta water, then once the
  • spaghetti is drained, place it back in the pot with a splash of the water and a ladle or two of the sauce beneath the
  • meatballs and cook it together over high heat for a minute. Tip spaghetti into a large, wide bowl, add the meatballs
  • on top. Note: If your family likes a lot of sauce with their spaghetti, you might consider making the meatballs with an
  • extra half or whole can. Just use what you need.
  • — “parmesan”-ed: Place meatballs in a shallow baking dish with some of their sauce. Tear about 8 ounces
  • mozzarella over the top and broil until melted. Finish with some parmesan, if desired, some breadcrumbs fried in a
  • little olive oil and/or chopped parsley

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 6 Servings (about 4 meatballs)

Calories: 305

Fat: 21 g

Carbohydrates: 8 g

Protein: 20 g
