Fluffy Homestyle Pancakes

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

breakfast • American

There is no need for boxed mix when homemade pancakes are this easy. These are the quintessential Saturday morning breakfast—tall, fluffy, and soaking up just the right amount of syrup. The secret to that diner-style rise is letting the batter rest for a few minutes before cooking to activate the baking powder.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3½ teaspoons baking powder (make sure it's fresh!)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1¼ cups whole milk (add up to 2 tbsp more if batter is very thick)
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter, plus more for the pan
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Directions

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt until well combined.
  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Create a well in the center. Add the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Whisk Gently: Whisk just until combined. Do not overmix. Small lumps are perfectly fine. Overmixing develops gluten and makes the pancakes tough.
  4. Rest Batter: Let the batter sit undisturbed for 5 minutes. This allows the baking powder to activate, creating bubbles that lead to fluffier pancakes.
  5. Heat: Warm a skillet or griddle over medium heat (about 350°F/175°C) and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  6. Cook: Scoop ¼ cup of batter per pancake onto the griddle. Cook until bubbles appear on the surface and pop, and the edges look set (about 2–3 minutes).
  7. Flip: Flip carefully and cook the other side until golden brown (another 1–2 minutes).
  8. Serve: Serve hot topped with butter, maple syrup, fruit, or whipped cream.

Notes

  • Consistency Check: If the batter seems too thick like cookie dough, add a splash more milk. If it's too runny, add a tablespoon of flour.
  • Extra Fluff: For souffle-style pancakes, separate the egg. Mix the yolk into the batter, whip the white to stiff peaks separately, and fold it in at the end.
  • Add-ins: Sprinkle blueberries, chocolate chips, or nuts onto the wet side of the pancake right after pouring the batter onto the griddle.
  • Keep Warm: To serve everyone at once, place cooked pancakes on a wire rack on a baking sheet in a 200°F oven while you finish the batter.
