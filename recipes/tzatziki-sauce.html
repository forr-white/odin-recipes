Creamy Greek Tzatziki Sauce

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauces & dips • Greek

Tzatziki is the quintessential Greek condiment. Cool, creamy, and tangy, it cuts through the richness of grilled meats like lamb and chicken. The secret to a thick, restaurant-quality tzatziki is removing as much liquid from the cucumber as possible before mixing it with the yogurt.

  • Box Grater (Use the large holes for the cucumber)
  • Cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel (Essential for squeezing the cucumber dry)
  • Microplane (For grating the garlic into a paste)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt (whole milk/full fat is best for texture)
  • ½ large English cucumber, grated (about ¾ cup packed)
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced or grated
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice (or white wine vinegar for a sharper tang)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ tsp kosher salt (plus more for draining cucumber)
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh mint

Directions

  1. Grate & Drain (Crucial Step): Grate the cucumber on the large holes of a box grater. Place the grated cucumber in the center of a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth. Twist and squeeze vigorously over the sink to remove as much liquid as possible. (If you skip this, your sauce will be watery within an hour).
  2. Mix Base: In a medium bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, minced garlic, chopped dill, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Combine: Fold the squeezed, dried cucumber into the yogurt mixture until evenly distributed.
  4. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving. This allows the garlic and dill flavors to "bloom" into the yogurt.
  5. Serve: Taste and adjust salt or lemon if needed. Serve chilled with pita bread, veggies, or grilled meats.

Notes

  • Yogurt Choice: Use "Greek" yogurt, which has already been strained. If you only have regular plain yogurt, you must strain it through a coffee filter or cheesecloth for 2 hours to thicken it before using.
  • Garlic Potency: Raw garlic gets stronger as it sits in the yogurt. If making this a day in advance, use slightly less garlic than you think you need.
  • Storage: Tzatziki keeps well in an airtight container in the fridge for 3–4 days. Stir before serving if any liquid separates.
