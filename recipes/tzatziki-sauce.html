Tzatziki Sauce

Submitted by Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Greek

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt (whole milk, plain)
  • ½ large English cucumber, grated (~¾ cup packed)
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced or grated
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice (or white wine vinegar for extra tang)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ tsp kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: 1 tbsp finely chopped mint for a cooling twist

Directions

  1. Grate & drain the cucumber: Grate the cucumber on the large holes of a box grater. Place in a clean towel or paper towels and squeeze out as much liquid as possible. This step prevents a watery sauce.
  2. Mix the base: In a medium bowl, combine the yogurt, garlic, dill, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Fold in cucumber: Stir in the drained cucumber until evenly distributed. Adjust salt and lemon to taste.
  4. Chill before serving: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let flavors meld.

Notes

  • Use thick, full-fat yogurt for the best texture.
  • If using regular (non-Greek) yogurt, strain it through cheesecloth or a coffee filter for 1–2 hours first.
  • Tzatziki keeps well for up to 3–4 days refrigerated in an airtight container.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 8 Servings (about 2 tbsp)

Calories: 55

Fat: 4.5 g

Carbohydrates: 1 g

Protein: 2.5 g
