Copycat Raising Cane’s Sauce

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • American

Let’s be honest: people go to Raising Cane’s for the sauce. It is savory, tangy, and distinctively peppery. While the restaurant keeps the recipe under lock and key, this copycat version gets the flavor profile 99% of the way there. The secret is using plenty of coarse black pepper and letting the flavors meld overnight.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup mayonnaise (Duke's or Hellmann's recommended)
  • ¼ cup ketchup
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon granulated garlic (or garlic powder)
  • ½ teaspoon coarsely ground fresh black pepper

Directions

  1. Combine: In a medium bowl, add the mayonnaise, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, salt, granulated garlic, and black pepper.
  2. Whisk: Stir or whisk vigorously until the mixture is completely smooth and the spices are evenly distributed.
  3. Rest (Crucial Step): Cover the bowl tightly or transfer to an airtight container. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. The flavors need time to meld together; eating it immediately will just taste like ketchup and mayo.
  4. Serve: Stir again before serving with chicken fingers, fries, or Texas toast.

Notes

  • The Pepper: The key to the authentic Cane's flavor is the black pepper. Use coarsely ground pepper (not the fine dust) so you get those little pops of heat.
  • Garlic: Granulated garlic has a slightly coarser texture than garlic powder, which mimics the original sauce better, but powder works fine in a pinch.
  • Storage: This sauce keeps well in the fridge for up to 1 week.
