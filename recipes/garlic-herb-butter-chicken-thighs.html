Garlic Herb Butter Chicken Thighs

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American

This recipe delivers high reward for low effort. By starting the chicken in a cold or medium pan and finishing it in the oven, you achieve shattering crisp skin while keeping the meat juicy. The garlic herb butter melts over the hot chicken at the end, creating an instant sauce perfect for drizzling over vegetables or rice.

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (about 2 lbs total)
  • 1½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or neutral oil)

Garlic Herb Butter

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced (or 1 tablespoon jarred garlic)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, finely chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ½ teaspoon dried rosemary, crushed
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C). Ensure you have a large oven-safe skillet (cast iron is best).
  2. Season: Pat the chicken thighs extremely dry with paper towels (this is key for crispy skin). Mix the dry spices (salt, pepper, garlic powder, dried thyme, rosemary) and sprinkle evenly over both sides of the chicken.
  3. Make Butter: In a small bowl, mash together the softened butter, minced garlic, parsley, dried herbs, and salt until combined. Set aside.
  4. Sear: Heat the olive oil in your skillet over medium-high heat. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down. Sear for 5–7 minutes without moving them. You want the skin to be deeply golden and crisp.
  5. Butter Baste: Flip the thighs so they are skin-side up. Dollop the garlic herb butter around the pan and slightly on top of the chicken. Let it bubble for 1 minute.
  6. Roast: Transfer the skillet to the hot oven. Roast for 18–25 minutes.
  7. Check Temp: The chicken is safe at 165°F, but thighs are best when cooked to an internal temperature of 175°F (80°C), as this breaks down the tough connective tissue.
  8. Rest & Serve: Remove from the oven. Spoon the melted pan juices over the chicken. Let rest for 5 minutes, then serve with the herby butter sauce spooned over the top.

Notes

  • Crispy Skin Secret: Moisture is the enemy of crispiness. Pat the skin very dry before seasoning. Also, do not crowd the pan; leave a little space between thighs.
  • One-Pan Meal: Toss halved baby potatoes or carrots into the skillet after searing the chicken. They will roast in the chicken fat and butter alongside the meat.
  • Boneless Option: If using boneless, skinless thighs, reduce the oven time to 12–15 minutes.
  • Pan Sauce: If you want a gravy, remove the cooked chicken, place the pan on low heat, whisk in 1 tablespoon of flour, and then whisk in ½ cup chicken broth and a squeeze of lemon.
