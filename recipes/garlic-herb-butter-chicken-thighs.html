Garlic Herb Butter Chicken Thighs

Submitted by Forrest White

dinner • American

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (about 2 lbs total)
  • 1½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or neutral oil)

Garlic Herb Butter

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced (or 1 tablespoon jarred garlic)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, finely chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ½ teaspoon dried rosemary, crushed
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt

Instructions

  1. Prep the oven and pan: Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C). Use an oven-safe skillet (cast iron works great).
  2. Pat dry and season: Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels. In a small bowl, mix the salt, pepper, garlic powder, dried thyme, and dried rosemary. Sprinkle the seasoning evenly over both sides of the chicken, focusing extra on the skin side.
  3. Make the garlic herb butter: In a separate bowl, combine the softened butter, minced garlic, parsley, dried thyme, dried rosemary, and salt. Mash together until fully combined and set aside.
  4. Sear the chicken: Heat the olive oil in your skillet over medium-high heat. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down and sear for 5–7 minutes, without moving them, until the skin is deeply golden and crisp.
  5. Flip and add butter: Flip the thighs so they are skin-side up. Dollop the garlic herb butter around and on top of the chicken thighs. Let it melt and start to bubble for 1–2 minutes.
  6. Roast in the oven: Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Roast for 18–25 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the thickest part of the thighs reaches 175°F (80°C) and the juices run clear.
  7. Baste and rest: Carefully remove the skillet from the oven. Spoon the melted garlic herb butter and pan juices over the chicken. Let the thighs rest in the pan for 5 minutes before serving.
  8. Serve: Serve the chicken thighs with the herby butter spooned over the top. They pair well with mashed potatoes, rice, roasted vegetables, or a simple side salad.

Notes

  • Crispy skin tip: The drier the skin, the crisper it gets. Pat very dry and don’t overcrowd the pan.
  • Fresh vs dried herbs: You can swap the dried thyme and rosemary in the rub for fresh if you have them. Use about 3 times the amount of fresh herbs.
  • Boneless thighs: Boneless, skinless thighs also work. Reduce oven time to about 12–15 minutes and check for doneness at 165°F (74°C).
  • Pan sauce: For a quick sauce, remove the chicken after roasting and place the skillet over low heat. Splash in a bit of chicken broth or a squeeze of lemon and whisk into the butter and drippings.
  • Make it a full meal: Add small halved baby potatoes or carrots to the skillet after searing the chicken. Toss them in the melted butter and roast everything together.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 1 chicken thigh (with skin, plus pan butter)

Servings: 6

Calories: ~310

Fat: ~25 g

Carbohydrates: ~1 g
