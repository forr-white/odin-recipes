Stuffed Wafflemaker Hash Browns

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

breakfast • American

Using a waffle iron for hash browns is a stroke of genius. The grid pattern creates maximum surface area, meaning you get way more crispy edges than you would in a skillet. Plus, you don't have to worry about flipping them! This recipe takes it a step further by stuffing cheese and ham inside for a complete breakfast pocket.

  • Waffle Iron (Belgian style works best for stuffing)
  • Clean Kitchen Towel (Essential for squeezing water out of the potatoes)
  • Cooking Spray

Ingredients

  • 1 bag (30 oz) frozen shredded hash browns, thawed
  • 4 tablespoons (½ stick) butter, melted
  • ¾ cup cheddar cheese, grated
  • ¾ cup ham, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Olive oil cooking spray (for the iron)

Directions

  1. Preheat: Preheat your waffle iron to medium-high heat.
  2. Squeeze Dry (Crucial): Place the thawed hash browns in the center of a clean kitchen towel. Twist and squeeze vigorously over the sink to remove as much moisture as possible. (Note: If the potatoes are wet, they will steam and become mushy instead of crispy).
  3. Season: Place the dried hash browns in a large bowl. Pour the melted butter over them, add the salt and pepper, and toss until evenly coated.
  4. Layer the Waffle: Spray the waffle iron generously with cooking spray.
    • Bottom: Scoop a heaping ½ cup of hash browns onto the center of the iron and spread slightly.
    • Filling: Sprinkle 2 tablespoons of cheese and some chopped ham in the center.
    • Top: Cover the filling with another ¼ cup of hash browns.
  5. Cook: Close the lid tightly. Cook for 10–15 minutes.
    Tip: Do not trust the "ready" light on your waffle maker—it is timed for batter, not potatoes. Wait until the potatoes are deeply golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve: Carefully remove the hash brown waffle with a fork (it will be hot and fragile). Repeat with remaining ingredients. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Don't Peek! Avoid opening the waffle iron for at least the first 5–8 minutes. If you open it too early while the potatoes are soft, the waffle will split in half.
  • Add-ins: You can customize the filling with cooked bacon, sautéed onions, or peppers. Just make sure any veggie add-ins are cooked first so they don't release water into the waffle.
  • Keeping Warm: Place finished waffles on a wire rack in a 200°F oven to keep them crisp while you cook the rest of the batch.
