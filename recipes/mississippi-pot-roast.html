Mississippi Pot Roast

Ingredients

  • 2 Tablespoons olive oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 2-3 pound chuck roast
  • salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1 packet ranch dressing mix
  • 1 packet dry onion soup mix (or au jus gravy mix)
  • ½ cup (1 stick) salted butter
  • 8 peperoncini peppers

Instructions

  • Heat up a large skillet on high. Add 2 Tablespoons olive oil to hot skillet. You want it really
    hot here to brown or "sear" the beef quickly. This can also be done in your slow cooker if
    you have the sear or saute option.
  • Take a paper towel and make sure you dry both sides of the 2-3 pound chuck roast. Season
    with a little bit of salt and pepper. Once the skillet is nice and hot, add the roast. Using tongs,
    flip the meat over and sear the other side of the roast for another 2-3 minutes.
  • Transfer meat to slow cooker. Sprinkle 1 packet ranch dressing mix and 1 packet dry onion
    soup mix over pot roast. Top with 1/2 cup (1 stick) salted butter then place 8 peperoncini
    peppers on and around the roast.
  • Cover and cook on low for 8 hours. DO NOT open the lid. It’s tempting but leave it alone!
  • After it has cooked, take two forks and start shredding the meat. Discard any big fatty
    pieces. Then serve.
