Mississippi Pot Roast

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American

Often referred to simply as "The Roast" on the internet, this recipe has a cult following for a reason. It sounds like an odd combination—ranch dressing, butter, and pickled peppers—but the result is magic. The vinegar from the peppers cuts through the rich fat of the roast, while the butter creates a velvety sauce. It is arguably the most tender roast you will ever make.

Ingredients

  • 2–3 pound beef chuck roast (boneless)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for searing)
  • 1 packet (1 oz) Ranch dressing mix
  • 1 packet (1 oz) Au Jus gravy mix (or Dry Onion Soup mix)
  • ½ cup (1 stick) salted butter
  • 8 whole peperoncini peppers
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

Directions

  1. Prep the Meat: Pat the chuck roast completely dry with paper towels. This is crucial for a good sear. Season lightly with salt and pepper (go easy on the salt, as the seasoning packets are salty).
  2. Sear: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over high heat. Once the oil is shimmering hot, add the roast. Sear for 2–3 minutes per side until a dark brown crust forms. Note: Do not skip this step; it adds deep flavor to the final dish.
  3. Assemble: Transfer the seared roast to the bottom of your slow cooker. Sprinkle the Ranch packet and the Au Jus (or Onion Soup) packet evenly over the meat.
  4. Top: Place the stick of butter directly on top of the seasoned meat. Scatter the peperoncini peppers around and on top of the roast. (Do not add water or broth; the roast and butter create their own liquid).
  5. Cook: Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours. Do not open the lid during cooking, as this releases heat and moisture.
  6. Shred: After 8 hours, the meat should be falling apart. Use two forks to shred the beef directly in the slow cooker, discarding any large pieces of fat. Toss the meat in the juices.
  7. Serve: Serve hot over mashed potatoes, egg noodles, or on sandwich buns.

Notes

  • Au Jus vs. Onion Soup: The original "Mississippi" recipe calls for Au Jus gravy mix. However, many people prefer Dry Onion Soup mix for a stronger onion flavor. Both work beautifully.
  • Butter Amount: If you prefer a less greasy gravy, you can reduce the butter to ½ stick (4 tablespoons), though the full stick provides the signature richness.
  • Spice Level: This dish is generally mild. The peppers add tang, not heat. If you want heat, slice the peppers open before cooking to release the seeds.
