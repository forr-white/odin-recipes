Honey-Lime Grilled Chicken

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican Fusion

This recipe is all about the glaze. By simmering the honey-lime marinade into a thick syrup before basting, you achieve a beautiful charred lacquer on the chicken that is both tangy and sweet. Using chicken thighs ensures the meat stays juicy even over the high heat of the grill.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs (trimmed of excess fat)

The Marinade & Glaze

  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • ¼ cup fresh lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon finely grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 medium cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt

Directions

  1. Mix Marinade: Combine all marinade ingredients in a small saucepan and stir well until the honey is fully incorporated.
  2. Marinate: Transfer 3 tablespoons of the mixture to a large zippered bag. Add the chicken thighs, seal, and massage well to coat. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to 12 hours.
  3. Reduce the Glaze: Heat the remaining marinade in the saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil and cook until syrupy and reduced by half (about 1–2 minutes). Watch carefully so it doesn't boil over. Set aside to cool.
  4. Grill (Option 1): Preheat an outdoor grill to medium-high. Add chicken and sprinkle lightly with extra salt and pepper. Cook for 12–15 minutes, turning frequently and basting generously with the reduced glaze during the last 5 minutes.
  5. Sauté (Option 2): Heat oil in a large skillet until almost smoking. Add chicken in batches (don't overcrowd). Cook for about 10 minutes, turning frequently and brushing with the glaze until charred in places and cooked through.
  6. Rest: Transfer chicken to a platter, tent loosely with foil, and allow to rest for 5 minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute.

Notes

  • Temperature Tip: While the original recipe suggests 160°F, chicken thighs are actually more tender when cooked to 175°F (80°C), as the dark meat connective tissue breaks down better at higher temperatures.
  • Ginger: For the best flavor, use a Microplane to grate fresh ginger. If you're out of fresh, you can substitute with ¼ tsp ground ginger.
  • Glazing: Because of the honey content, the glaze can burn if applied too early. Save the heavy basting for the final few minutes of cooking.
