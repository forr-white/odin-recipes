Classic Italian Meatballs

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian

There is nothing more comforting than a pot of homemade meatballs simmering on the stove. Unlike many recipes that require frying or baking first, these meatballs are poached directly in the tomato sauce. This keeps them incredibly tender and infuses the sauce with savory depth. The blend of beef and pork ensures they stay juicy and flavorful.

  • Dutch Oven or Large Pot (Essential for simmering the sauce and meatballs together)
  • Large Mixing Bowl (For combining the meat mixture without overworking it)
  • Microplane or Box Grater (Freshly grated cheese makes a big difference)

Ingredients

Meatballs

  • 1 lb ground meat (50/50 beef & pork blend recommended)
  • ⅔ cup fresh bread crumbs (or ½ cup panko)
  • ⅓ cup milk or water
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp finely grated Parmesan or Romano cheese (optional but recommended)
  • 1 tsp coarse or kosher salt
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes or black pepper
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 garlic clove, minced

Sauce

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 (28 oz) can tomato puree or crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp coarse or kosher salt

Directions

1. Prepare the Meatballs

  1. Mix: In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, bread crumbs, milk (or water), parsley, cheese, 1 teaspoon salt, pepper, onion powder, eggs, and half of the minced garlic.
  2. Combine: Mix gently with a fork or your hands just until ingredients are evenly distributed. Do not overmix, or the meatballs will be tough.
  3. Shape: Form into 1½ to 2-inch meatballs and place them on a plate.
  4. Chill: Refrigerate the formed meatballs for 30 minutes. This helps them firm up so they don't fall apart in the sauce.

2. Make the Sauce

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the remaining garlic and red pepper flakes; cook until lightly golden and fragrant, about 30 seconds to 1 minute.
  2. Simmer Base: Carefully add the tomatoes (watch for splatter) and season with the remaining salt. Simmer on low for 10 minutes if using puree, or 20 minutes if using crushed tomatoes.

3. Cook the Meatballs

  1. Poach: Reduce the heat to the lowest gentle simmer. Carefully drop the meatballs into the sauce one by one.
  2. Cover: Cover the pot and do not stir for at least the first 20 minutes. Stirring too early can break the meatballs before they set.
  3. Finish: After 20 minutes, you can gently stir to ensure even cooking. Cook for a total of about 25–30 minutes, or until the meatballs are fully cooked through. Cut one open to check if needed. Serve hot.

Notes

  • With Spaghetti: Cook spaghetti until very al dente. Reserve some pasta water. Toss drained pasta with a splash of water and some sauce over high heat for 1 minute, then top with meatballs.
  • Saucy Option: If your family prefers lots of extra sauce for dipping bread, use an extra ½ to 1 can of tomatoes.
  • Meatball Parm Style: Transfer cooked meatballs and sauce to a baking dish, top with torn mozzarella, and broil until bubbly. Finish with extra Parmesan and toasted breadcrumbs.
