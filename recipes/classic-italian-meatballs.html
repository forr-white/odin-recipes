Classic Italian Meatballs

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian

Ingredients

Meatballs

  • 1 lb ground meat (beef & pork blend recommended)
  • ⅔ cup fresh bread crumbs or ½ cup panko
  • ⅓ cup milk or water
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped parsley
  • 2 tbsp finely grated Parmesan or Romano cheese (optional)
  • 1 tsp coarse or kosher salt
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes or black pepper
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 garlic clove, minced

Sauce

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 (28 oz) can tomato puree or crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp coarse or kosher salt

Instructions

1. Prepare the Meatballs

  1. In a large bowl, combine the meat, bread crumbs, milk/water, parsley, cheese (if using), 1 teaspoon salt, pepper, onion powder, eggs, and half of the minced garlic.
  2. Mix gently with a fork to evenly distribute ingredients.
  3. Form into 1½–2-inch meatballs and place on a plate.
  4. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to help them firm up.

2. Make the Sauce

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the remaining garlic and pepper flakes; cook until lightly golden, 30 seconds to 1 minute.
  3. Add tomatoes carefully and season with remaining salt.
  4. Simmer on low for 10 minutes (puree) or 20 minutes (crushed tomatoes).

3. Cook the Meatballs

  1. Reduce heat to the lowest gentle simmer.
  2. Add meatballs to the sauce one by one.
  3. Cover and do not stir for at least the first 20 minutes.
  4. Cook a total of about 25 minutes, or until fully done. Cut one open to check if needed.

Notes

  • As-is: Serve straight from the pot with crusty bread.
  • With spaghetti: Cook spaghetti very al dente. Reserve pasta water. Toss drained pasta with a splash of water and some sauce over high heat for 1 minute, then add meatballs.
  • Saucy option: Use an extra ½–1 can of tomatoes if your family prefers lots of sauce.
  • “Parmesan-ed”: Transfer meatballs to a baking dish, top with torn mozzarella, and broil until bubbly. Finish with Parmesan, toasted breadcrumbs, or parsley.
