Rigatoni with Sausage, Tomatoes, and Zucchini

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian

This is the perfect bridge between a heavy meat sauce and a light summer pasta. Instead of a thick marinara, we create a "broken" sauce using white wine, the juices from bursting cherry tomatoes, and the rendered fat from the Italian sausage. The rigatoni ridges catch every bit of flavor, while the zucchini adds a fresh bite.

  • Pasta Pot with Strainer
  • Wooden Spoon (Best for breaking up sausage and scraping the fond off the pan)

Ingredients

The Pasta

  • ¾ lb rigatoni pasta (or penne/ziti)
  • Salt for boiling water

The Rustic Sauce

  • 1 lb Italian sausage (mild, sweet, or spicy), casings removed
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 2 small zucchini, diced into half-moons
  • 2 cups grape or cherry tomatoes
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup dry white wine (or chicken broth)
  • ¼ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)

Finishing

  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ cup fresh basil, chopped
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Directions

  1. Boil Water: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over medium-high heat. (Don't add the pasta yet).
  2. Sauté Base: Add olive oil to a large, wide pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion and crushed red pepper flakes. Cook until the onion is very soft (about 8–10 minutes).
  3. Brown Meat: Add the sausage. Use a wooden spoon to break it into small crumbles. Cook until the sausage is browned and cooked through.
  4. Aromatics: Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another minute to caramelize it slightly (this removes the "tinny" taste).
  5. Simmer Sauce: Add the grape tomatoes and white wine (or stock). Scrape the bottom of the pan to release any browned bits. Season with salt and pepper. Cook for about 10 minutes. The tomatoes should burst and release their juices. (Tip: Press them gently with your spoon to help them pop).
  6. Cook Pasta & Zucchini: While the sauce simmers, drop the rigatoni into the boiling water. Cook until al dente (usually 1 minute less than package directions).
    Simultaneously, stir the zucchini into the sauce and cook for just 5 minutes so it stays crisp-tender.
  7. Combine: Before draining the pasta, reserve ½ cup of the starchy cooking water. Drain the pasta and immediately toss it into the pot with the sauce.
  8. Finish: Stir vigorously to coat the pasta. If the sauce looks dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta water. Remove from heat. Stir in the fresh basil.
  9. Serve: Divide among bowls and top generously with grated Parmesan cheese.

Notes

  • Wine Selection: Use a dry white wine like Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc. The acidity cuts through the fat of the sausage. If avoiding alcohol, chicken broth works, though the flavor will be less bright.
  • Zucchini Texture: Add the zucchini near the end (Step 6). If you add it too early with the onions, it will turn into mush by the time the pasta is ready.
  • Pasta Shape: Rigatoni is ideal because the sauce gets trapped inside the tubes, but Penne or Orecchiette are great substitutes.
