High Protein Potato Bowls

dinner • American

Ingredients

Bowl Ingredients

  • 4 large sweet potatoes (approx. 1200g), cubed
  • 0-calorie avocado spray (for roasting)
  • 1 ½ tsp salt, divided
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika (for potatoes)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (for potatoes)
  • 1 white onion (approx. 150g), diced
  • 2 lb (908g) lean ground beef (96/4)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 ½ tsp paprika (½ tsp smoked + 1 tsp regular)
  • 1–2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust for spice)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1–2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce (optional but recommended)
  • 1 bag Mexican cheese

Garlic Herb Sauce

  • 1 cup (240g) non-fat Greek yogurt (or dairy-free substitute)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill or cilantro (optional flavor boost)
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Water, as needed to thin

Directions

  1. Roast the sweet potatoes: Preheat oven to 400°F. Spray a baking tray with avocado oil. Toss cubed sweet potatoes with salt, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. Roast for 30–40 minutes, flipping halfway for caramelized edges.
  2. Sauté aromatics: Sauté diced onions for 3–5 minutes until softened and slightly browned. Add garlic and cook until fragrant.
  3. Brown the beef: Add ground beef and cook until halfway browned. Stir in tomato paste, cumin, paprika, chili powder, cayenne, and black pepper. Cook 2–3 more minutes to toast the spices. Add Worcestershire and cook until fully browned.
  4. Make the Garlic Herb Sauce: Blend yogurt, lemon juice, parsley, optional dill/cilantro, garlic powder, onion powder, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika (if using), salt, and pepper. Thin with water to desired consistency.
  5. Assemble: Divide roasted potatoes into 8 portions. Top with beef mixture and finish with Mexican cheese. Drizzle with garlic herb sauce or serve on the side.

Notes

  • For extra crispy sweet potatoes, leave space between cubes so they roast instead of steam.
  • Ground turkey or chicken can replace beef for a lighter option without sacrificing protein.
  • Tomato paste and Worcestershire add major depth — don't skip them if you want maximum flavor.
  • The Garlic Herb Sauce also works great as a dip for roasted vegetables or as a spread for wraps.
  • To meal prep, assemble bowls without sauce; add sauce right before eating.
  • If sauce thickens in the fridge, whisk in a splash of lemon juice or water.

Nutritional Information

Carbohydrates: 32 g
