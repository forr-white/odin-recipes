High Protein Potato Bowls

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American

Sweet potatoes meet savory spiced beef in this meal-prep friendly bowl. The secret weapon is the yogurt-based garlic herb sauce, which adds creaminess and extra protein without the heavy calories of traditional mayonnaise-based dressings. It’s perfect for fueling an active lifestyle.

Ingredients

The Potatoes

  • 4 large sweet potatoes (approx. 1200g), cubed
  • Avocado oil spray
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp salt

The Beef Mixture

  • 2 lb (908g) lean ground beef (96/4 recommended)
  • 1 white onion (approx. 150g), diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1–2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce (optional but recommended)
  • Spices: 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, ½ tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp regular paprika, 1–2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust for heat), 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp black pepper.
  • 1 bag (8 oz) Mexican blend cheese

Garlic Herb Sauce

  • 1 cup (240g) non-fat Greek yogurt (or dairy-free substitute)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill or cilantro (optional)
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Water, to thin as needed

Directions

  1. Roast Potatoes: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Spray a large baking tray with avocado oil. Toss the cubed sweet potatoes with salt, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. Spread them in a single layer (use two pans if needed to avoid steaming). Roast for 30–40 minutes, flipping halfway, until edges are caramelized.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: While potatoes roast, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onions and sauté for 3–5 minutes until softened. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Cook Beef: Add the ground beef to the skillet. Cook, breaking it up, until mostly browned.
  4. Season: Stir in the tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, and all the beef spices (cumin, paprika, chili powder, cayenne, salt, pepper). Cook for another 2–3 minutes to toast the spices and fully cook the beef.
  5. Make Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, lemon juice, herbs, garlic powder, onion powder, Dijon, salt, and pepper. Add water one teaspoon at a time until it reaches a drizzle-able consistency.
  6. Assemble: Divide the roasted potatoes into 8 bowls/containers. Top with the beef mixture and a sprinkle of Mexican cheese. Drizzle with the garlic herb sauce right before serving.

Notes

  • Crispy Potatoes: The key to non-mushy sweet potatoes is space. If they are piled on top of each other, they will steam.
  • Protein Swap: Ground turkey or chicken works perfectly if you don't eat red meat.
  • Meal Prep Tip: Store the sauce in a separate small container or dressing cup so you can reheat the beef and potatoes without heating the yogurt sauce.
  • Sauce Thickness: The sauce may thicken in the fridge overnight. Simply stir in a few drops of water or lemon juice to loosen it up again.
