Creamy Tuscan Meatball Gnocchi

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian-American

Ingredients

Base

  • 1.5 lbs thawed meatballs
  • Chicken stock (enough to cover meatballs)
  • 1 handful chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 3 tsp jarred garlic or 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1–1½ tsp salt
  • 1–2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Add-Ins

  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 1 lb gnocchi
  • 2–3 cups baby spinach
  • ½–1 cup heavy cream

Instructions

  1. Add thawed meatballs to a pot and pour in enough chicken stock to just cover them.
  2. Add sun-dried tomatoes, garlic, salt, Italian seasoning, and garlic powder.
  3. Cover and bring to a boil. Reduce to medium and cook 10–12 minutes.
  4. Stir in cream cheese until fully melted and combined.
  5. Add gnocchi, cover, and cook for 3–4 minutes until tender.
  6. Stir in baby spinach and cook until wilted.
  7. Pour in heavy cream and simmer 2–3 minutes to thicken.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

Notes

  • Add red pepper flakes for a spicy kick.
  • A splash of white wine adds depth before adding cream cheese.
  • Use turkey or chicken meatballs for a lighter version.
  • Add extra chicken stock or cream if reheating—the gnocchi absorbs liquid.
