Creamy Tuscan Meatball Gnocchi

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian-American

This one-pot wonder combines pillowy gnocchi and savory meatballs in a rich, creamy sauce inspired by Tuscan flavors. With tangy sun-dried tomatoes, fresh spinach, and a luxurious garlic cream base, it is a hearty, restaurant-quality meal that comes together in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients

Base

  • 1.5 lbs thawed meatballs (pre-cooked frozen style recommended)
  • Chicken stock (approx. 2–3 cups, enough to just cover meatballs)
  • 1 handful chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 3 tsp jarred garlic or 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1–1½ tsp salt
  • 1–2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Add-Ins

  • 8 oz cream cheese, cut into cubes
  • 1 lb potato gnocchi
  • 2–3 cups baby spinach
  • ½–1 cup heavy cream

Directions

  1. Simmer Base: Add the thawed meatballs to a large pot or Dutch oven and pour in enough chicken stock to just cover them. Add the sun-dried tomatoes, minced garlic, salt, Italian seasoning, and garlic powder.
  2. Cook Meatballs: Cover and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium and cook for 10–12 minutes to infuse the broth with flavor and heat the meatballs through.
  3. Melt Cheese: Stir in the cubed cream cheese. Whisk or stir continuously until it is fully melted and combined with the broth to create a creamy base.
  4. Cook Gnocchi: Add the gnocchi, cover again, and cook for 3–4 minutes until the gnocchi are tender and pillowy.
  5. Wilt Spinach: Stir in the baby spinach and cook for 1 minute until wilted.
  6. Finish: Pour in the heavy cream and simmer for 2–3 minutes to thicken the sauce to your liking. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed.

Notes

  • Spice It Up: Add red pepper flakes with the garlic for a spicy "Arrabbiata" kick.
  • Wine: A splash of dry white wine added before the cream cheese brings a nice acidity to balance the richness.
  • Meat Choice: Use turkey or chicken meatballs for a lighter version, or spicy Italian sausage meatballs for more flavor.
  • Leftovers: The gnocchi will continue to absorb liquid in the fridge. When reheating, add a splash of chicken stock or milk to loosen the sauce.
