Classic Tres Leches Cake

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • Mexican

Tres Leches ("Three Milks") is one of the most beloved Latin American desserts. It starts with a light, airy sponge cake that is essentially dry on its own, designed specifically to act as a sponge. It is then soaked in a rich mixture of heavy cream, evaporated milk, and condensed milk, transforming it into a decadent, moist, and sweet pudding-like cake.

  • 9-inch Springform Pan (Best for removing the cake cleanly, though a 9x13 dish works too)
  • Electric Mixer (Crucial for whipping the egg whites to stiff peaks)
  • Wooden Skewer or Fork (For poking holes in the cake to absorb the milk)
  • Rubber Spatula (For gently folding the whites into the batter)

Ingredients

The Cake Sponge

  • 4 Large eggs, separated, room temperature
  • ⅔ cup sugar, divided (⅓ cup for yolks, ⅓ cup for whites)
  • ⅔ cup cake flour (sifted)
  • Dash of salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

The "Tres Leches" Soak

  • ¾ cup heavy whipping cream
  • ¾ cup evaporated milk
  • ¾ cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon rum extract (optional, but authentic)

Topping

  • 1¼ cups heavy whipping cream
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • Optional: Dulce de leche or sliced fresh strawberries for garnish

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan with parchment paper and grease the paper/sides.
  2. Beat Yolks: In a large bowl, beat the 4 egg yolks until slightly thickened. Gradually add ⅓ cup sugar, beating on high speed until thick and lemon-colored (ribbon stage). Fold in the vanilla and the cake flour, a third at a time, being careful not to overmix.
  3. Whip Whites: In a separate clean bowl, add the salt to the 4 egg whites. With clean beaters, beat on medium until soft peaks form. Gradually add the remaining ⅓ cup sugar, 1 tablespoon at a time, beating on high after each addition until sugar is dissolved and stiff, glossy peaks form.
  4. Fold: Gently fold a third of the egg whites into the yolk batter to lighten it. Then, carefully fold in the remaining whites until just combined. Do not deflate the batter. Gently spread into the prepared pan.
  5. Bake: Bake until the top springs back when lightly touched, about 20–25 minutes. Cool for 10 minutes in the pan, then remove the ring and transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  6. The Soak: Once cool, place the cake on a rimmed serving plate (rimmed is important to catch runoff). Poke holes all over the top with a skewer or fork. In a small bowl or pitcher, whisk together the cream, evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and extracts. Pour slowly and evenly over the cake, allowing it to absorb.
  7. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is even better) to allow the cake to fully soak up the liquid.
  8. Top & Serve: Just before serving, beat the 1¼ cups heavy cream until it begins to thicken. Add the 3 tablespoons sugar and beat until soft peaks form. Spread generously over the top of the cake. Garnish with dulce de leche or strawberries if desired.

Notes

  • Room Temperature Eggs: Cold eggs are harder to whip to full volume. Separate your eggs while cold, then let them sit at room temp for 30 minutes before whipping.
  • Cake Flour: Using cake flour instead of all-purpose flour ensures a lighter, finer crumb that absorbs the milk better without becoming mushy.
  • Serving Dish: Ensure your serving platter has a lip or rim. Even after soaking, a little milk might seep out at the bottom.
