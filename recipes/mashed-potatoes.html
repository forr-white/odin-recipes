Mashed Potatoes

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • American

Yukon Gold potatoes are the gold standard for mashing because of their naturally buttery flavor and middle-of-the-road starch content. Unlike Russets, which can be mealy, Yukons hold their structure while still becoming incredibly creamy. This recipe uses room-temperature dairy to ensure the potatoes stay hot and fluffy during the mixing process.

  • Large Stock Pot (6-quart or larger)
  • Potato Ricer or Masher (A ricer creates the smoothest, lump-free texture)
  • Vegetable Peeler

Ingredients

  • 3.5 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes
  • 3.5 teaspoons salt (for boiling water), plus more to taste
  • 6 tablespoons salted butter, room temperature
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • 1 cup half-and-half
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper, plus more to taste

Directions

  1. Prep Dairy: In a small bowl, whisk together the half-and-half and sour cream. Set aside and allow to come to room temperature. Adding cold dairy to hot potatoes can make them gummy.
  2. Prep Potatoes: Peel the potatoes and rinse them well. Cut each potato into 3–4 even slices (keeping the pieces large prevents them from absorbing too much water). Place them in a large pot.
  3. Start Cold: Add cold water to the pot until the potatoes are submerged by at least an inch. Always start potatoes in cold water so the outside and inside cook at the same rate.
  4. Simmer: Bring to a gentle boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, add the 3.5 tsp salt. Reduce heat to maintain a soft boil and cook for 20–25 minutes until very fork-tender.
  5. Steam Dry: Drain the potatoes thoroughly. Place them back into the warm pot (with the heat off) for 5 minutes. This allows excess moisture to escape as steam, ensuring your mash isn't watery.
  6. Butter & Mash: Add the butter to the pot and let it melt into the hot potatoes. Use a potato masher or ricer to break them down.
  7. Incorporate: Gradually drizzle in about ¾ of the dairy mixture. Continue mashing until creamy and smooth. Only add the remaining liquid if you prefer a thinner consistency. Note: Do not over-mash, or the starch will turn "gluey."
  8. Season: Taste and add more salt or pepper as needed. Serve immediately with a pat of butter on top.

Notes

  • No Lumps: If you want restaurant-quality smoothness, pass the cooked potatoes through a ricer or food mill instead of using a hand masher.
  • Gravy: These potatoes are the perfect base for our Homemade Brown Gravy.
  • Reheating: If making these ahead of time, reheat them in a slow cooker on "warm" with an extra splash of milk to keep them moist.
