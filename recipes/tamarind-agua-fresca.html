Tamarind Agua Fresca

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • Mexican

Agua de Tamarindo is a staple in Mexican taquerias. Made from the pulp of the tamarind pod, it has a unique flavor profile that balances earthy sweetness with a distinct sour tang—think of it as a more complex lemonade. This recipe uses tamarind concentrate for a quick, accessible version that doesn't require boiling and peeling pods.

Ingredients

The Drink

  • 6 cups cold water
  • ⅓ cup tamarind concentrate (adjust to taste)
  • ⅓ cup white sugar (or agave syrup)

To Serve

  • 6 cups ice
  • 6 slices fresh orange
  • 6 slices fresh lime
  • Optional: Tajín (chili-lime salt) for the rim

Directions

  1. Mix Base: In a large pitcher, combine the water and the sugar. Stir vigorously until the sugar is completely dissolved.
  2. Add Tamarind: Stir in the tamarind concentrate. (Note: Concentrate is thick and sticky, so you may need to press it against the side of the pitcher with your spoon to get it to dissolve fully).
  3. Taste Test: Taste the mixture. Tamarind brands vary in acidity.
    • Too sour? Add another tablespoon of sugar.
    • Too weak? Add another spoonful of concentrate.
  4. Serve: Fill six glasses with plenty of ice. Pour the Agua Fresca over the ice.
  5. Garnish: Garnish each glass with a slice of orange and a slice of lime.

Notes

  • Spicy Twist: Tamarind pairs beautifully with chili. For a spicy version, rim the glasses with Tajín (chili-lime seasoning) before pouring the drink.
  • Concentrate vs. Paste: Make sure you buy "Tamarind Concentrate" (usually found in Asian or Latin aisles). If you buy "Tamarind Paste" (a solid block), you will need to dissolve it in boiling water and strain out the seeds/fibers first.
  • Carbonated Version: For a sparkling soda version, fill the glass halfway with the tamarind mixture and top with club soda or sparkling water.
