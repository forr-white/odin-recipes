Pineapple Upside Down Cake

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

This retro classic remains a favorite for a reason. There is something magical about pouring cake batter over a layer of butter and brown sugar; as it bakes, the bottom becomes a gooey, caramelized glaze that seeps into the fruit. When flipped, you have a stunning, glistening presentation that requires no frosting.

  • 9x13 Baking Dish (Glass or ceramic works best for monitoring the browning)

  • Offset Spatula (For spreading the thick batter over the fruit without disturbing the design)

Ingredients

The Caramel Fruit Topping

  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 (20-oz) cans pineapple rings, drained (pat dry)
  • 1 cup maraschino cherries (stems removed)

The Cake Batter

  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 egg yolks, room temperature
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk, room temperature
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat the oven to 350°F. Spray a 9x13 baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Assemble Topping: Pour the melted stick of butter into the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Sprinkle the packed brown sugar evenly over the melted butter. Arrange the pineapple rings on top of the sugar. Place a cherry in the center of each ring and in the gaps between rings. Set aside.
  3. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, sift together the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt.
  4. Cream Butter & Sugar: In a large bowl using an electric mixer, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy (about 2 minutes).
  5. Add Wets: Beat in the eggs and egg yolks one at a time. Add the oil and vanilla extract. Mix until just combined.
  6. Combine (Alternating Method): Add the flour mixture in three parts, alternating with the milk.
    Order: ⅓ Flour → ½ Milk → ⅓ Flour → ½ Milk → Remaining Flour.
    Mix gently after each addition until just combined. Do not overmix.
  7. Bake: Carefully pour the thick batter over the arranged fruit. Use a spatula to smooth it into an even layer, being careful not to shift the pineapples underneath. Bake for 35–45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool & Flip: Remove from the oven. Let cool for exactly 30 minutes. Run a knife around the edges. Place a large serving platter or cutting board upside down over the pan. Using oven mitts, grip both the pan and the board, and flip quickly. Lift the pan off to reveal the cake.
  9. Serve: Serve warm or at room temperature.

Notes

  • Dry the Fruit: Use paper towels to pat the pineapple rings and cherries dry before placing them in the pan. Excess juice can make the cake soggy.
  • The Flip Timing: Waiting 30 minutes is crucial. If you flip too soon, the cake is too fragile and might break. If you wait too long, the caramel hardens and the fruit sticks to the pan.
  • Room Temp Ingredients: Ensure your milk and eggs are at room temperature so the batter emulsifies properly instead of curdling.
