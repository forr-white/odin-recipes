Chicken Cobbler

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American

This Chicken Cobbler took the internet by storm for a reason. It is the ultimate "dump-and-bake" comfort meal that tastes like a cross between a rich chicken pot pie and a buttery biscuit casserole. The secret weapon? A box of Cheddar Bay Biscuit mix that forms a perfect golden crust over the savory filling.

Recommended Tools

  • 9×13-inch Baking Dish (Standard size is essential for the layers to set correctly)
  • Whisk (For mixing the biscuit batter and the soup layer)
  • Mixing Bowls (You'll need two: one for the biscuit mix, one for the broth)

Ingredients

Base

  • 1 stick unsalted butter, melted
  • Shredded rotisserie chicken (enough to cover the bottom of the dish, approx. 3–4 cups)
  • 1 bag (10–12 oz) frozen mixed vegetables (peas and carrots blend works best)

Seasonings

  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp dried thyme

Biscuit Layer

  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 box Red Lobster Cheddar Bay Biscuit mix (including the seasoning packet)

Top Layer

  • 2 cups chicken broth (approx. 1½ cans)
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of chicken soup

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Butter Base: Pour the melted butter directly into the bottom of a 9×13-inch casserole dish. Tilt the pan if needed so the bottom is fully coated.
  3. Chicken & Veggies: Spread the shredded rotisserie chicken evenly over the melted butter. Sprinkle the frozen mixed vegetables evenly over the chicken. Do not stir.
  4. Season: Sprinkle the salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and thyme over the chicken and vegetables.
  5. Biscuit Layer: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the milk, Cheddar Bay Biscuit mix, and the included seasoning packet until just combined (a few lumps are okay). Pour this mixture evenly over the chicken and vegetables. Do not stir.
  6. Soup Layer: In a separate bowl, whisk together the chicken broth and cream of chicken soup until smooth. Gently pour this mixture over the biscuit layer, trying to cover the surface as evenly as possible. Do not stir! (Trust the process).
  7. Bake: Bake, uncovered, at 350°F for 60–70 minutes. You want the top to be golden brown and the center to be set (it shouldn't be liquid/jiggly).
  8. Rest: Remove from the oven and let the cobbler rest for about 10–15 minutes before serving. This allows the sauce to thicken up perfectly.

Notes

  • The "Do Not Stir" Rule: Just like a fruit cobbler, the layers will magically swap places and settle during baking. Stirring will ruin this effect and result in a gummy texture.
  • Flavor Variations:
    • Creamy Herb: Use garlic powder, dried parsley, dill, and Italian seasoning.
    • Southern Comfort: Use seasoned salt, paprika, and poultry seasoning.
    • Cajun Kick: Add Cajun seasoning and a pinch of cayenne.
  • Vegetables: You can swap frozen mixed vegetables for just peas and carrots, or even diced fresh vegetables (though fresh veggies may add a bit more crunch).
  • Browning: If the cobbler browns too quickly on top before the 60 minutes are up, tent loosely with foil during the final 15 minutes.
