Royal Icing

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

Royal Icing is the secret to those perfectly smooth, bakery-style decorated cookies. Unlike buttercream, it dries hard to a matte finish, allowing you to stack cookies for gifts or shipping. This recipe uses meringue powder instead of raw egg whites, making it safer to eat, more stable, and easier to store.

  • Stand Mixer (Essential for getting the icing fluffy and white without tiring your arm)
  • Gel Food Coloring (Water-based liquid drops will ruin the consistency; gel is best)
  • Piping Bags or Squeeze Bottles

Ingredients

  • 4 cups powdered sugar (confectioners' sugar)
  • 3 tablespoons meringue powder
  • 9–10 tablespoons warm water (plus more for thinning)
  • Gel food coloring (optional)

Directions

  1. Combine: In the large bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the whisk attachment (or using a hand mixer), combine the powdered sugar, meringue powder, and 9 tablespoons of water.
  2. Whip: Beat on high speed for 1.5 to 2 minutes. The icing should become bright white and fluffy.
  3. Check Consistency (The 10-Second Rule): Lift the whisk out of the bowl. The icing should drizzle down and smooth itself out completely within 5–10 seconds.
    • Too thick? Add more water, 1 teaspoon at a time.
    • Too thin? Add a little more sifted powdered sugar.
  4. Color: If using colors, separate the icing into small bowls and stir in gel food coloring. Cover any unused icing immediately (see notes).
  5. Dry: Pipe onto cool cookies. Allow the cookies to dry at room temperature for at least 2 hours (or overnight for a rock-hard finish). To speed up drying, place cookies in front of a gentle fan—do not put them in the refrigerator, as humidity can cause colors to bleed.

Notes

  • Cover It Up: Royal icing dries incredibly fast. When not actively using it, cover the surface of the icing directly with plastic wrap or a damp paper towel to prevent a crust from forming.
  • Grease-Free Zone: Ensure your bowl and whisk are completely free of grease or oil, or the meringue will not set properly.
  • Consistencies: This recipe creates a "flood" consistency (smooths out in 10 seconds). If you need "stiff" icing for writing text or making flowers, use less water (start with 5-6 tbsp).
