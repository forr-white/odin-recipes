Copycat Texas Roadhouse Rolls

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

bread • American

These rolls are legendary for a reason. They are pillowy soft, slightly sweet, and glazed with melted butter right out of the oven. The secret to their distinct texture is the scalding of the milk and the generous amount of yeast. Serve them warm with the signature cinnamon honey butter for the full restaurant experience.

  • Stand Mixer (This dough is sticky and requires 8-10 minutes of kneading)
  • Instant Read Thermometer (Crucial for ensuring the milk isn't too hot for the yeast)
  • Baking Sheet (Standard half-sheet pan)
  • Pizza Cutter (The easiest way to cut the dough into uniform squares)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened, divided (4 tbsp for dough, 2 tbsp for brushing)
  • 1 (¼-oz.) envelope active dry yeast (approx. 2¼ tsp)
  • 1½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 large egg yolk, at room temperature
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature, divided (1 for dough, 1 for egg wash)
  • 3¾ cups all-purpose flour, divided, plus more for work surface
  • Cooking spray
  • 1 tablespoon water (for egg wash)
  • Cinnamon Honey Butter, for serving

Directions

  1. Scald Milk: Heat milk in a small saucepan over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until just bubbling around the edges (scalding), about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and pour into the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook attachment.
  2. Cool & Bloom: Stir in sugar and 4 tablespoons butter; let cool, stirring occasionally, until mixture is between 110°F and 120°F (43°C and 48°C), about 10 minutes. Stir in yeast; let stand until mixture is foamy, about 10 minutes.
  3. Mix Dough: Add salt, egg yolk, 1 egg, and ½ of the flour. Beat on low speed until combined, about 1 minute. Still on low speed, carefully add the remaining flour, ½ cup at a time, and beat until dough just comes together.
  4. Knead: The dough will be very sticky and wet. Increase speed to medium, and beat until dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl and is smooth and elastic, 8 to 10 minutes.
  5. First Rise: Coat a large bowl with cooking spray; transfer dough to the prepared bowl. Cover loosely and let rise in a warm place until about doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  6. Shape: Punch down dough and turn onto a lightly floured surface. Press or roll dough into a rectangle about ½-inch thick. Trim edges to create a 16x8-inch rectangle. Fold the dough in half (long ways) to form a 16x4-inch rectangle; pinch the edges to seal. (This fold creates the pillowy layers).
  7. Cut & Second Rise: Use a sharp knife or pizza cutter to cut the dough into 16 (2-inch) squares. Place squares onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet about ½-inch apart. Cover loosely with plastic wrap; set aside at room temperature until doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  8. Bake: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Whisk together the remaining egg and water. Brush tops of rolls with egg wash. Bake until golden brown, 12 to 16 minutes.
  9. Finish: Melt the remaining 2 tablespoons butter. Immediately brush the hot rolls with melted butter. Serve warm with Cinnamon Honey Butter.

Notes

  • Don't Rush the Rise: Yeast needs time. If your kitchen is cool, the rise might take longer than an hour. Look for the "doubled in size" visual cue rather than just the clock.
  • Sticky is Good: Resist the urge to add too much extra flour during kneading. A tacky dough yields a softer roll.
  • The Fold: The folding step in the directions is what gives these rolls their signature height and ability to pull apart easily.
