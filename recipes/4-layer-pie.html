4 Layer Pie

Submitted by Penny Dillard

desserts • American

This classic dessert—often called "Chocolate Delight," "Chocolate Lush," or "Better Than Anything Cake"—is a crowd favorite for a reason. It starts with a crunchy, buttery pecan crust, followed by a sweetened cream cheese layer, a rich chocolate pudding layer, and is finally crowned with whipped topping. It’s cool, creamy, and perfect for potlucks.

Ingredients

Layer 1 (The Crust)

  • 1 ½ cups flour
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 stick butter, melted

Layer 2 (Cream Cheese)

  • 1 (8 oz) package cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 8 oz Cool Whip

Layer 3 (Pudding)

  • 1 large or 2 small packages instant chocolate pudding
  • 2 ¾ cups milk

Layer 4 (Topping)

  • 8 oz Cool Whip
  • Chopped pecans for topping (optional)

Directions

  1. Layer 1: Mix flour, chopped pecans, and melted butter until combined. Pat mixture into a 9×13-inch pan. Bake at 350°F for 20 minutes. Cool completely.
  2. Layer 2: In a mixing bowl, beat cream cheese, powdered sugar, and Cool Whip until smooth. Spread over cooled crust. Chill until set.
  3. Layer 3: Prepare instant chocolate pudding with milk using a mixer until thickened. Spread over the cream cheese layer. Chill until set.
  4. Layer 4: Spread remaining Cool Whip over the pudding layer. Top with chopped pecans. Serve chilled.

Notes

  • Cool the Crust: Make sure the crust cools completely before adding the next layers or they’ll melt and mix.
  • Cream Cheese: Use full-fat cream cheese for the smoothest texture in the second layer.
  • Chill Time: Chill the dessert for at least 4 hours (or overnight) for clean slices and firm layers.
  • Milk Type: Instant pudding sets best with cold cow's milk; don’t use plant milk unless the pudding box specifies it will work.
  • Cool Whip vs Cream: You can substitute homemade whipped cream for Cool Whip, but the final texture will be softer.
  • Toasting Nuts: Toast the pecans before adding them to boost flavor and crunch.
  • Servings: For a lighter dessert, cut into 18–24 pieces — it’s rich!
  • Storage: This dessert keeps well in the fridge for 3–4 days; do not freeze.
