King Ranch Casserole

Submitted by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Tex-Mex

Named after one of the largest ranches in the world, King Ranch Casserole is a quintessential Texas comfort food. While many modern versions rely on "cream of" canned soups, this recipe takes it back to its roots with a from-scratch sauce, fresh poblanos, and perfectly poached chicken. It’s essentially a Tex-Mex lasagna layered with corn tortillas instead of pasta.

Ingredients

For the Poached Chicken

  • 1½ tbsp cumin seeds
  • 3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 10 oz each)
  • 6 cups unsalted chicken broth
  • 2 small jalapeño chiles, halved (seeds optional for heat)
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt

For the Casserole

  • 18 (5½-inch) corn tortillas
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 large red bell peppers, chopped
  • 1 large poblano pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 2½ tsp chicken bouillon (granulated)
  • 10 oz can Rotel (diced tomatoes and green chiles), drained
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ⅓ cup sour cream
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped (plus more for garnish)
  • 2½ cups Monterey Jack cheese, shredded (divided)
  • 1½ tsp kosher salt
  • Nonstick cooking spray

Directions

  1. Poach Chicken: In a medium pot over medium heat, toast cumin seeds until fragrant (40 seconds). Add chicken, broth, jalapeños, and salt. Increase heat to medium-high until the broth reaches 150°–160°F. Reduce heat to low and cook until chicken reaches an internal temp of 150°F (30–35 mins). Remove chicken and let rest for 10 mins before shredding with two forks. Strain and reserve 2½ cups of the broth.
  2. Crisp Tortillas: Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Arrange tortillas on two baking sheets, spraying both sides with oil. Bake for 14 minutes, flipping halfway, until slightly crisp. Let cool.
  3. Make Sauce: In a large Dutch oven over medium-high, melt butter. Sauté onion, bell peppers, poblano, and garlic until soft (8 mins). Stir in flour, chili powder, and bouillon; cook for 1 minute. Add drained tomatoes, cream, and the 2½ cups reserved broth. Simmer until thickened (4 mins).
  4. Combine: Remove sauce from heat. Stir in the shredded chicken, cilantro, sour cream, and salt until fully incorporated.
  5. Layer: In a 9x13-inch baking dish, place 6 tortillas on the bottom. Spoon 3 cups of chicken mixture over them, then ½ cup cheese. Repeat for a second layer. Top with the final 6 tortillas, the remaining chicken mixture, and the remaining 1½ cups of cheese.
  6. Bake: Bake on the top third rack at 400°F for 15–20 minutes until bubbling and the cheese is golden brown. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Notes

  • Rotisserie Shortcut: To save time, use a pre-cooked rotisserie chicken. Skip Step 1 and add 2 tsp ground cumin and ¼ tsp cayenne pepper to the sauce in Step 3.
  • The "Puff": Poaching chicken at a lower temperature (150°F) ensures it stays incredibly juicy once it undergoes the second bake in the oven.
  • Tortillas vs. Chips: While some use tortilla chips, toasted corn tortillas provide a much better structure and authentic corn flavor without the excess salt.
