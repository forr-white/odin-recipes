Cheesecake

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

This is the definitive guide to making a bakery-quality New York style cheesecake at home. It is ultra-rich, dense, and creamy, sitting atop a thick, buttery graham cracker crust. While it takes some patience to bake and cool properly, the result is a flawless, crack-free dessert that is sure to impress.

Recommended Tools

  • 9-inch Springform Pan (Essential for easy removal)
  • Stand Mixer or Hand Mixer (To ensure the cream cheese is perfectly smooth)
  • Roasting Pan (Large enough to hold the springform pan for the water bath)
  • Heavy Duty Aluminum Foil (To waterproof the pan)

Ingredients

For the Crust

  • 2 cups (198 grams) graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup (67 grams) sugar
  • 7 tablespoons (99 grams) butter, melted

For the Cheesecake

  • 5 blocks (40 ounces total) full-fat cream cheese, room temperature
  • 1½ cups (300 grams) granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 5 large eggs, room temperature
  • 3 large egg yolks, room temperature
  • ½ cup (113 grams) heavy cream, room temperature

Directions

Prepare the Crust

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly spray a 9-inch springform pan with non-stick spray.
  2. Waterproof the Pan: Wrap the bottom and sides of the pan with heavy-duty aluminum foil. I recommend doing several diligent layers here to ensure no water creeps through when you place the pan in the water bath. Set pan aside.
  3. Mix: In a large bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter; stir well to combine.
  4. Bake Crust: Firmly pat the mixture into the prepared pan. Bake in preheated oven for 8 minutes. Place partially baked crust on a cooling rack and set aside while you prepare the filling.

Prepare the Filling & Bake

  1. Beat Cream Cheese: In the body of a stand mixer fitted with the whisk attachment (or using a hand mixer), beat the softened cream cheese until completely smooth, scraping the bowl as needed. Do not rush this step; lumps here mean lumps later.
  2. Add Sugar: Add sugar and vanilla and beat smooth, scraping down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed.
  3. Add Eggs: Add in the eggs and yolks, one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. Add Cream: Add in the heavy cream and beat until it's just incorporated into the batter. Do not overmix once the eggs are added.
  5. Assemble: Pour filling into prepared crust and, using a silicone spatula, smooth the top.
  6. Water Bath: Place the foil-wrapped cheesecake pan into a large, deep roasting pan. Fill the roasting pan with about 2 inches of hot water. This is your water bath and will help ensure your cheesecake comes out crack-free.
  7. Bake: Carefully place the pan in the oven and bake for 1 hour and 10 minutes.
  8. Cool in Oven: Turn the oven off and let the cheesecake sit, undisturbed, for 45 minutes inside the oven with the door shut. Do not open the door!
  9. Chill: Remove cake from oven. The center should still be slightly wiggly. Gently run a knife around the very edge of the cake to loosen it from the pan. Place the pan on a cooling rack to cool completely, then loosely cover with plastic wrap and chill in the fridge for at least 8 hours.

Notes

  • Room Temperature: It is absolutely critical that your cream cheese, eggs, and cream are at room temperature. If they are cold, the batter will be lumpy.
  • The Water Bath: Don't skip it! The steam keeps the air moist and the heat gentle, preventing the top from drying out and cracking.
  • Don't Peek: Opening the oven door releases heat and causes temperature fluctuations that lead to cracks. Trust the timer.
  • Storage: Cheesecake will keep, covered in the fridge, for 5 days. It can also be frozen for up to 2 months; thaw overnight in the fridge before serving.
