Steak Avocado Corn Bowl

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican Fusion

This bowl is the answer to "healthy but satisfying." It combines the rich, savory flavor of seared flank steak with fresh, colorful vegetables and hearty grains. The contrast between the hot steak, the sweet corn, and the cool, creamy avocado makes every bite interesting. It works perfectly for dinner or high-protein meal prep.

Ingredients

Steak & Marinade

  • 1 lb flank steak (skirt steak or sirloin also work)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and black pepper

The Bowl Build

  • 2 cups cooked quinoa or brown rice
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh grilled, frozen sautéed, or canned)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 ripe avocados, pitted and sliced
  • 1 small red onion, diced

Finish

  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Optional: Hot sauce or salsa

Directions

  1. Marinate: In a small bowl, mix the olive oil, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture thoroughly over the flank steak. Let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes (or refrigerate up to 2 hours).
  2. Prep Corn:
    • Fresh: Grill on the cob for 10 minutes, then cut kernels off.
    • Frozen: Sauté in a hot skillet for 5 minutes until slightly charred.
    • Canned: Drain and rinse.
  3. Cook Steak: Heat a grill or cast iron skillet to medium-high heat. Sear the steak for 4–5 minutes per side.
    Target Temps: 130°F for medium-rare, 145°F for medium.
  4. Rest & Slice (Crucial): Remove steak from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes. Slice against the grain into thin strips. (Slicing with the grain will result in tough, chewy meat).
  5. Assemble: Divide the warm quinoa or rice among bowls. Arrange the sliced steak, corn, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and avocado slices on top.
  6. Season: Drizzle fresh lime juice over the avocado and steak. Garnish generously with cilantro and hot sauce if desired. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Avocado Prep: If you are making this for meal prep, leave the avocado off until you are ready to eat, or toss the avocado slices in lime juice to slow down browning.
  • Customize: This bowl is very flexible. Add black beans for extra fiber, crumbled cotija cheese for saltiness, or a dollop of Greek yogurt for creaminess.
  • The Cut: Flank steak is lean and flavorful but can be tough. If you prefer a more tender cut, sirloin or ribeye works great (though they are higher in fat).
