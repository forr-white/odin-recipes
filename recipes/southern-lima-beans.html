Southern Lima Beans (Butter Beans)

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • Southern American

In the South, large dried lima beans are often called "Butter Beans" because of the rich, velvety texture they develop when cooked slowly. The secret to this recipe isn't the beans themselves, but the "pot likker"—a savory broth created by simmering smoked turkey wings before the beans even hit the pot.

Ingredients

The Beans

  • 1 lb dried large lima beans
  • Water for soaking

The Broth Base

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter, divided
  • 1 small onion, diced (about ½ cup)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 smoked turkey wings (or 1 large smoked turkey leg/ham hock)
  • 6–7 cups water or chicken broth

Seasoning

  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ¼ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste depending on saltiness of meat)
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper

Directions

  1. Soak (The Night Before): Rinse the dried lima beans and pick out any stones or shriveled beans. Place them in a large bowl and cover with water by at least 2 inches. Soak overnight. Drain and rinse before cooking. (See Notes for "Quick Soak" method).
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat, add 1 tablespoon of oil. Sauté the diced onion for 3–5 minutes until softened. Add minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Make the Stock: Add the smoked turkey wings, 6 cups of water (or broth), onion powder, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, salt, and black pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 30–45 minutes. This creates a flavorful smoky broth.
  4. Simmer Beans: Stir in the soaked and drained lima beans. Simmer uncovered for 1½ to 2 hours. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Add more water if the liquid reduces too much; the beans should always be just barely covered.
  5. Finish: The beans are done when they are tender and the broth has thickened to a gravy-like consistency. Remove the turkey wings, pull the meat off the bones, and stir the meat back into the pot.
  6. Enrich: Stir in the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil (or butter) for a glossy finish. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Serve over white rice or with cornbread.

Notes

  • Quick Soak Method: Forgot to soak overnight? Place beans in a pot, cover with water, and bring to a rapid boil for 2 minutes. Turn off heat, cover tightly with a lid, and let sit for 1 hour. Drain and proceed with recipe.
  • Thickening Trick: If your broth is too thin at the end, take a spoon and smash some of the soft beans against the side of the pot. Stir them in to naturally thicken the liquid.
  • Meat Substitutes: Smoked turkey wings offer great flavor with less fat, but smoked ham hocks, neck bones, or even chopped andouille sausage are excellent traditional alternatives.
