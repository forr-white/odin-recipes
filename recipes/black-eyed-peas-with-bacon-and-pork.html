Black-Eyed Peas with Bacon and Pork

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • Southern American

In the South, eating black-eyed peas on New Year's Day is said to bring good luck and prosperity for the year ahead. Whether you are superstitious or not, this recipe is a winner. We simmer the peas slowly with salty bacon, tender pork shoulder, and aromatic vegetables until the broth is rich, creamy, and smoky.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb dried black-eyed peas
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 6 oz pork shoulder, diced into ½ inch pieces
  • 4 strips thick bacon, cut into ½ inch pieces
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 1½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon fresh cracked black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Hot pepper vinegar, as desired (for serving)

Directions

  1. Soak: Put dried peas in a large pot and cover with about 4 inches of water. Soak the peas overnight, then drain the water and rinse well.
  2. Sear the Pork: Heat the oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. When the oil is shimmering, add the diced pork. Sear until the pork is browned on all sides, 4 to 5 minutes.
  3. Add Aromatics: Add the bacon, onion, and garlic to the pot and cook, stirring, until the onion and garlic are lightly browned, about 6 to 8 minutes.
  4. Season and Simmer Broth: Add the salt, black pepper, cayenne, and garlic powder. Cook until the entire mixture is coated with the spices, about 2 minutes. Pour in the stock and water and drop in the bay leaves. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer, covered, for about 30 minutes to tenderize the pork.
  5. Cook the Peas: When the pork begins to fall apart, add the prepared (soaked and rinsed) peas to the pot. Simmer gently until the peas are very soft, about 1 to 1½ hours.
  6. Finish: Taste for seasonings. Discard the bay leaves and transfer the black-eyed peas to a serving bowl. Add a splash of hot pepper vinegar if desired.

Notes

  • For Creamier Peas: Using the back of a spoon, smash some of the peas against the inside of the pot then stir them into the mixture. This will break up some of the peas and thicken the broth. Alternatively, you can puree 1 cup of the peas and broth in a blender, then return it to the pot.
  • Quick Soak Method: If you forgot to soak overnight, place peas in a pot, cover with water, boil for 2 minutes, then remove from heat, cover, and let stand for 1 hour. Drain and proceed.
  • Serving: Traditionally served with collard greens (for money) and cornbread (for gold).
