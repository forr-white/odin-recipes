Cornbread Casserole

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • Southern American

Often called "corn pudding" or "spoon bread," this casserole is a staple on Southern holiday tables. It sits somewhere between a souffle and traditional cornbread—moist, creamy, sweet, and savory all at once. Best of all, it's a "dump and stir" recipe that takes minutes to prep.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 2 (8.5 oz) boxes Jiffy-style cornbread mix
  • 2 (14.75 oz) cans creamed corn (do not drain)
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 stick (½ cup) butter, melted

Optional Add-Ins

  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 small can (4 oz) diced green chiles
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp salt (only if needed—mixes vary in saltiness)

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9×13 inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Mix: In a large bowl, mix together the cornbread mixes, creamed corn, sour cream, eggs, and melted butter until fully combined.
  3. Add-ins: Fold in the optional cheddar cheese or green chiles if using.
  4. Pour: Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top with a spatula.
  5. Bake: Bake for 45–55 minutes. The top should be lightly golden brown, and the center should be set (it shouldn't jiggle too much like liquid). A toothpick inserted in the center should come out clean.
  6. Rest: Let the casserole rest for at least 10 minutes before serving. This allows it to firm up and makes serving easier.

Notes

  • Texture Control: For a firmer, more cake-like casserole, reduce the sour cream to ¾ cup. For a creamier, pudding-like texture, stick to the full cup.
  • Sweet vs. Savory: If you prefer a sweeter side dish, add 2 tablespoons of sugar to the batter.
  • Spicy Kick: For a version with heat, add 1–2 diced fresh jalapeños or ½–1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper.
  • Can Draining: Do not drain the creamed corn; the liquid is essential for the moisture of the dish. However, if you add whole kernel corn as a variation, drain that can.
