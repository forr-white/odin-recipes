Louisiana Style Red Beans and Rice

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • Cajun

In New Orleans, Monday was traditionally laundry day, and red beans were the perfect meal because they could simmer on the stove all day with minimal attention. This version uses the "brining" method to ensure the beans are creamy rather than gritty, combined with the "Holy Trinity" of Cajun cooking (onion, celery, and bell pepper).

Ingredients

The Beans

  • 1 lb (450g) dry red kidney beans
  • 2 tbsp (30g) kosher salt (for brining)
  • 6 cups cold water (for brining)

The Holy Trinity & Meat

  • 1 lb cooked andouille sausage, cut into ½-inch disks
  • 1 smoked ham hock
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 4 ribs celery, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or lard

Seasoning & Aromatics

  • 4 medium cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp ground sage
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Hot sauce (Crystal or Frank's) to taste
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Directions

  1. The Brine: Place beans in a large bowl and cover with 6 cups of cold water. Add the 2 tbsp kosher salt and stir until dissolved. Let soak at room temperature for 8 to 16 hours. Drain and rinse.
  2. Sauté: In a large Dutch oven, heat oil or lard over medium-high heat. Add the andouille and cook until lightly browned (about 5 minutes). Add the onion, bell pepper, and celery. Season lightly with salt and cook until vegetables are softened and starting to brown (about 8 minutes).
  3. Spice: Add the garlic and cook for 45 seconds. Stir in the cayenne, sage, and a generous amount of black pepper. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Simmer: Add the beans, ham hock, thyme, and bay leaves. Add enough water to cover by about 2 inches (6 to 8 cups). Bring to a boil, then reduce to a very low simmer. Cover and cook until beans are completely tender (1.5 to 2.5 hours).
  5. The Creaminess: Remove the lid. Continue to simmer, stirring occasionally, for 20 minutes. As you stir, mash a few beans against the side of the pot with your spoon—this releases starches that turn the liquid creamy.
  6. Finish: Discard bay leaves and thyme stems. Season with hot sauce and a splash of cider vinegar if desired. Serve over hot steamed white rice.

Notes

  • The Overnight Secret: Like most stews, these beans are significantly better the next day. If possible, cook them a day ahead, refrigerate, and reheat them for the best texture.
  • Creaminess: If the beans are tender but the liquid is still thin, keep simmering without the lid. The "cream" is formed by the bean starches emulsifying with the fat from the sausage and ham hock.
  • Salt: Don't skip the brine. Brining the beans in salted water helps the skins soften properly so they don't burst before the insides are creamy.
