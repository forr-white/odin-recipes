Creamy Cajun Chicken Orzo

Submitted by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Fusion

This dish brings the heat of the Bayou to the comfort of Italian pasta. It is a true "one-pan" meal where the orzo cooks directly in the liquid, releasing its starch to create a naturally thick, luxurious sauce without needing a roux. The combination of spicy Cajun seasoning, sweet sun-dried tomatoes, and heavy cream creates a flavor profile that is impossible to stop eating.

Ingredients

  • 3 chicken breasts, cut into small strips
  • 1–3 tbsp Cajun seasoning (start with less if your blend is very salty or spicy)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup (200g) orzo pasta
  • 1 cup (240ml) chicken broth or stock
  • ⅓ cup (60ml) heavy cream
  • 3 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese, plus extra to serve

Directions

  1. Sear Chicken: Combine the chicken strips, Cajun seasoning, and salt in a bowl. Heat the butter and olive oil in a large chef's pan or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Pan-fry the chicken until it starts to color and is mostly cooked through. (It doesn't need to be 100% done as it will simmer later).
  2. Aromatics: Add the onion, garlic, chopped sun-dried tomatoes, and tomato paste to the pan with the chicken. Stir to combine. Cook over low heat for five minutes, stirring frequently, until the onions are soft and the tomato paste has darkened slightly.
  3. Simmer: Stir in the dry orzo and toast it in the pan fats for a minute. Add the chicken broth. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover the pan, and cook for about 20 minutes.
  4. Monitor: Orzo absorbs liquid readily and can stick to the bottom. Stir frequently during the 20 minutes. If the pan gets too dry before the pasta is cooked, add a splash of extra water or broth.
  5. Check Doneness: Uncover the pan and test the orzo—it should be tender but still have a slight "al dente" bite.
  6. Finish: Stir in the heavy cream and the grated Parmesan. Simmer for 1 more minute until the sauce is thick, creamy, and indulgent. Check the seasoning (add more salt or Cajun spice if needed) and serve hot with extra Parmesan cheese.

Notes

  • Veggie Boost: To make this a complete meal, stir in a handful of fresh baby spinach or diced bell peppers during the last 2 minutes of cooking.
  • Lighter Version: You can substitute half-and-half for the heavy cream, though the sauce will be slightly thinner.
  • Orzo Watch: Orzo is technically pasta, not rice. It can go from "perfect" to "mushy" quickly, so keep testing it toward the end of the cooking time.
