"Party Batch" Spinach Artichoke Queso

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

appetizers • American • Party Food

This recipe was designed to solve a specific problem: How do you feed 80 people a high-quality warm dip without spending all day in the kitchen? This hybrid recipe combines the stability of food-service Queso Blanco with the classic flavors of Spinach Artichoke dip. It yields over a gallon of dip that stays creamy for hours.

Recommended Tools

  • 6-Quart Slow Cooker (Do not use anything smaller than 6 quarts for this volume)
  • Clean Kitchen Towel (For squeezing spinach)
  • Sturdy Whisk

Ingredients

The Dairy Base

  • 1 gallon Queso Blanco Dip (Food service #10 can or 4 large 32oz tubs)
  • 2 blocks (8 oz each) cream cheese, cubed
  • 1 bag (1 lb) shredded Monterey Jack (or Mozzarella)

The Mix-Ins

  • 2 large jars (33 oz each) marinated artichoke hearts
  • 1 bag (10–12 oz) frozen chopped spinach, thawed
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

For Serving

  • 6–7 lbs tortilla chips (Choose "Restaurant Style" or sturdy chips; thin chips will break)

Directions

  1. Prep the Spinach (The Most Important Step): Thaw the frozen spinach completely. Place it in a clean kitchen towel and squeeze it bone dry. If you skip this, your dip will be green and watery. Once dry, fluff it with a fork.
  2. Prep the Artichokes: Drain the jars of artichokes, but reserve ½ cup of the marinade oil. Chop the artichoke hearts into small, bite-sized pieces.
  3. Combine: In a 6-quart (or larger) slow cooker, combine the gallon of Queso Blanco, the cubed cream cheese, the shredded Monterey Jack, the dried spinach, the chopped artichokes, the reserved marinade oil, and the garlic powder.
  4. Cook: Cover and cook on HIGH for 1 hour.
  5. Stir: Remove lid and stir. It will look lumpy and separated at this point—that is normal. Cover and cook on HIGH for another 30–60 minutes.
  6. Finish: Whisk or stir vigorously to smooth out the cream cheese and combine the fats. The texture should be creamy and thick.
  7. Serve: Turn the slow cooker to WARM. Serve with plenty of chips.

Notes

  • Party Maintenance: Stir the dip every 30–45 minutes during the party to prevent a skin from forming. If it gets too thick near the end of the night, stir in a splash of milk or water to loosen it up.
  • The Secret Ingredient: Don't throw away all the artichoke marinade! That ½ cup of reserved oil adds a tangy, herbal flavor that cuts through the heavy cheese.
  • Yield: This makes approximately 1.25 gallons of dip. This is enough for 70–80 guests as an appetizer.
