Crockpot Marinara Chicken Sandwich

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian-American

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 2 lbs chicken breast
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1–2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • ¼–½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1–2 tbsp jarred minced garlic
  • 24 oz marinara sauce

For Serving

  • Sub rolls
  • 4–8 slices provolone cheese
  • Shredded Parmesan
  • Fresh basil (optional)

Garlic Toasted Rolls

  • 3 tbsp softened butter
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½–1 tsp garlic powder

Instructions

  1. Season chicken breasts with salt, pepper, onion powder, Italian seasoning, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Place chicken in the slow cooker and spoon minced garlic over the top.
  3. Pour marinara sauce over the chicken.
  4. Cook on low for 6–7 hours or high for 3–4 hours, until chicken easily shreds.
  5. Shred chicken with two forks, return to crockpot, and let it absorb flavor for 10–15 minutes.
  6. Make garlic-butter rolls by mixing softened butter, salt, and garlic powder. Spread on rolls, then toast until golden.
  7. Assemble sandwiches with shredded marinara chicken, provolone slices, and shredded Parmesan. Broil 1–2 minutes if desired.

Notes

  • Add sautéed onions or bell peppers for extra flavor and texture.
  • For a chicken-parm twist, add extra Parmesan and broil with a sprinkle of Italian breadcrumbs.
  • If the marinara is thin, stir in 1–2 tbsp tomato paste near the end.
  • Great for meal prep—reheats perfectly and stays juicy.
