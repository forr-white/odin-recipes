Crockpot Marinara Chicken Sandwich

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian-American

Think of this as a deconstructed Chicken Parmesan sandwich made incredibly easy. Instead of breading and frying, chicken breasts are slow-cooked in seasoned marinara sauce until they fall apart. Served on homemade garlic-buttered toasted rolls with plenty of melty provolone, it’s a crowd-pleasing dinner with barely any prep work.

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 2 lbs chicken breast
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1–2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • ¼–½ tsp red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 1–2 tbsp jarred minced garlic
  • 24 oz jar marinara sauce

Garlic Toasted Rolls

  • Sub rolls or hoagie buns
  • 3 tbsp salted butter, softened
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½–1 tsp garlic powder

Assembly

  • 4–8 slices provolone cheese
  • Shredded Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil (optional)

Directions

  1. Season Chicken: Season the chicken breasts on both sides with salt, pepper, onion powder, Italian seasoning, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Load Crockpot: Place the seasoned chicken in the slow cooker. Spoon the minced garlic over the top. Pour the entire jar of marinara sauce over the chicken.
  3. Cook: Cover and cook on LOW for 6–7 hours or HIGH for 3–4 hours, until the chicken is tender enough to shred easily.
  4. Shred: Shred the chicken directly in the pot using two forks. Stir well to coat the meat in the sauce. Let it sit on warm for 10–15 minutes to absorb the flavor.
  5. Prep Buns: While the chicken rests, mix the softened butter, salt, and garlic powder in a small bowl. Spread this garlic butter on the cut sides of the sub rolls.
  6. Toast: Place the rolls butter-side up on a baking sheet. Broil or bake at 400°F (200°C) until golden brown and toasted.
  7. Assemble: Pile the shredded marinara chicken onto the toasted buns. Top generously with provolone slices and shredded Parmesan.
  8. Melt: Place the open-faced sandwiches back under the broiler for 1–2 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and melted. Top with fresh basil if desired.

Notes

  • Add Veggies: Sautéed onions and green bell peppers make a fantastic addition to the sandwich topping.
  • Chicken Parm Style: For extra crunch, sprinkle toasted Italian breadcrumbs over the melted cheese right before serving.
  • Sauce Texture: If the sauce seems too thin after shredding, stir in 1–2 tablespoons of tomato paste to thicken it up.
  • Meal Prep: The shredded chicken keeps perfectly in the fridge for 4 days and actually tastes better the next day.
