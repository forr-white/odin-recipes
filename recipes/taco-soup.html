Cheesy Tex-Mex Taco Soup

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Tex-Mex

This isn't your standard watery vegetable soup. This is full-blown comfort food—essentially a cross between a hearty chili and a pot of liquid gold queso. Loaded with ground beef, beans, and the creamy richness of Velveeta and cream cheese, this soup is perfect for dipping tortilla chips or warming up on a cold night.

Ingredients

Soup Base

  • 1 lb ground beef (85/15 or 90/10)
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz) chili beans, undrained
  • 1 can (15 oz) sweet corn, drained
  • 1 can (10 oz) mild Rotel (diced tomatoes & green chilies), undrained
  • 1 can (4 oz) mild diced green chiles
  • 6 oz tomato paste
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (approx. 1 oz)
  • 3 cups beef broth

The Cheesy Finish

  • 16 oz Velveeta cheese, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened and cubed

Toppings (The Fun Part)

  • Tortilla strips or crushed chips
  • Lime wedges
  • Avocado slices
  • Queso fresco or Cotija cheese, crumbled
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Directions

  1. Brown Beef: In a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat, brown the ground beef, breaking it into small crumbles as it cooks. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Add the chopped onion to the beef and cook for 3–4 minutes, until the onion is softened and fragrant.
  3. Build the Base: Stir in the chili beans (with liquid), drained corn, tomato paste, Rotel, green chiles, taco seasoning, and beef broth. Mix thoroughly to dissolve the tomato paste.
  4. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, to let the flavors meld.
  5. The Melt: Reduce heat to LOW. (Crucial: Do not let the soup boil vigorously once cheese is added). Add the cubed Velveeta and cream cheese.
  6. Finish: Stir frequently for 10–15 minutes until all the cheese is fully melted and the soup is velvety smooth.
  7. Serve: Taste and adjust with salt or pepper if needed. Ladle into bowls and top generously with tortilla strips, avocado, and fresh lime juice.

Notes

  • Spice Level: This recipe is mild and family-friendly. To kick it up, use "Hot" Rotel, add a diced jalapeño with the onions, or stir in cayenne pepper with the taco seasoning.
  • Thickness: If you prefer a stew-like consistency, you can stir in a handful of crushed tortilla chips during the simmer step; the corn masa will naturally thicken the broth.
  • Storage: Because of the dairy, reheat this soup gently on the stove (low heat) rather than boiling it in the microwave, to prevent the cheese from separating.
