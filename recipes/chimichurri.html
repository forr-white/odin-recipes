Chimichurri

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Argentinian

Chimichurri is the table condiment of choice in Argentina and Uruguay, and for good reason. It is a bright, herbaceous, uncooked sauce that cuts through the richness of grilled meats perfectly. Packed with parsley, garlic, oregano, and vinegar, it adds an instant burst of fresh flavor to steak, chicken, fish, or toasted bread.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup chopped parsley (flat-leaf Italian parsley)
  • 4 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 shallot, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 small red chili, seeded and diced (or 2 tsp red pepper flakes)
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
  • ⅔ cup good-quality extra-virgin olive oil

Directions

  1. Pulse Aromatics: Add the parsley, garlic, shallot, oregano, chili (or pepper flakes), vinegar, salt, and pepper to the bowl of a food processor.
  2. Mince: Pulse until finely minced. Be extra careful not to over-process, as this can turn the mixture into a mushy paste instead of nicely minced herbs. Remove the lid and scrape down the sides as needed.
  3. Add Oil: Transfer the mixture to a medium bowl (or a jar) and stir in the extra-virgin olive oil, mixing gently to combine. (Note: Do not blend the oil in the food processor, or it may turn bitter and emulsified).
  4. Rest: Let the chimichurri rest for about 15 minutes at room temperature to allow the flavors to meld. Taste and season with additional salt if needed.
  5. Serve: Serve over your favorite grilled meats and vegetables, or use as a marinade!

Notes

  • Hand Chopped vs. Processor: While a food processor is faster, hand-chopping the herbs and garlic yields a more traditional, rustic texture that sits beautifully on top of steak.
  • Don't Blend the Oil: Adding the oil by hand is crucial. If you blend olive oil at high speed, the polyphenols can oxidize and create a bitter taste.
  • Storage: Chimichurri keeps well in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks, but the oil will solidify. Let it sit at room temperature before serving to liquify again.
  • Variations: Feel free to add fresh cilantro or fresh oregano for a different herbaceous profile.
