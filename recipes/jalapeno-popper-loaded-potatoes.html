Jalapeno Popper Loaded Potatoes

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • American

This recipe combines two of the best comfort foods: the classic loaded baked potato and the spicy jalapeno popper. By twice-baking the potatoes, we ensure the skins are salty and crisp while the filling becomes a rich, whipped mixture of cream cheese and cheddar. It's a decadent side dish that often steals the show as the main event.

Ingredients

  • 4 large Russet baking potatoes
  • 9 oz bacon, cooked and chopped
  • 6 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 6 oz sharp cheddar cheese, grated
  • 3 fresh jalapenos, deseeded and finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for coating)
  • Sour cream (for serving)
  • Fresh chives, chopped (for garnish)

Directions

  1. The Initial Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Scrub the potatoes and prick them a few times with a fork. Rub with olive oil and a generous pinch of salt and pepper. Bake directly on the oven rack (or a baking sheet) for 60–70 minutes until tender.
  2. Prep the Shells: Once the potatoes are cool enough to handle, slice off the top third of each potato. Using a spoon, scoop out most of the center into a large bowl, leaving about ¼-inch of potato "wall" to keep the shell sturdy.
  3. Mash the Filling: In the bowl with the potato centers, add the softened cream cheese, garlic powder, butter, salt, pepper, and most of the cheddar, bacon, and diced jalapenos. Mash and stir until well combined and creamy.
  4. The Second Bake: Spoon the filling back into the potato shells, piling it high. Top with the remaining cheddar, bacon, and jalapenos.
  5. Crisp It Up: Increase the oven temperature to 430°F (220°C). Place the stuffed potatoes on a baking sheet and bake for 12–15 minutes until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and slightly browned.
  6. Garnish: Serve hot with a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkle of fresh chives.

Notes

  • Softened Cream Cheese: Make sure your cream cheese is at room temperature before mixing, or you'll end up with lumps in your potato mash.
  • Heat Level: For a spicier potato, keep the seeds in one of the jalapenos when dicing. For a milder version, ensure all white pith and seeds are removed.
  • Make Ahead: You can bake the potatoes and fill them up to a day in advance. Store them covered in the fridge, then do the final 430°F bake right before serving.
