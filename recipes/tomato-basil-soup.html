Roasted Tomato Basil Soup

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • Italian

The difference between good tomato soup and great tomato soup is roasting. By charring the tomatoes and garlic in the oven first, we concentrate their sweetness and add a smoky depth. This recipe also features a brilliant secret: instead of heavy cream, a single potato is simmered and blended into the broth, creating a velvety texture that keeps the soup naturally dairy-free and vegan.

Ingredients

The Roast

  • 2½ lbs Roma tomatoes, halved lengthwise
  • 1¼ lbs grape or cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 8 cloves garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper

The Soup Base

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and diced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced (the secret thickener!)
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 cups vegetable broth

The Finish

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves, loosely packed
  • Optional: Shaved Parmesan cheese or croutons

Directions

  1. Roast: Preheat oven to 430°F (220°C). Place the Roma and grape tomatoes on a large baking sheet with the smashed garlic cloves. Drizzle with 2 tablespoons of oil and season generously with salt and pepper. Roast for 25–30 minutes, or until soft and charred on top.
  2. Sauté Base: While tomatoes roast, heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large soup pot over medium-high heat. Add the onion, red bell pepper, and diced potato. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion is transparent and the potato begins to crisp on the edges (about 6–7 minutes).
  3. Simmer: Stir the tomato paste into the vegetable mixture. Pour in the vegetable broth and season with a little more salt. Cover and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the potato is tender when pierced with a fork.
  4. Combine: Add the roasted tomatoes, roasted garlic, and all the pan juices into the pot. Stir in the fresh basil leaves (save a few for garnish). Simmer for just 2 minutes until basil wilts.
  5. Blend: Remove from heat. Use an immersion blender (stick blender) to puree the soup until smooth. (Alternatively, transfer to a standard blender in batches, venting the lid to release steam).
  6. Refine (Optional): If you want a perfectly silky restaurant-style texture, pour the soup through a mesh strainer to remove tomato skins. If you prefer a rustic texture, skip this step.
  7. Serve: Ladle into bowls. Garnish with fresh basil and shaved Parmesan. Serve immediately with crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich.

Notes

  • Why Potato? The starch from the potato emulsifies the soup when blended, giving it a creamy mouthfeel without needing dairy. It makes the soup lighter but just as satisfying.
  • Tomato Types: Roma tomatoes are best for roasting because they have lower water content and more flesh. Mixing them with cherry tomatoes adds sweetness.
  • Storage: This soup freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight before reheating.
