Sourdough Starter

A simple, reliable recipe to begin your sourdough journey.

Home

Ingredients

  • 60g (½ cup) whole wheat or rye flour (Day 1)
  • 60g (¼ cup) water (room temperature)
  • Unbleached all-purpose flour (for feedings)
  • Additional water as needed

Instructions

  1. Day 1: Mix 60g flour with 60g water in a clean jar. Stir until no dry flour remains. Cover loosely and let sit at room temperature (70–75°F is ideal) for 24 hours.
  2. Day 2: Check for bubbles. Discard half the mixture (about ½ cup). Add 40g wheat flour + 20g all-purpose flour + 60g water. Stir, cover, and rest another 24 hours.
  3. Days 3–5: Repeat the process daily: discard half, feed with 60g flour + 60g water. You should start to notice more bubbles and a tangy aroma.
  4. Day 6–7: The starter should be doubling in size within 4–6 hours of feeding. It should be airy, bubbly, and smell pleasantly sour. At this point, it’s ready to bake with.
Note: If your kitchen is cold, it may take longer for your starter to mature. Be patient and continue daily feedings until it’s consistently active.
