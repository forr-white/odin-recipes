Pinto Beans for Refried Beans

sides • Mexican

Great refried beans don't come from a can; they start with a flavorful pot of "Frijoles de la Olla" (pot beans). By slow-cooking the beans with bacon and salt pork, we infuse them with savory fat from the very beginning. This recipe gives you the tender base beans and the instructions to turn them into the creamy, authentic side dish found in Mexican restaurants.

Ingredients

The Pot Beans (Base)

  • 2½ cups dried pinto beans
  • 2 strips thick-cut bacon, diced
  • 4 oz salt pork, sliced (approx. ½ block)
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 tsp SPG seasoning (Salt, Pepper, Garlic blend)
  • 6–7 cups water (enough to cover beans by 2 inches)

For Refrying

  • 2 tbsp lard (manteca) or bacon grease
  • Bean broth (liquid from the pot beans)

Directions

  1. Prep Beans: Rinse the dried beans in a colander. Sort through them to remove any small stones or shriveled beans.
  2. Slow Cook: Place the rinsed beans in a slow cooker. Add the diced bacon, salt pork, crushed garlic, SPG seasoning, and water.
  3. Simmer: Turn the slow cooker to High for 5–6 hours (or Low for 8–10 hours). The beans are done when they are completely tender and creamy inside. Note: Check halfway through and add more hot water if the liquid level gets too low.
  4. The "Refry": To make refried beans, heat the lard (or bacon grease) in a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Mash & Fry: Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked beans into the hot fat. Sauté for 2 minutes. Add about ½ cup of the bean broth (liquid from the slow cooker). Use a potato masher to smash the beans into the liquid until you reach your desired consistency.
  6. Finish: If the beans are too dry, add more broth. If they are too runny, cook for a few more minutes to evaporate the liquid. Serve hot.

Notes

  • SPG Substitute: If you don't have an SPG blend, use 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp black pepper, and ½ tsp garlic powder.
  • Salt Warning: Salt pork and bacon are salty. Taste the beans before adding extra salt at the end.
  • Lard vs. Oil: Authentic refried beans use lard (manteca). Vegetable oil works, but you will lose that signature rich, savory flavor.
