Fruit Roll Ups

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

snacks • American

Store-bought fruit snacks are often loaded with corn syrup and artificial dyes. These homemade Fruit Roll Ups (or fruit leather) are made with just three simple ingredients: fresh fruit, a touch of sweetener, and lemon juice. They are chewy, sweet, and a perfect healthy snack for kids and adults alike.

Recommended Tools

  • High-Speed Blender (Essential for getting a silky smooth puree)
  • Baking Sheets (You will likely need two for a full batch)
  • Parchment Paper or Silicone Mats (Do not use wax paper—it will stick!)
  • Offset Spatula (Helps spread the mixture perfectly thin)

Ingredients

General Base (Create Your Own Flavor)

  • 3 cups diced fresh fruit (strawberries, peaches, mangoes, berries, etc.)
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice (preserves color and adds brightness)
  • ¼ cup honey (substitute brown rice syrup or agave if vegan)

Flavor Variation: Mango-Peach

  • 1½ cups diced peaches
  • 1½ cups diced mango
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup honey

Flavor Variation: Strawberry-Blackberry Swirl

  • Red Mix: 1½ cups diced strawberries + 1 tsp lemon juice + 2 tbsp honey
  • Purple Mix: 1½ cups blackberries + 1 tsp lemon juice + 2 tbsp honey

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat your oven to its lowest setting (usually 150°F to 170°F). If your oven only goes down to 200°F, prop the door open slightly with a wooden spoon. Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats.
  2. Blend: In a blender, puree your chosen fruit, lemon juice, and sweetener until completely smooth. Blend for at least 2 minutes to ensure no chunks remain. (For the "Swirl" version, blend the two fruit mixtures separately in two batches).
  3. Spread: Pour the puree onto the prepared baking sheets. Use a spatula to spread the mixture into a thin, even layer about ⅛-inch thick.
  4. Tap: Tap the baking sheet firmly against the counter a few times to release air bubbles and even out the surface. This is crucial for even drying.
  5. Bake (Dehydrate): Place in the oven and bake for 6–8 hours. The time will vary based on your oven temperature and humidity. The fruit leather is done when the center is no longer tacky to the touch and the surface is smooth and shiny.
  6. Cool & Roll: Remove from the oven and let cool completely on the pan. Once cool, peel the leather off the parchment (or keep it on the paper), cut into strips using scissors or a pizza cutter, and roll them up.

Notes

  • Don't Rush It: This is a low-and-slow process. If you increase the heat to speed it up, the edges will burn while the center stays wet.
  • Paper Choice: Do not use wax paper or aluminum foil. The fruit acid will react with foil, and wax paper will melt or stick permanently. Use high-quality parchment or a silicone mat.
  • Texture: If the edges become brittle, just trim them off. The goal is a pliable, chewy texture.
