Fruit Roll Ups

snacks • American

Ingredients

Mango-Peach Fruit Roll Ups

  • 1 ½ cup diced peaches
  • 1 ½ cup diced mango
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup honey, mays sub brown rice syrup or honey if vegan

Strawberry-Blackberry Swirl Fruit Roll Ups

  • 1 ½ cup diced strawberries
  • 1 ½ cup blackberries
  • 2 tsp fresh lemon juice, divided
  • 4 tbsp honey, divided, may sub brown rice syrup or honey if vegan

General Base To Make Your Own Flavor

  • 3 cup diced fresh fruit
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup honey, may sub brown rice syrup for honey if vegan

Directions

Mango-Peach

  1. Preheat the oven to 150 degrees F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a blender, puree all the ingredients until completely smooth and no chunks remain, about 2 minutes. Divide the mixture in 2 and pour onto the prepared baking sheets. Spread the mixture to an 1/8″ thick even layer. It is REALLY important to make sure the mix is all in one even layer. To help with this, I found topping the pan against the count a few times really helpful. Place in oven and bake for 6-8 hours, until center is not tacky anymore. Remove from oven and allow to cool before cutting.
  3. Cut into strips and roll um up! Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks or freeze.

Strawberry-Blackberry Swirl

  1. Preheat the oven to 150 degrees F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a blender, puree the strawberries, 1 tsp lemon juice, and 2 tbs honey until completely smooth and no chunks remain, about 2 minutes. Pour onto one of the prepared baking sheets. Spread the mixture to an 1/8″ thick even layer. It is REALLY important to make sure the mix is all in one even layer. To help with this, I found topping the pan against the count a few times really helpful. Place in oven and bake for 6-8 hours, until center is not tacky anymore. Remove from oven and allow to cool before cutting.
  3. In a clean blender, puree the blackberries, remaining lemon juice, and remaining honey until completely smooth and no chunks remain, about 2 minutes. Pour onto the second prepared baking sheet. Spread the mixture to an 1/8″ thick even layer. It is REALLY important to make sure the mix is all in one even layer. To help with this, I found topping the pan against the count a few times really helpful. Place in oven and bake for 6-8 hours, until center is not tacky anymore. Remove from oven and allow to cool before cutting.
  4. Once both fruit roll ups are cool, cut into strips and roll um up! Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks or freeze.
