Traditional Pork Tamales

dinner • Mexican

Ingredients

For the pork

  • 3-1/2 lbs pork shoulder
  • 10 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 cups Red Chili Sauce

For the masa & assembly

  • 3/4 cup shortening
  • 6 cups masa harina
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • Reserved pork cooking broth (about 6 cups)
  • 50 dried corn husks (about 8 inches long)

Directions

  1. In a 5-quart Dutch oven, bring the pork, water, onion, garlic, and 1-1/2 teaspoons salt to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and simmer, covered, for about 2-1/2 hours, or until the meat is very tender.
  3. Remove the meat from the broth and allow both meat and broth to cool slightly.
  4. Shred the meat using 2 forks, discarding excess fat.
  5. Strain the broth and reserve 6 cups.
  6. In a large saucepan, heat the red chili sauce, add the shredded meat, and simmer, covered, for 10 minutes. Remove from heat.
  7. To make the masa, beat the shortening on medium speed in a large bowl for 1 minute until fluffy.
  8. In a separate bowl, stir together the masa harina, baking powder, and 2 teaspoons salt.
  9. Alternately add the masa harina mixture and reserved broth to the shortening, beating well after each addition, until the dough is soft and spreadable.
  10. Meanwhile, soak the corn husks in warm water for at least 20 minutes. Rinse to remove any corn silk and drain well.
  11. To assemble each tamale, spread about 2 tablespoons of masa in the center of a corn husk, leaving space at the top and bottom.
  12. Place about 1 tablespoon of the pork and sauce mixture down the center of the masa.
  13. Fold in the sides of the husk, then fold up the bottom to enclose the filling.
  14. Place a mound of extra husks or a foil ball in the center of a steamer basket set inside a Dutch oven.
  15. Lean the tamales in the basket, open side up, arranging them snugly.
  16. Add water to the Dutch oven to just below the basket.
  17. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to maintain a gentle simmer.
  18. Cover and steam for about 40 minutes, adding more water as needed, until the masa is firm and pulls away easily from the husk.

Nutrition

Yield: about 50 tamales

Serving Size: 1 tamale

Calories: 290

Fat: 15 g

Carbs: 24 g

Protein: 14 g
