Blueberry Muffins with Streusel Crumb Topping

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

These aren't your average boxed-mix muffins. We use yogurt to ensure a moist, tender crumb, and we load them with extra blueberries for bursts of fruit in every bite. The real star, however, is the buttery cinnamon streusel topping that adds a satisfying crunch to the fluffy muffin top.

  • Standard Muffin Pan (12-cup capacity)
  • Paper Liners (makes cleanup instant)
  • Mixing Bowls (one for dry, one for wet, one for streusel)
  • Wire Cooling Rack (essential for preventing soggy bottoms)

Ingredients

For the Muffins

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • ½ cup canola oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2⅓ cups blueberries, fresh or frozen (divided use)

For the Streusel Topping

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ⅔ cup sugar
  • 1 stick salted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a standard muffin pan with paper liners and set aside.
  2. Make the Streusel: In a small bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and cinnamon. Add the melted butter and stir with a fork until the mixture is crumbly. Set aside.
  3. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, stir together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  4. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs and sugar until combined. Whisk in the yogurt, oil, and vanilla extract.
  5. Combine: Fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix everything together by hand just until combined. Do not overmix.
  6. Prepare Blueberries: In a small bowl, sprinkle 1 cup of the blueberries with 1–2 tablespoons of flour and toss them until they are coated with a thin layer. Gently fold these coated berries into the batter.
  7. Fill: Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tins, filling about ⅔ of each cup.
  8. Top: Distribute the remaining blueberries on top of the batter in the cups, then cover the blueberries generously with the streusel topping.
  9. Bake: Place the pan in the oven and immediately reduce the heat to 375°F (190°C). Bake for 18–20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  10. Cool: Let cool for 5 minutes in the pan, then remove the muffins and transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Notes

  • Why coat the berries? Tossing the blueberries in flour before adding them to the batter prevents them from sinking to the bottom of the muffin while baking.
  • The Temperature Trick: Starting at 400°F and then dropping to 375°F helps create a rapid rise, giving you those beautiful, bakery-style domed tops.
  • Don't Overmix: When combining wet and dry ingredients, stop as soon as the flour streaks disappear. Overmixing leads to tough, chewy muffins.
  • Storage: Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
