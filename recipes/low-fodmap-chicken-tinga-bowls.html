Low FODMAP Chicken Tinga Bowls

mains • Mexican • lactose-free • kosher • low FODMAP

Submitted by Forrest White

Overview

Classic smoky Mexican tinga flavor, reworked to be Low FODMAP, lactose-free, and kosher-friendly. Chicken is simmered in a tomato-chipotle sauce made with garlic-infused oil and scallion tops, then served over rice with crunchy veggies and avocado for a satisfying lunch bowl.

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 1½ lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 1 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil (FODMAP-safe, no garlic pieces)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

For the Tinga-Style Sauce

  • 1 cup canned diced tomatoes (no onion or garlic added)
  • ½ cup tomato sauce (no onion or garlic)
  • 1–2 tsp chipotle paste OR ½ canned chipotle pepper, seeds removed (to tolerance)
  • ½ cup Low FODMAP chicken broth (onion-free)
  • 2 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil
  • 2 tbsp sliced scallion tops only (green parts; discard white bulbs)
  • 1 tsp fresh lime juice
  • Salt, to taste

For the Bowls

  • 2 cups cooked white rice or quinoa
  • 1 cup shredded green cabbage or lettuce
  • 1 medium tomato, diced
  • 1 avocado, sliced (about ¼ avocado per serving for Low FODMAP)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Season the chicken: In a bowl, combine chicken with garlic-infused olive oil, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Toss until evenly coated.
  2. Cook the chicken: Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the seasoned chicken and cook, turning occasionally, until cooked through and browned in spots (about 8–10 minutes depending on thickness). Transfer to a plate and let rest for 5 minutes, then slice or shred.
  3. Start the sauce: In the same skillet, add the garlic-infused olive oil for the sauce. Warm over medium heat, then add diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, chipotle paste or pepper, and chicken broth.
  4. Simmer: Stir in the scallion tops and lime juice. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened. Season with salt to taste.
  5. Add the chicken: Return the sliced or shredded chicken to the skillet. Toss in the sauce until well coated and heated through. Reduce heat to low to keep warm.
  6. Prepare the bowl components: While the sauce simmers, cook the rice or quinoa if you haven’t already. Shred the cabbage or lettuce, dice the tomato, slice the avocado, and chop the cilantro.
  7. Assemble the bowls: Divide the rice or quinoa among 4 bowls. Top each with a generous portion of chicken tinga, then add cabbage or lettuce, diced tomato, and avocado slices.
  8. Finish & serve: Garnish each bowl with fresh cilantro and a lime wedge. Serve immediately, letting everyone squeeze extra lime over their bowls to taste.

Notes

  • Keep it Low FODMAP by using only the green tops of scallions and a verified onion-free chicken broth.
  • Adjust the chipotle amount based on your spice tolerance; start with less and add more as needed.
  • For meal prep, store rice and chicken separately from the fresh toppings and assemble bowls just before serving.
  • To keep the dish kosher and lactose-free, avoid cheese or sour cream toppings, or use certified pareve, lactose-free cheese alternatives if desired.
