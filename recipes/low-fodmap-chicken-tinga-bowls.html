Low FODMAP Chicken Tinga Bowls

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

mains • Mexican • lactose-free • low FODMAP

Chicken Tinga is traditionally laden with onions and garlic—two of the highest FODMAP ingredients. This version captures the signature smokiness of the chipotle and the richness of the tomato sauce using **garlic-infused olive oil** and the **green tops of scallions**. It’s a bright, satisfying meal that is as friendly to your gut as it is to your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 1½ lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 1 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil (ensure it is strained/no pieces)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

For the Tinga-Style Sauce

  • 1 cup canned diced tomatoes (no onion or garlic added)
  • ½ cup tomato sauce (plain/no onion or garlic)
  • 1–2 tsp chipotle paste (or ½ canned chipotle pepper, seeds removed)
  • ½ cup Low FODMAP chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp garlic-infused olive oil
  • 2 tbsp scallion tops only (green parts only)

For the Bowls

  • 2 cups cooked white rice or quinoa
  • 1 cup shredded green cabbage or lettuce
  • 1 medium tomato, diced
  • 1 avocado (limit to ⅛ or ¼ per serving for Low FODMAP)
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges

Directions

  1. Season: In a medium bowl, toss the chicken with garlic-infused oil, paprika, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  2. Cook Chicken: Heat a skillet over medium heat. Cook chicken for 8–10 minutes, turning once, until the internal temperature reaches 165°F. Transfer to a plate, let rest for 5 minutes, then shred or slice into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Build Sauce: In the same skillet, combine the sauce ingredients: diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, chipotle, and broth.
  4. Simmer: Stir in the scallion greens and lime juice. Simmer for 8–10 minutes until the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded.
  5. Combine: Fold the shredded chicken back into the skillet. Toss until the meat is fully coated in the smoky sauce and heated through.
  6. Assemble: Divide rice among 4 bowls. Top with the chicken tinga mixture, cabbage, tomatoes, and a small portion of avocado. Garnish with cilantro and a heavy squeeze of lime.

Notes

  • FODMAP Tip: The white part of the scallion (the bulb) contains high amounts of fructans. Always discard the whites and use only the dark green stalks for this diet.
  • Broth: Most commercial chicken broths contain onion and garlic powder. Look for brands specifically labeled "Low FODMAP" or "Onion/Garlic Free."
  • Avocado: While avocado is Low FODMAP, it is "dose-dependent." Stick to roughly 30g (about ⅛ to ¼ of a fruit) per serving to avoid symptoms.
