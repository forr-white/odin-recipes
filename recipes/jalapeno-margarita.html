Jalapeno Margarita

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • Mexican

This isn't your average syrupy margarita. By using fresh-squeezed lime and orange juice with a touch of agave, we create a clean, crisp base that allows the heat of the jalapeno to shine. Shaking the pepper slices directly with the ice provides a controlled, bright spice without the bitterness of muddling the seeds.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz silver (blanco) tequila
  • 3 tbsp (1.5 oz) fresh-squeezed lime juice
  • 1 tbsp (0.5 oz) fresh-squeezed orange juice
  • 1.5–2 tsp light agave nectar (adjust to sweetness preference)
  • ¼–½ fresh jalapeno pepper, cut into thin round slices
  • Kosher salt or Tajín (for the rim)
  • Ice cubes
  • Extra lime wedges and jalapeno slices for garnish

Directions

  1. Rim the Glass: Pour a thin layer of salt onto a small plate. Rub a lime wedge around the top edge of your glass to moisten it. Dip the glass into the salt at an angle, turning it to coat the rim evenly. Fill the glass with ice and set aside.
  2. Combine: Fill a cocktail shaker with fresh ice. Add the tequila, lime juice, orange juice, agave nectar, and the jalapeno slices.
  3. Shake: Tightly close the shaker and shake vigorously for at least 30 seconds. This chills the drink and "bruises" the jalapenos to release their oils and heat.
  4. Strain & Serve: Strain the mixture into your prepared, salt-rimmed glass. Garnish immediately with fresh lime wedges and a few additional jalapeno slices. Serve cold.

Notes

  • Control the Heat: If you prefer a milder drink, remove the white pith and seeds from the jalapeno slices before adding them to the shaker. For extra spice, let the jalapenos sit in the tequila for 5 minutes before shaking.
  • Orange Juice: A splash of fresh orange juice acts as a natural sweetener and replaces the need for calorie-heavy triple sec or Cointreau.
  • Rim Variety: For a smoky and spicy twist, try using Tajín or a mix of salt and smoked paprika for the rim.
