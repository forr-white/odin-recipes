Chicken Enchilada Casserole

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican

Rolling individual enchiladas can be time-consuming and messy. This casserole solves that problem by stacking the ingredients lasagna-style. By poaching the chicken directly in the enchilada sauce, the meat stays incredibly moist and flavorful. It's the perfect lazy weeknight dinner that still delivers big Mexican flavors.

  • Small Baking Dish (Approx 7x11 or 8x8 inches fits 6 tortillas perfectly)
  • Medium Saucepan (For poaching the chicken)
  • Cheese Grater (Freshly grated cheese melts best!)

Ingredients

  • 1 (14 oz) can Enchilada Sauce (red or green)
  • 3 cups shredded Monterey Jack Cheese
  • 6 corn tortillas
  • 2 chicken breasts

Directions

  1. Poach Chicken: Cut each chicken breast into about 3 pieces to speed up cooking. Place them in a small pot and pour the enchilada sauce over them. Cook covered on low-to-medium heat until chicken is cooked through, about 20 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. (No water is needed; the chicken cooks in the sauce).
  2. Shred: Remove the cooked chicken from the pot and shred it using two forks. Reserve the sauce remaining in the pot.
  3. Prep: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Layer 1: Spread about ¼ cup of the leftover warm enchilada sauce over the bottom of a small baking dish (one that fits 2 tortillas side-by-side or slightly overlapping). Place 2 tortillas on the bottom. Top with ⅓ of the shredded chicken, ⅓ of the remaining sauce, and ⅓ of the cheese.
  5. Layer 2: Place 2 more tortillas on top. Top with ⅓ of the chicken, ⅓ of the remaining sauce, and ⅓ of the cheese.
  6. Layer 3: Repeat with the final 2 tortillas, the remaining chicken, the remaining sauce, and the remaining cheese.
  7. Bake: Bake uncovered for 20 to 30 minutes, until the casserole is bubbly and the cheese has melted and started to brown on top.
  8. Serve: Let stand for 5 minutes before serving warm.

Notes

  • Shortcut: To save even more time, use 2 cups of shredded rotisserie chicken. Skip the poaching step and just mix the rotisserie chicken with 1/2 cup of the enchilada sauce before layering.
  • Tortillas: Corn tortillas offer the best flavor and texture for this dish. Flour tortillas can become gummy when baked in sauce.
  • Additions: Feel free to add a layer of black beans, corn, or diced onions between the tortillas for extra bulk.
