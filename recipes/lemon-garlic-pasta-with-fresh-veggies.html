Lemon Garlic Pasta with Fresh Veggies

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Italian

This pasta dish is proof that healthy eating doesn't have to be bland. By using high-protein penne and an abundance of fresh garden vegetables, we create a voluminous meal that is high in fiber and protein. The sauce is light and bright, relying on garlic-infused olive oil and a splash of citrus rather than heavy butter or cream.

Ingredients

Pasta & Produce

  • 2 cups high-protein penne pasta, uncooked
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, stemmed, seeded and sliced into thin strips
  • 1 zucchini (small to medium), halved lengthwise and sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ¼ cup red onion, diced

Aromatics & Finishing

  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, loosely packed and torn
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt (divided)
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Directions

  1. Boil Pasta: Cook the penne in a large pot of salted water according to package directions. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water before draining. Set pasta aside.
  2. Sauté Veggies: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the broccoli, red onions, bell pepper, and zucchini. Sprinkle with half of the salt. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onions are translucent and veggies are tender-crisp.
  3. Infuse Garlic: Add the sliced garlic to the skillet. Cook for 30–60 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to let the garlic brown or burn.
  4. Simmer: Add the broth and halved cherry tomatoes. Cook for an additional 3 minutes. The tomatoes should just begin to soften.
  5. Toss: Add the cooked pasta into the skillet. If the pasta seems dry, splash in a bit of the reserved pasta water to create a light sauce.
  6. Finish: Remove from heat. Toss in the torn basil leaves, lemon juice, remaining salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes. Stir well to combine and serve immediately.

Notes

  • Pasta Water Secret: The starchy water used to boil the pasta helps the lemon-oil sauce cling to the noodles. Always save a little before draining!
  • Veggie Variations: This recipe is "fridge-clearance" friendly. Asparagus, snap peas, or spinach also work beautifully here.
  • Protein Boost: Add grilled chicken strips, sautéed shrimp, or chickpeas to further increase the protein content.
  • Cheese: While not included in the macros, a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano is a classic addition.
