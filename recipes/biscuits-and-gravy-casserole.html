Biscuits and Gravy Casserole

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

breakfast • Southern American

This Biscuits and Gravy Casserole combines all the best parts of a country breakfast—savory sausage gravy, fluffy eggs, melted cheese, and buttery biscuits—into one easy bake. It’s perfect for feeding a crowd on holiday mornings because you can do all the work at once rather than standing over the stove flipping eggs.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage, pork or turkey
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 ¾ cups whole milk, divided
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1½ cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 can refrigerated biscuit dough (8 biscuits), or 8 homemade biscuits, cut into quarters

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat your oven to 375°F and lightly grease a 9×13-inch baking dish.
  2. Brown the Sausage: In a large skillet set over medium heat, cook and crumble the sausage until browned. Drain excess grease if needed. Note: This is optional. The grease adds tons of savory sausage flavor.
  3. Mix Eggs: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and ¼ cup milk until smooth. Set aside.
  4. Make the Roux: Sprinkle the flour, salt, pepper, and garlic powder over the cooked sausage. Stir and cook for about 30 seconds to cook out the raw flour taste.
  5. Make the Gravy: Gradually whisk in the remaining 2 ½ cups milk. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat, then reduce to medium-low. Stir until thickened, about 5 minutes.
  6. Assemble: Spread the sausage gravy evenly into the prepared baking dish and sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese over the gravy.
  7. Top: Arrange the biscuit quarters evenly on top, leaving space between each of the pieces. Pour the egg mixture over the top.
  8. Bake: Bake uncovered for 30 to 35 minutes, until the biscuits are golden and the center is set. An instant-read thermometer should register 165°F in the middle.
  9. Serve: Allow the casserole to rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy!

Notes

  • Biscuit Size: If you use "Grand" style biscuits, cut them into smaller pieces (6-8 pieces per biscuit) to ensure they cook through completely without burning the top.
  • Make Ahead: You can brown the sausage and make the gravy the night before. Store it in the fridge, then gently reheat it before assembling the casserole the next morning.
  • Spicy Kick: Use "Hot" breakfast sausage or add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the gravy for a little heat.
