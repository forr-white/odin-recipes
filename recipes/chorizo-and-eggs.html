Chorizo and Eggs

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

breakfast • Mexican

Huevos con Chorizo is a staple in Mexican kitchens. The rich, spiced paprika and chili oils from the chorizo bleed into the eggs, creating a flavorful, vibrant scramble. While it can be eaten on a plate with beans, it is most famously served inside warm flour tortillas for breakfast tacos.

Ingredients

The Scramble

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 Roma tomato, finely chopped
  • ½ white onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 9–12 ounces Mexican pork chorizo (raw, uncooked)
  • 6 large eggs
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

For Serving

  • Queso fresco or Cotija cheese
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Warm flour or corn tortillas
  • Salsa or hot sauce

Directions

  1. Sauté Veggies: Heat the olive oil in a medium skillet over medium-high heat. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and onions. Cook for 5 minutes until the onions are translucent and the tomatoes start to soften.
  2. Add Garlic: Stir in the garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Cook Chorizo: Remove the chorizo from the casing and add it to the skillet. Using a wooden spoon or spatula, crumble it up into small pieces and sauté for 8 minutes until fully cooked through and browned.
  4. Drain Fat (Important): If the chorizo is extra fatty and releases a lot of grease (it likely will), carefully tilt the pan and spoon off or drain the excess fat. Leave just enough to coat the pan for the eggs.
  5. Scramble: In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs. Pour them into the skillet over the meat and veggies. Let them sit for about 30 seconds to set slightly, then continue gently stirring until the eggs are just cooked through but still moist.
  6. Finish: Remove the pan from the heat. Taste first, then season with salt and black pepper (chorizo is already salty, so be careful). Garnish with queso fresco and cilantro, and serve immediately with warm tortillas.

Notes

  • Chorizo Type: Make sure you buy Mexican Chorizo (raw, fresh ground pork) and not Spanish Chorizo (cured, dried sausage). They are very different products.
  • Grease Control: Chorizo is naturally very fatty. Do not skip the draining step, or your eggs will end up oily.
  • Serving Idea: Serve with a side of refried beans and avocado for a complete "Desayuno Completo."
