Savory Potato Skins

Submitted by Andrea Holcomb

snacks • American

Most restaurant potato skins are loaded with cheese and bacon, but the skin itself is delicious enough to stand on its own. This recipe turns the humble potato peel into a crispy, seasoned chip. It’s a fantastic zero-waste snack that lets you use the scooped-out potato for mash while enjoying the skins as a crunchy appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 4 large baking potatoes (Russets work best, about 12 oz each)
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Optional Dip: Sour cream and chopped fresh chives

Directions

  1. Bake Potatoes: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Scrub the potatoes clean and pierce them several times with a fork to allow steam to escape. Place on a baking sheet and bake until tender, about 1 to 1¼ hours.
  2. Cool & Slice: Remove potatoes from the oven and let them cool until they are easy to handle. Cut each potato in half lengthwise.
  3. Scoop: Gently scoop out the white pulp, leaving a ¼-inch thick shell of potato attached to the skin. (Save the scooped-out potato for mashed potatoes or gnocchi!)
  4. Cut into Strips: Cut each hollowed-out potato boat lengthwise into 4 strips (quarters). You should end up with 32 strips total. Return them to the baking sheet, skin-side down.
  5. Season: In a small bowl, mix the salt, garlic powder, and paprika. Brush the inside (flesh side) of the skins with the melted butter. Sprinkle the seasoning mix evenly over the butter.
  6. Broil: Turn the oven to Broil. Place the pan 4–5 inches from the heat source. Broil until golden brown and crispy, about 5–8 minutes. Watch closely to prevent burning.
  7. Serve: Serve warm, optionally with a side of sour cream and chives for dipping.

Notes

  • Don't Waste the Inside: This recipe pairs perfectly with a mashed potato dinner. Bake the potatoes, scoop them out for mash, and save the skins for a pre-dinner snack.
  • Extra Crispy: If you want them extra crispy, you can brush the outer skin with a little oil or butter as well before broiling.
  • Cheesy Version: If you prefer a "loaded" style, sprinkle cheddar cheese and bacon bits on top during the last minute of broiling.
