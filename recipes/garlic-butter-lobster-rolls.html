Garlic Butter Lobster Rolls

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American (New England)

There are two main schools of thought for lobster rolls: Maine (chilled with mayo) and Connecticut (warm with butter). This recipe is a classic Connecticut-style roll. It skips the heavy mayonnaise in favor of warm, tender chunks of lobster tossed in a rich garlic-lemon butter sauce, served in a perfectly toasted bun.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lobster meat (freshly cooked or high-quality frozen/thawed)
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 4 New England-style split-top rolls
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Directions

  1. Prep Lobster: If using raw lobster, boil or steam until just cooked through (bright red), about 8–10 minutes. Let cool slightly, remove shells, and chop the meat into large bite-sized chunks. If using pre-cooked meat, simply chop it.
  2. Make Butter Sauce: In a skillet over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant but not browned, about 1–2 minutes.
  3. Warm Meat: Reduce heat to low. Add the chopped lobster to the skillet. Stir gently for just 2–3 minutes. You only want to warm the lobster through and coat it in the butter; cooking it too long will make it rubbery.
  4. Season: Remove from heat. Stir in the fresh lemon juice and chopped parsley. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper if desired.
  5. Toast Buns: While the lobster rests, butter the outsides of the New England rolls. Toast them in a separate pan (or on a griddle) over medium heat until golden brown and crisp on both sides.
  6. Assemble: Fill each warm, toasted roll generously with the buttery lobster mixture. Drizzle any remaining garlic butter from the pan over the top. Serve immediately with extra lemon wedges.

Notes

  • The Bun Matters: A true New England roll is a "split-top" hot dog bun with flat sides for grilling. If you can't find them, you can slice a tiny sliver off the sides of a regular hot dog bun to create a flat surface for toasting.
  • Don't Overcook: Lobster turns tough very quickly. If you are starting with pre-cooked meat (which most frozen options are), be very gentle when warming it in the butter.
  • Add-ins: Some purists add a pinch of celery salt or minced chives for extra depth.
