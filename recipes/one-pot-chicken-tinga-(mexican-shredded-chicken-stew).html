One-Pot Chicken Tinga

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican

Originating from Puebla, Mexico, Tinga is a dish defined by the smoky heat of chipotles in adobo and the acidity of tomatoes. This one-pot method maximizes flavor by searing the chicken and blistering the vegetables in the same pan. It also includes a "secret weapon"—a dash of fish sauce—which mimics the savory depth usually only found in slow-simmered stews.

  • Dutch Oven or Large Saucepan (Deep enough to hold the liquid and chicken)
  • Immersion Blender (Allows you to blend the sauce directly in the pot)
  • Tongs (For flipping the chicken and removing the hot tomatillos)

Ingredients

The Chicken & Fat

  • 2 bone-in, skin-on chicken breast halves (about 1¼ lbs)
  • 2 tablespoons lard or vegetable oil
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

The Sauce Base

  • 6 oz tomatillos, husked and rinsed (about 2 medium)
  • 6 oz ripe plum tomatoes (about 2 medium)
  • 4 medium garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1 small white onion, finely chopped
  • 2 to 3 canned chipotle chiles in adobo sauce (+ 1 tbsp sauce from can)
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken stock (or water)

Aromatics & Seasoning

  • 2 teaspoons dried Mexican oregano
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons cider vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons Asian fish sauce (optional but recommended)

Directions

  1. Sear Chicken: Season chicken generously with salt and pepper. Heat the lard (or oil) in a Dutch oven over high heat. Place chicken skin-side down. Cook without moving it until well-browned (6–8 minutes). Flip and cook for 2 minutes on the other side. Remove chicken to a plate.
  2. Blister Veggies: Immediately add the whole tomatillos, tomatoes, and garlic cloves to the hot fat in the pot. Cook, flipping occasionally, until blistered and browned in spots (about 5 minutes).
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Add the chopped onion and cook until softened (2 minutes). Add the oregano and bay leaves; cook for 30 seconds until aromatic.
  4. Simmer Chicken: Pour in the vinegar and chicken stock. Return the chicken breasts to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20–30 minutes, or until the chicken reaches 145°F internally.
  5. Reduce & Blend: Remove the chicken to a bowl to cool. Increase heat to high and boil the sauce (uncovered) until reduced by half (about 5 minutes). Add the chipotle chiles and adobo sauce. Remove and discard the bay leaves. Use an immersion blender to blend the sauce until smooth.
  6. Shred & Finish: Discard the skin and bones from the chicken. Shred the meat finely. Return the meat to the sauce. Stir in the fish sauce. Simmer for a few minutes until the sauce clings to the chicken. Season with salt to taste.

Notes

  • Why Skin-On? Even though we discard the skin later, cooking the breast with the skin on protects the meat from drying out and renders delicious fat into the sauce base.
  • Fish Sauce: It may sound odd in a Mexican dish, but it adds a punch of umami (savory flavor) that makes the tomatoes taste meatier. You won't taste "fish" in the final result.
  • Serving: Tinga is traditionally served on tostadas with crema and cheese, but it is excellent in tacos, burritos, or rice bowls.
