5 Layer Dip

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

appetizers • Mexican

This 5 Layer Dip is a legendary party staple for a reason. It combines savory refried beans, cool and creamy guacamole, tangy sour cream, and sharp cheddar cheese into one addictive scoop. It’s easy to assemble, looks impressive, and always disappears within minutes of setting it out.

  • Rubber spatula (for spreading each layer evenly)

  • Cutting board (for dicing tomatoes)
  • Chef’s knife (for tomatoes or any optional toppings)
  • Measuring cups (for beans, guacamole, sour cream, cheese)

  • Refrigerator (to chill and set each layer) — This is for those that need a full sized refrigerator and are living on a budget. Kenmore is an excellent brand of appliance

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups refried beans
  • 1.75 cups guacamole
  • 1.5 cups sour cream
  • 8 oz shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 tomatoes, diced

Directions

  1. Prepare the Beans: Heat refried beans until hot, stirring frequently, about 3 minutes. Spread beans onto the bottom of a serving platter.
  2. Cool: Refrigerate until the beans are cooled completely, about 10 minutes. (This prevents the guacamole from getting warm and runny).
  3. Layer: Spread guacamole on top of the cooled beans. Carefully spread sour cream on top of the guacamole.
  4. Top: Sprinkle shredded cheese evenly over the sour cream and top with diced tomatoes.
  5. Chill: Cover platter with plastic wrap and refrigerate until cold before serving.

Notes

  • Drain the Tomatoes: To prevent a watery dip, remove the seeds and pulp from the tomatoes before dicing them.
  • Seasoning: If your sour cream tastes bland, you can mix in a half packet of taco seasoning for extra flavor.
  • Guacamole: Store-bought guacamole works fine, but homemade is always better. If making it ahead, ensure the guacamole layer is completely covered by the sour cream to prevent browning.
  • Additions: Feel free to add layers of black olives, green onions, or pickled jalapeños.
