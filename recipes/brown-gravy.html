Brown Gravy

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • American

This homemade brown gravy is a kitchen staple that comes together in minutes. By combining chicken and beef broth, you achieve a complex depth of flavor that tastes like it simmered all day—no pan drippings required. The secret finishing step gives it a glossy, restaurant-quality shine.

  • Medium Saucepan (perfect size for rapid simmering)
  • Whisk (essential for a lump-free gravy)
  • Small Jar with Lid (the easiest way to mix the cornstarch slurry)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cube beef bouillon (or 1 teaspoon Better Than Bouillon)
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1½ teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • ¼ cup cold water
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Directions

  1. Combine Base: Add the chicken broth, beef broth, beef bouillon, onion powder, garlic powder, and Worcestershire sauce to a medium saucepan. Stir to combine.
  2. Make Slurry: Combine the cold water and cornstarch in a small bowl with a lid (or a jar). Shake vigorously to combine until smooth.
  3. Boil and Thicken: Bring the gravy mixture in the saucepan to a boil. Whisk in the cornstarch mixture immediately. Decrease heat to medium-low and continue to whisk to ensure that it blends smoothly.
  4. Simmer: Let it gently simmer until it reaches your desired thickness (usually 2–3 minutes).
  5. Finish: Remove from heat. Swirl in one tablespoon of cold butter just before serving to add a silky, glossy finish.

Notes

  • Why Two Broths? Using only beef broth can sometimes taste one-note or metallic. Cutting it with chicken broth rounds out the flavor profile for a more savory "roasted" taste.
  • The Butter Finish: This technique is called "monter au beurre" in French cooking. Adding cold butter at the very end emulsifies the sauce, giving it a velvety mouthfeel and shine.
  • Salty? Store-bought broths vary wildly in sodium. Use low-sodium broth if you are sensitive to salt, as the bouillon also adds saltiness.
