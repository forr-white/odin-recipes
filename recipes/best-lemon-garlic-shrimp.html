Best Lemon Garlic Shrimp

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mediterranean

This Lemon Garlic Shrimp is the definition of a high-reward, low-effort meal. Marinating the shrimp briefly ensures every bite is packed with zesty citrus and savory garlic flavor. It sears up in minutes, creating a luscious butter sauce that is perfect for spooning over pasta, rice, or crusty bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 ½ tbsp olive oil, divided
  • Juice of one lemon
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 lemon, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp parsley, freshly chopped

Directions

  1. Marinate: Using a medium-sized bowl, combine the shrimp, ½ tablespoon olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, paprika, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper to taste. Marinate for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat the pan: In a large skillet, melt the butter and remaining tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Sear: Add the marinated shrimp (along with any juices) and lemon slices to the skillet in a single layer.
  4. Cook and Serve: Cook shrimp for 2–3 minutes on each side until pink and opaque. Do not overcook. Remove from heat immediately and serve with freshly chopped parsley on top.

Notes

  • Don't Overcook: Shrimp cook very quickly. As soon as they turn pink and form a "C" shape, they are done. If they curl into a tight "O", they are overcooked and rubbery.
  • Serving Suggestions: Serve over angel hair pasta, jasmine rice, or with a side of roasted asparagus.
  • Spice Level: If you are sensitive to heat, reduce the red pepper flakes to ¼ teaspoon or omit them entirely.
  • Thawing: If using frozen shrimp, thaw them completely under cold running water and pat them dry before marinating to ensure a good sear.
