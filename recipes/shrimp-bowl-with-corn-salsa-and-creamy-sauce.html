Shrimp Bowl with Corn Salsa

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican Fusion

This bowl is the definition of summer food—fresh, colorful, and light. It combines smoky, spiced shrimp with the sweetness of corn and the richness of avocado. The real star, however, is the creamy garlic sauce that ties everything together. It works perfectly as a low-carb meal or served over rice for something heartier.

Ingredients

The Shrimp

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

The Corn Salsa

  • 1 bag (10–12 oz) frozen corn, thawed (or fresh corn cut from cob)
  • 1 cup red onion, diced
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional)
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • Salt to taste

Creamy Garlic Sauce

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

The Bowl Base

  • 1 large avocado, sliced or mashed
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Cooked white rice or quinoa (optional base)

Directions

  1. Season Shrimp: Preheat your grill (or indoor grill pan) to medium-high heat. In a large bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne. Ensure they are evenly coated.
  2. Make Salsa: While the grill heats, combine the thawed corn, diced red onion, ½ cup chopped cilantro, minced jalapeño, and lime juice in a bowl. Season with salt to taste. Mix well and set aside.
  3. Make Sauce: In a separate small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, sour cream, 1 tbsp cilantro, lemon juice, minced garlic, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  4. Grill: Place the shrimp on the hot grill. Cook for 2–3 minutes per side until pink and opaque. (Watch closely—shrimp become rubbery if overcooked). Remove from heat immediately.
  5. Assemble: Start with a base of avocado (or rice, if using). Arrange the grilled shrimp on top. Spoon a generous amount of corn salsa over the shrimp.
  6. Finish: Drizzle heavily with the creamy garlic sauce. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Stovetop Method: No grill? No problem. Sear the shrimp in a skillet over medium-high heat with a little butter for 2 minutes per side.
  • Corn Tip: For extra flavor, char the corn in a dry skillet for 5 minutes before mixing it into the salsa. This mimics the flavor of grilled "elote."
  • Make Ahead: The sauce and salsa can be made up to 2 days in advance. Cook the shrimp fresh for the best texture.
