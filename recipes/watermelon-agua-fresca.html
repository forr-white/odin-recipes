Watermelon Agua Fresca

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • Mexican

Agua de Sandía is light, hydrating, and not too sweet—essentially the perfect summer beverage. Unlike a smoothie, this is strained to be thin and refreshing like a juice. The addition of fresh lime juice cuts the sugar of the melon and makes the flavor pop.

  • Blender
  • Fine Mesh Sieve (Essential for straining out the pulp)
  • Large Pitcher

Ingredients

  • 6 cups ripe watermelon, cubed (seedless is easier, but seeded works if straining)
  • 2 cups cold water
  • 1 large lime, juiced (approx. 2 tbsp)
  • 1–2 tablespoons sweetener (sugar, agave, or honey), optional depending on melon sweetness
  • 1 cup ice cubes (plus more for serving)
  • Fresh mint sprigs (for garnish)

Directions

  1. Prep Melon: Cut the watermelon into cubes. You need about 6 heaping cups.
  2. Blend: Add the watermelon, cold water, and lime juice to a blender. Blend on high for 1 minute until completely liquefied.
  3. Strain (Recommended): For an authentic texture, pour the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve into a pitcher to remove the fibrous pulp. (Note: If you have a high-powered blender and don't mind a thicker texture, you can skip this, but straining makes it more refreshing).
  4. Sweeten: Taste the juice. If the watermelon wasn't very sweet, stir in 1 tablespoon of sugar or agave syrup. If the melon was ripe, you might not need any sugar at all.
  5. Chill: Stir in 1 cup of ice to cool it down quickly. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to let flavors meld.
  6. Serve: Pour over fresh ice in glasses. Garnish with a sprig of mint and a slice of watermelon.

Notes

  • Choosing a Melon: Look for a watermelon with a creamy yellow "field spot" on one side. This indicates it sat on the vine longer and is likely sweeter.
  • Storage: Natural separation will occur (the "water" separates from the "solid" particles). This is normal. Just shake the jar or stir the pitcher vigorously before serving. Keeps for 3 days.
  • Adult Version: This makes an incredible cocktail mixer. Add a shot of tequila or vodka and coat the rim of the glass with Tajín (chili-lime salt).
