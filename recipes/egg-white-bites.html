Egg White Bites

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

breakfast • American

These are a homemade copycat of the famous "Sous Vide" egg bites from Starbucks. The secret ingredient is ricotta cheese, which gives the egg whites a velvety, creamy texture without needing a fancy water bath machine. Packed with protein and veggies, they are the perfect grab-and-go healthy breakfast.

Recommended Tools

  • Silicone Muffin Liners (Highly recommended—eggs stick to metal pans like crazy!)
  • Blender or Food Processor (For the smoothest texture)
  • 12-Cup Muffin Pan

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups ricotta cheese (whole milk preferred for texture)
  • 1½ cups liquid egg whites (carton)
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1 cup kale or spinach, shredded and stems removed
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Spray a 12-cup muffin pan heavily with nonstick spray, or better yet, line with silicone baking cups.
  2. The Base: For the best texture, place the ricotta cheese and liquid egg whites in a blender or food processor. Blend until completely smooth and combined. (Alternatively, whisk vigorously in a bowl, though the texture will be slightly more rustic).
  3. Add Veggies: Pour the egg mixture into a bowl (if you used a blender). Fold in the diced bell pepper, shredded kale, salt, and pepper until evenly distributed.
  4. Fill: Transfer the mixture to the muffin cups, filling them about ¾ full (leave ¼ inch of space at the top as they will puff up).
  5. Bake: Bake for 25–30 minutes. The edges should be golden, and the centers should be fully set and spring back to the touch.
  6. Cool: Let them cool in the pan for 5–10 minutes. They will deflate slightly as they cool—this is normal. Pop them out and serve.

Notes

  • Sticking Warning: Egg whites act like glue in metal pans. Even with cooking spray, they can be hard to remove. Silicone liners are the best investment for this recipe.
  • Steam Bath Trick: To make them even softer and more like "sous vide," place a baking dish filled with 1 inch of hot water on the bottom rack of the oven while baking to create steam.
  • Storage: These freeze beautifully. Wrap individually in plastic wrap and freeze. Reheat in the microwave for 45–60 seconds.
