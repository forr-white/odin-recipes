Sheet Pan Quesadillas

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican American

If you need to feed a hungry family or a party crowd, standing at the stove flipping individual quesadillas is a nightmare. Enter the Sheet Pan Quesadilla. By arranging tortillas to overlap, you create one giant, crispy, cheesy mega-quesadilla that bakes in the oven. Slice it into squares and you're done!

Recommended Tools

  • Rimmed Baking Sheet (Standard Half Sheet) (You actually need two of these—one to bake in, and one to place on top as a weight)
  • Pastry Brush (For buttering the tortillas)
  • Pizza Cutter (The easiest way to slice the finished dish)

Ingredients

The Filling

  • 3 cups cooked chicken, shredded (rotisserie works great)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can (10 oz) Rotel (diced tomatoes with green chilies), drained
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper

The Build

  • 8 large flour tortillas (burrito size are best for overlapping)
  • 4 cups shredded Mexican blend cheese
  • 3 tbsp salted butter, melted

Chipotle Crema (Optional)

  • ½ cup sour cream
  • 1–2 tbsp adobo sauce (from a can of chipotle peppers)
  • ½ lime, juiced
  • Pinch of salt

Directions

  1. Prep Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Make Filling: In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, black beans, drained Rotel, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Toss until well combined.
  3. Arrange Base: Brush the bottom and sides of a large rimmed baking sheet generously with melted butter. Arrange 6 tortillas around the perimeter of the pan so that half of each tortilla hangs over the edge. Place 1 tortilla in the center to cover the gap.
  4. Layer: Sprinkle 2 cups (half) of the cheese evenly over the tortillas. Spread the chicken mixture on top of the cheese. Top with the remaining 2 cups of cheese.
  5. Fold: Place the last tortilla in the center on top of the filling. Fold the overhanging tortillas inward toward the center. They should overlap and completely cover the filling.
  6. Butter & Press: Brush the top of the folded tortillas generously with the remaining melted butter. Place a second baking sheet directly on top of the quesadilla. (Note: This weight keeps the quesadilla flat and ensures the bottom gets crispy).
  7. Bake: Bake for 20 minutes with the second pan on top.
  8. Crisp: Remove the top baking sheet. Bake for another 10–15 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  9. Serve: Let cool for 5 minutes before slicing into squares. Serve with the chipotle crema (whisk sour cream, adobo, lime, and salt), fresh cilantro, and jalapeños.

Notes

  • Don't Skip the Weight: Using the second sheet pan on top is the secret. If you don't weight it down, the tortillas will puff up, the filling will separate, and it won't be sliceable.
  • Vegetarian Option: Swap the chicken for an extra can of beans, corn, and sautéed bell peppers.
  • Drain Well: Make sure the Rotel tomatoes and black beans are drained very well, otherwise the bottom crust may get soggy.
