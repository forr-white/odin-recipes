Fondant Potatoes

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • French

Pommes de Terre Fondantes ("Melting Potatoes") is a classic French technique that transforms humble ingredients into a luxurious side dish. The potatoes are cut into cylinders, seared until golden brown, and then slow-roasted in plenty of butter and chicken stock. The result is a potato with a crispy top and bottom, and an interior that is incredibly creamy and flavorful.

  • Cast Iron Skillet or Stainless Steel Pan (Must be oven-safe)
  • Vegetable Peeler (Y-peelers are generally faster)
  • Tongs (For flipping the hot potatoes)

Ingredients

  • 4 medium russet potatoes (about 1½ pounds total)
  • 3 cloves garlic, lightly smashed and peeled
  • 4 tablespoons (½ stick) unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme, plus more for garnish
  • ¾ cup low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth

Directions

  1. Prep Potatoes: Peel the potatoes. Slice off the rounded ends to create flat tops and bottoms, then cut each potato in half crosswise. You should have 8 sturdy, flat potato cylinders (about 2 inches tall).
  2. Soak: Place the potato rounds in a large bowl and cover with cold water. Let sit for 20 minutes at room temperature. This removes excess starch and prevents the outside from getting gummy.
  3. Preheat: Arrange a rack in the middle of the oven and preheat to 400°F (200°C).
  4. Dry: Drain the potatoes, rinse with cold water, and pat them very dry with paper towels. Season both flat sides with the kosher salt and black pepper.
  5. Sear: Heat the canola oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add the potatoes cut-side down in a single layer. Cook without moving them until deep golden brown, about 6 to 7 minutes.
  6. Add Aromatics: Flip the potatoes using tongs. Add the smashed garlic, butter cubes, and thyme sprigs to the pan. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes, spooning the foaming butter over the potatoes, until the butter starts to brown and smell nutty.
  7. Braise: Carefully pour in the chicken broth (it will steam vigorously).
  8. Bake: Transfer the skillet to the oven. Bake until the potatoes are fork-tender and the broth has reduced significantly, about 30 to 35 minutes.
  9. Serve: Serve hot, garnished with fresh thyme and drizzled with the remaining buttery pan juices.

Notes

  • Herb Options: Thyme is classic, but rosemary, sage, or oregano work beautifully as well.
  • Make Ahead: You can peel and cut the potatoes and leave them soaking in water in the fridge for up to 2 days before cooking.
  • Sticking: If the potatoes are stuck to the pan when you try to flip them in step 5, let them cook for another minute. They will release naturally once a crust forms.
