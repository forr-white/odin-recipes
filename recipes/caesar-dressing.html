Caesar Dressing

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Italian American

This homemade Caesar Dressing is rich, creamy, and packed with that signature savory punch you crave. By using high-quality mayonnaise as the base, you get a perfectly emulsified dressing in minutes without worrying about raw eggs. It’s perfect for salads, wraps, or even as a dip for roasted vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Mayo (use a high-quality brand like Duke's or Hellmann's)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp Dijon Mustard
  • 1 tsp anchovy paste
  • 2 small or 1 large clove garlic, minced
  • ¼ tsp salt and pepper
  • ½ cup freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano

Directions

  1. Combine Base: In a medium bowl or jar, add the mayonnaise, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, anchovy paste, and minced garlic. Whisk until smooth and well combined.
  2. Add Cheese: Stir in the freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Whisk again until the cheese is incorporated.
  3. Season: Add salt and pepper. Taste and adjust if necessary (add more lemon for acidity or more pepper for spice). Serve immediately or chill.

Notes

  • Anchovy Paste: Don't skip it! It provides the essential umami "Caesar" flavor. It won't taste fishy, just savory. If you absolutely must substitute it, add extra Worcestershire sauce and a pinch of salt.
  • Storage: Store in an airtight jar in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
  • Consistency: If the dressing is too thick for your liking, whisk in a teaspoon of water or milk at a time until it reaches your desired consistency.
