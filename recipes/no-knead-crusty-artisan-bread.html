No-Knead Crusty Artisan Bread

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

bread • European

This is the bread recipe that changed home baking forever. By using a high hydration dough and a very long, slow rise (12–18 hours), the gluten develops naturally without any kneading. Baking it inside a preheated Dutch oven traps the steam, creating a professional-quality crackly crust and a soft, airy interior.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (or bread flour for a chewier texture)
  • 1½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1½ cups warm water (approx. 100°F–110°F)

Directions

  1. The Mix: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and yeast. Pour in the warm water and stir with a wooden spoon or spatula until combined. The dough will look "shaggy," sticky, and messy—this is normal. Do not knead.
  2. The Long Rise: Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap. Let it sit on the counter at room temperature for 12 to 18 hours. The dough is ready when it has puffed up, is dotted with bubbles, and jiggles when you move the bowl.
  3. Preheat: When ready to bake, place your empty Dutch oven (with the lid on) into the oven. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C). Let the pot heat up for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Shape: While the oven heats, turn the sticky dough out onto a heavily floured surface. Fold the corners of the dough inward a few times to form a rough ball.
  5. Rest: Place the dough ball on a large square of parchment paper. Cover loosely with plastic wrap or a towel and let it rest while the oven finishes preheating.
  6. Bake (Covered): Carefully remove the hot Dutch oven. Lift the dough by the corners of the parchment paper and lower it into the pot (paper and all). Cover with the hot lid. Bake for 30 minutes.
  7. Bake (Uncovered): Remove the lid. Bake for an additional 10–15 minutes until the bread is a deep golden brown and the crust is hard.
  8. Cool: Remove the bread from the pot and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 15 minutes before slicing.

Notes

  • Parchment Paper Tip: The dough is very sticky. Shaping it on parchment paper and using the paper as a "sling" to lower it into the hot pot is much safer than trying to drop it in by hand.
  • Doneness Test: Tap the bottom of the loaf. If it sounds hollow, it is cooked through.
  • Flour: You can substitute up to 1 cup of whole wheat flour for a heartier loaf, though it will be slightly denser.
