Triple Cheese Au Gratin Potatoes

Submitted by Forrest White / Cook Anything Kitchen

Side Dish • Cheddar, Monterey Jack & Parmesan baked potatoes

Ingredients

Potatoes & Base

  • 3 lbs russet or Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced (⅛–¼ inch)
  • 2 tbsp butter (for greasing the dish)
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Cheese Sauce

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk (or half-and-half for richer sauce)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp Cheddar
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper

Optional Add-Ins

  • Fresh thyme or parsley
  • Sautéed onions
  • Bacon bits
  • Crushed garlic

Directions

  1. Prep the potatoes
    Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9×13 baking dish with butter. Thinly slice the potatoes (a mandoline makes this easy) and set aside.
  2. Make the cheese sauce
    In a large saucepan, melt 4 tbsp butter over medium heat. Whisk in 4 tbsp flour and cook for 1–2 minutes to form a blond roux. Slowly add the milk, whisking constantly, until thickened (about 3–5 minutes). Stir in the garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the Cheddar, Monterey Jack, and half of the Parmesan and stir until smooth and creamy.
  3. Assemble the casserole
    Layer half of the sliced potatoes in the prepared baking dish. Pour half of the cheese sauce evenly over the potatoes. Repeat with the remaining potatoes and cheese sauce. Sprinkle the remaining Parmesan over the top.
  4. Bake
    Cover the dish tightly with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 20–30 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly and the potatoes are tender when pierced with a knife.
  5. Rest & serve
    Let the Au Gratin potatoes rest for about 10 minutes before serving so the sauce can set and thicken slightly.

Notes

  • Yukon gold potatoes stay creamy and hold their shape; russets get softer and fluffier.
  • Add crushed garlic to the roux for a bolder, savory flavor.
  • A splash of chicken broth in the sauce adds deeper savory richness.
  • For an ultra-cheesy top, add an extra ½ cup of Cheddar during the last 10 minutes of baking.
