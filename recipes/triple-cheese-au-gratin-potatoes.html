Triple Cheese Au Gratin Potatoes

Submitted by Forrest White/Cook Anything Kitchen

sides

Ingredients

  • Potatoes & Base
  • 3 lbs russet or Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced (⅛–¼ inch)
  • 2 tbsp butter (for greasing the dish)
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • Cheese Sauce
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk (or half-and-half for richer sauce)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp Cheddar
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • Optional add-ins
  • Fresh thyme or parsley
  • Sautéed onions
  • Bacon bits
  • Crushed garlic

Directions

  1. 1. Prep the potatoes
  2. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Grease a 9×13 baking dish with butter.
  4. Thinly slice potatoes (mandoline makes this easy).
  5. 2. Make the cheese sauce
  6. In a large saucepan, melt 4 tbsp butter over medium heat.
  7. Whisk in 4 tbsp flour and cook 1–2 minutes to form a blond roux.
  8. Slowly add the milk, whisking constantly until thickened (about 3–5 minutes).
  9. Add seasonings: garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, pepper.
  10. Stir in Cheddar, Monterey Jack, and half the Parmesan until smooth.
  11. 3. Assemble
  12. Layer half the potatoes in the dish.
  13. Pour half the cheese sauce overtop.
  14. Repeat with remaining potatoes and sauce.
  15. Finish with the remaining Parmesan on top.
  16. 4. Bake
  17. Cover with foil and bake 45 minutes.
  18. Remove foil and bake 20–30 more minutes until golden and bubbly.
  19. Let rest 10 minutes before serving so it sets.

Notes

  • Yukon golds stay creamy and hold shape better; russets get softer and fluffier.
  • Add crushed garlic to the roux for extra flavor.
  • A splash of chicken broth in the sauce = deeper savory richness.
  • For ultra-cheesy top, add ½ cup extra Cheddar during the last 10 minutes.
