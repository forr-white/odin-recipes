Triple Cheese Au Gratin Potatoes

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • American

While scalloped potatoes are delicious, Au Gratin potatoes take it up a notch with the addition of plenty of cheese. This recipe features a "Triple Cheese" bechamel sauce using Sharp Cheddar for bite, Monterey Jack for meltiness, and Parmesan for a salty, nutty finish. It is the ultimate holiday side dish.

  • 9x13 Baking Dish (Glass or ceramic works best)
  • Mandoline Slicer (Essential for getting uniform thin slices so they cook evenly)
  • Whisk (To ensure a smooth, clump-free cheese sauce)

Ingredients

The Potatoes

  • 3 lbs Russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced (⅛ inch thick)
  • 2 tbsp butter (for greasing dish)

The Cheese Sauce

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk (warmed slightly helps prevent lumps)
  • 1 cup Sharp Cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup Monterey Jack cheese, shredded
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated (divided)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp paprika (smoked paprika is great too)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Generously grease a 9×13 inch baking dish with butter. Peel and slice the potatoes thinly (approx ⅛ inch). (Tip: Do not soak the potatoes in water; you want the starch to help thicken the sauce).
  2. Make Roux: In a large saucepan, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1–2 minutes until bubbling and golden (blond roux).
  3. Make Bechamel: Slowly pour in the milk a little at a time, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon (about 3–5 minutes).
  4. Melt Cheese: Remove from heat. Stir in the garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the Cheddar, Monterey Jack, and half of the Parmesan. Stir until smooth and creamy.
  5. Assemble: Layer half of the sliced potatoes in the baking dish. Pour half of the cheese sauce over them. Layer the remaining potatoes on top and cover with the remaining sauce. Sprinkle the rest of the Parmesan cheese on top.
  6. Bake Covered: Cover the dish tightly with aluminum foil (spray the foil with oil so cheese doesn't stick). Bake for 45 minutes.
  7. Bake Uncovered: Remove the foil. Bake for an additional 20–30 minutes, or until the top is bubbly and golden brown, and the potatoes are tender when pierced with a knife.
  8. Rest (Crucial): Let the dish rest for at least 15 minutes before serving. This allows the sauce to set so it stays creamy rather than running all over the plate.

Notes

  • Potato Choice: Yukon Golds will result in a creamier, slightly firmer texture. Russets will be fluffier and softer as they break down more. Both are excellent.
  • Extra Crispy Top: If you love a crunchy topping, sprinkle ½ cup of Panko breadcrumbs mixed with melted butter over the top during the last 20 minutes of baking.
  • Make Ahead: You can bake this fully, let it cool, and reheat it the next day (covered) at 350°F for 30 minutes. It often tastes even better the next day!
