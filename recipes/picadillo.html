Picadillo

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Latin American

Picadillo is a classic Latin American comfort dish, but every country makes it differently. This is the Mexican-style (Norteño) version—a savory, soupy ground beef stew packed with root vegetables and simmered in a fresh homemade tomato-chile salsa. Unlike Cuban picadillo, this version skips the raisins and olives in favor of potatoes, carrots, and squash.

Ingredients

The Fresh Salsa

  • 4 Roma tomatoes
  • 2 fresh jalapeños (stems removed)
  • 3 serrano peppers (optional, for extra heat)
  • ¼ white onion
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1 tbsp chicken bouillon (e.g., Knorr or Maggi)
  • 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano
  • ½ cup water

The Beef & Spices

  • 2 lbs lean ground beef
  • ½ onion, finely diced
  • 1 tbsp salt (plus more to taste)
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • ¼–½ cup canned tomato sauce (for color and body)

The Vegetables

  • 3 small potatoes, washed and diced small
  • 8 oz baby carrots, chopped (or 2 large carrots, diced)
  • 2 cups Mexican squash (calabacita) or zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup frozen or canned sweet corn

Directions

Make the Salsa

  1. Boil: Place the Roma tomatoes, jalapeños, and serranos in a small pot. Cover with water and bring to a boil. Simmer for about 10 minutes until the tomatoes are soft and skins are peeling.
  2. Blend: Transfer the boiled tomatoes and peppers to a blender (discard the boiling water). Add the ¼ raw white onion, 2 garlic cloves, ½ cup fresh water, chicken bouillon, and oregano. Blend until completely smooth. Set aside.

Cook the Stew

  1. Brown Beef: Heat a drizzle of oil in a large deep skillet over medium-high heat. Sauté the diced onion until tender. Add the ground beef. Break it apart with a spoon and season with the chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, pepper, and salt. Cook until fully browned.
  2. Hard Veggies: Add the diced potatoes and carrots to the browned meat. Stir well and cook for 5 minutes. (This head start ensures they soften before the delicate squash is added).
  3. Simmer: Pour in the blended fresh salsa and the canned tomato sauce. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. Taste the liquid and add salt if needed.
  4. Soft Veggies: Add the Mexican squash (zucchini), peas, and corn. Stir gently.
  5. Finish: Cover and simmer for 7–10 minutes, or until the potatoes and carrots are fork-tender and the squash is cooked through. Serve hot with Mexican rice and warm tortillas.

Notes

  • Heat Control: This recipe uses 5 chiles (2 jalapeño + 3 serrano) which creates a medium-hot broth. For a milder dish, remove the seeds from the peppers after boiling, or omit the serranos entirely.
  • Potatoes: Cut your potatoes into small, uniform cubes (about ½ inch) so they cook quickly and evenly. If they are too large, the rest of the stew will overcook while waiting for them.
  • Variations: Some families add green beans or chopped chayote to their picadillo. Feel free to swap veggies based on what you have in the fridge.
