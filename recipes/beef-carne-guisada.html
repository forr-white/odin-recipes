Beef Carne Guisada

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican

This Beef Carne Guisada is the ultimate Tex-Mex comfort food. Tender chunks of beef are simmered in a rich, savory gravy made from dried chiles and spices until they melt in your mouth. Serve it with warm flour tortillas to soak up every drop of the sauce.

Ingredients

For the Red Chile Sauce

  • 4 New Mexico Chile pods, dried
  • 2 Chile Ancho pods, dried
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 7 oz tomato sauce
  • 2½ cups water (divided use)
  • 1¼ tsp salt

For the Beef & Stew

  • 2–3 lbs skirt steak, cut into thin slices or cubes
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp beef bouillon
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp garlic salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1½ tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • ½ white onion, diced
  • 2 Roma tomatoes, diced
  • 7 oz green chiles (canned or roasted)
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)

Directions

  1. Prepare the Chile Sauce: Put New Mexico and Ancho Chiles in a small saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a boil on high heat. Once they begin to boil, turn off the heat. Transfer the chiles to a blender along with the garlic cloves, tomato sauce, 1¼ tsp salt, and 2 cups of fresh water. Blend until completely smooth. Set aside.
  2. Season the Beef: In a small bowl, mix the flour, 1½ tsp salt, black pepper, oregano, bouillon, cumin, and garlic salt. Toss this spice mixture with your sliced skirt steak until the meat is well coated.
  3. Sauté Vegetables: Over medium-high heat, add the oil to a large skillet or Dutch oven. Once hot, add the bell pepper and onion. Sauté for 3 minutes until slightly softened.
  4. Brown the Beef: Add the seasoned steak to the pan and stir frequently until browned. Once browned, add the diced tomatoes and green chiles. Mix until well combined. Let cook for a couple of minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Simmer: Add the blended red chile sauce to the pan and stir. Bring to a simmer, then taste for salt. Add the bay leaf, cover the pan, and let cook for 20–25 minutes until the sauce thickens and meat is tender.

Notes

  • Meat Choice: Skirt steak cooks relatively quickly and has great flavor, but you can also use chuck roast cut into small cubes. If using chuck, you may need to simmer for 45–60 minutes to get it tender.
  • Consistency: If the gravy gets too thick while simmering, add a splash of water or beef broth to reach your desired consistency.
  • Serving: This is traditionally served with rice, beans, and fresh flour tortillas.
