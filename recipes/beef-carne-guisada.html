Ingredients

  • 4 New Mexico Chile pods
  • 2 Chile ancho
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 7oz tomato sauce
  • 2 1/2 cup water
  • 1 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2-3 lbs of skirt steak, cut into thin slices
  • 3 tbsp all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp beef bouillon
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp garlic salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 white onion, diced
  • 2 roma tomatoes, diced
  • 7oz green chiles

Directions

  1. Put New Mexico and Ancho Chiles in a small saucepan and cover with water, on high heat, bring to a boil. Once they begin to boil, turn off the heat and transfer the chiles to a blender along with garlic cloves, tomato sauce, salt, and 2 cups of water. Blend until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Mix all purpose flour, salt, black pepper, oregano, bouillon, cumin, and garlic salt. Mix spices in with your skirt steak until well coated.
  3. Over medium high heat, add oil. Once hot, add bell pepper and onion. Saute for 3 minutes.
  4. Add steak and stir frequently, until browned. Once browned, add tomatoes and green chiles. Mix until well combined. Let cook for a couple minutes, stirring occasionally. Add sauce and stir.
  5. Bring to a simmer then taste for salt. Add bay leaf and cover then let cook for 20-25 minutes.
