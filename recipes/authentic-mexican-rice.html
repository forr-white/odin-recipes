Authentic Mexican Rice

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • Mexican

Forget the dry, bland boxed stuff. This Authentic Mexican Rice recipe uses a fresh tomato and garlic puree to achieve that vibrant orange color and savory flavor you love at your favorite restaurants. By toasting the rice first, you ensure every grain stays separate and fluffy.

Ingredients

  • 2 small roma tomatoes
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon Goya seasoning con culantro y achiote (the orange packet; use about half a packet)
  • For garnish: fresh chopped cilantro, lime wedges

Directions

  1. Make the puree: Roughly chop the roma tomatoes. Place the tomatoes, the garlic cloves, and ¾ cup of water in a blender. Blend on high until smooth.
  2. Measure liquid: Pour the tomato mixture into a measuring cup. You should have about 1 cup.
  3. Toast the rice: Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the rice and cook, stirring occasionally, for 1 minute until the grains turn opaque and slightly golden.
  4. Combine: Add 1 cup of the tomato mixture, 3 cups of water, salt, and Goya seasoning. (Note: The total liquid should equal 4 cups. If you have more than 1 cup of tomato puree, reduce the water slightly.) Stir to combine.
  5. Simmer: Bring mixture to a boil and then cover and reduce heat to low. Cook for 20 minutes.
  6. Rest and Fluff: Remove from the heat and let sit, covered, for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork, stirring in any of the tomato mixture that may have settled on top, and serve.

Notes

  • Don't Peek: Once you cover the pot and reduce the heat, do not lift the lid until the full cooking and resting time is up. Releasing the steam can result in undercooked rice.
  • The Toasting Step: Frying the rice in oil before adding liquid is traditional (known as "dorar") and prevents the rice from becoming mushy.
  • Sazon Substitutes: If you can't find Goya Sazon (culantro y achiote), you can substitute with a mix of turmeric, cumin, and extra paprika, though the flavor profile will be slightly different.
