Chicken Corn Chowder

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • American

This Chicken Corn Chowder is the definition of hearty comfort food. It starts with a base of crispy bacon and savory vegetables, thickened into a creamy chowder loaded with tender chunks of chicken, soft potatoes, and sweet corn. It’s a meal in a bowl that warms you from the inside out.

Ingredients

  • 6 strips bacon, cut into small pieces
  • 1.5 pounds chicken breast
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 small potatoes, diced (Yukon Gold or Red potatoes work best)
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 2 cups frozen corn
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Herbs for garnish (parsley, chives, or thyme), optional

Directions

  1. Render Bacon: Heat a large Dutch oven or soup pot over medium heat. Once hot, add the bacon pieces and cook, stirring frequently until just crispy, about 5 minutes. Remove the bacon from the pan and set aside, leaving the grease in the pot.
  2. Brown Chicken: Keep about one tablespoon of the bacon grease in the pan (drain excess if necessary). Season each side of the chicken breast with a pinch of salt and pepper. Brown each side of the chicken in the bacon fat for 3 minutes per side. Remove chicken from the pot (it will finish cooking later).
  3. Sauté Veggies: Add the butter to the pot. Once melted, add the onion and celery. Sauté until translucent and fragrant, about 5–7 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for an additional minute.
  4. Make Roux: Add the flour to the veggies and sauté for one minute, stirring frequently to cook out the raw flour taste.
  5. Simmer: Add the potatoes, chicken broth, salt, pepper, and the partially cooked chicken breasts back to the pot. Reduce the heat to medium-low and bring to a simmer. Cook, stirring frequently, for about 15–20 minutes or until the potatoes are tender and the chicken is cooked through.
  6. Shred: Remove the chicken from the pot and shred it into bite-sized pieces using two forks.
  7. Finish: Add the shredded chicken, corn, and heavy cream to the pot. Bring to a light simmer (do not boil vigorously), stirring frequently until everything is combined and thickened. Taste and add more salt and pepper as needed.
  8. Serve: Serve hot, topped with the reserved crumbled bacon and fresh herbs if desired.

Notes

  • Potato Choice: Yukon Gold or Red potatoes are best for chowder because they hold their shape well after boiling. Russet potatoes can be used, but they tend to fall apart and thicken the soup further.
  • Don't Boil Cream: Once you add the heavy cream, keep the heat at a gentle simmer. Boiling vigorously can cause the dairy to curdle or separate.
  • Leftovers: This soup thickens as it cools. When reheating leftovers, you may need to add a splash of chicken broth or milk to loosen it up.
