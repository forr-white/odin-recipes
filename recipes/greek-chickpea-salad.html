Greek Chickpea Salad

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • Greek

This is the ultimate "no-wilt" salad. Because there are no fragile leafy greens, this Greek Chickpea Salad actually gets better the longer it sits in the fridge, making it perfect for meal prep, picnics, or potlucks. It is packed with plant-based protein, crunchy vegetables, and salty feta cheese.

Ingredients

The Salad

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas (garbanzo beans), drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 English cucumber, diced (about 2 cups)
  • ½ red onion, finely diced
  • ½ cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped

The Dressing

  • ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Directions

  1. Prep Veggies: Chop the cucumber, tomatoes, and onion. Try to dice the cucumber and onion to roughly the same size as the chickpeas for the best texture in every bite.
  2. Make Dressing: In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, dried oregano, salt, pepper, and minced garlic until emulsified.
  3. Combine: In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, diced cucumber, red onion, olives, crumbled feta, and fresh parsley.
  4. Toss: Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat everything evenly.
  5. Marinate (Recommended): While you can eat it immediately, letting the salad sit in the refrigerator for 20–30 minutes allows the chickpeas to absorb the lemon-oregano flavor.

Notes

  • Cucumber Tip: If using a standard waxy cucumber (not English), peel it and scoop out the watery seeds before dicing to prevent the salad from getting soggy.
  • Protein Boost: Add grilled chicken or canned tuna to turn this side dish into a full meal.
  • Storage: This salad keeps well in the refrigerator for up to 3–4 days. Stir well before serving as the dressing may settle at the bottom.
