Easy Homemade Pickles

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

snacks • American

Forget the complicated water bath canning process. These "Refrigerator Pickles" are incredibly easy to make. Because they aren't cooked to death in a canner, they retain a satisfying crunch that store-bought brands often lack. The brine uses apple cider vinegar for a slightly sweeter, tangier profile than standard white vinegar.

Recommended Tools

  • Wide Mouth Mason Jars (Quart size is standard; wide mouth makes packing spears much easier)
  • Small Saucepan (For boiling the brine)
  • Chef's Knife (For cutting uniform spears or chips)

Ingredients

  • 12 Kirby cucumbers (or enough to fill your jars tightly)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced or smashed
  • 2 tablespoons mustard seeds
  • 1 large sprig fresh dill (or 1 tbsp dried dill weed)
  • 2 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 1¼ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon pickling salt or kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons sugar

Directions

  1. Prep Veggies: Wash the cucumbers thoroughly. Slice them into spears (quarters lengthwise) or chips (coins), depending on your preference.
  2. Pack: Tightly pack the cucumbers into heat-safe glass jars (1 large quart jar or 2 pint jars). Squeeze them in tight so they don't float. Add the garlic, mustard seeds, and fresh dill into the gaps.
  3. Make Brine: In a small pot, combine the apple cider vinegar, water, salt, and sugar. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring until the salt and sugar are completely dissolved.
  4. Pour: Pour the hot brine carefully over the cucumbers, filling the jar to within ½ inch of the rim.
  5. Cool & Store: Seal the jars with lids. Let them sit on the counter to cool to room temperature (about 1 hour), then transfer to the refrigerator.
  6. Wait: Refrigerate for at least 48 hours before opening to allow the flavors to penetrate the cucumbers. They will keep for up to 2 weeks.

Notes

  • Cucumber Choice: Use Kirby or Persian cucumbers. Do not use standard slicing cucumbers (the dark green shiny ones) from the grocery store; their skins are waxed and thick, which prevents the brine from soaking in.
  • Spicy Option: Add 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes or a sliced fresh jalapeño to the jar for a spicy kick.
  • Not Shelf Stable: These are refrigerator pickles. They are not shelf-stable and must be kept in the fridge.
