Jalapeno Cream Gravy

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Southern American

This isn't your average country gravy. By roasting the jalapenos and garlic under a broiler first, we infuse the cream base with a deep, smoky heat. It’s the ultimate topping for chicken fried steak, steak fingers, or even a savory breakfast biscuit.

Ingredients

  • 2 large jalapeños, halved lengthwise (stems and seeds removed)
  • 2 cloves garlic, skin on
  • 2 tablespoons bacon grease (or vegetable oil)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1½ cups whole milk
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Roast: Place the halved jalapeños (skin-side up) and the garlic cloves on a baking sheet. Place under the broiler for about 5 minutes, or until the jalapeño skins are blackened and blistered.
  2. Prep Aromatics: Remove from the oven. Let them cool slightly, then peel off the blackened jalapeño skins. Dice the peppers finely. Squeeze the roasted garlic out of its skin and mince into a paste.
  3. Make the Roux: In a medium skillet, heat the bacon grease over medium-low heat. Whisk in the flour to form a paste (roux). Cook, whisking constantly, for about 1 minute until fragrant and lightly golden.
  4. Thicken: Slowly pour in the milk a little at a time, whisking vigorously to incorporate. Continue to cook and stir for 2–4 minutes until the gravy thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  5. Season: Stir in the diced jalapeños, roasted garlic, and cumin. Season with salt and a generous amount of black pepper.
  6. Adjust & Serve: If the gravy is too thick, whisk in an extra splash of milk. Serve hot over your favorite protein or biscuits.

Notes

  • Bacon Grease: Using bacon grease adds a savory, smoky layer that oil cannot replicate. If you have it, use it!
  • Heat Level: For more heat, keep the seeds from one of the roasted jalapeños and mix them into the final gravy.
  • Roasting Tip: Leaving the garlic in its skin while broiling prevents it from burning and becoming bitter; it steams inside the skin instead.
