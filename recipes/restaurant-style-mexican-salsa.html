Restaurant Style Mexican Salsa

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Mexican

The secret to that addictive salsa served at your favorite Mexican restaurant is usually a combination of canned tomatoes (for texture and consistency) and fresh aromatics (onion, cilantro, lime). This 5-minute blender recipe hits all the right notes: bright, savory, and just spicy enough.

Ingredients

The Base

  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1–2 fresh Roma tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 medium white onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1–2 jalapeños, stemmed (keep seeds for heat, remove for mild)

Seasoning & Acid

  • ½ cup fresh cilantro leaves (stems are fine too!)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin (optional)
  • ½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Directions

  1. Combine: In a food processor or blender, add the canned tomatoes, fresh Roma tomatoes, onion, garlic, and jalapeños.
  2. Season: Add the cilantro, fresh lime juice, salt, and cumin (if using).
  3. Pulse: Pulse the machine 5–10 times. Do not just run it on high. You want to chop the ingredients into a salsa consistency, not purée them into a soup. Stop when it reaches your desired texture.
  4. Adjust: Taste a spoonful with a tortilla chip (chips are salty, so always taste with one). Add more salt, lime, or chili if needed.
  5. Rest: Transfer to a bowl or jar. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld and the onion bite to mellow out.

Notes

  • Roasted Variation: For a smokier "Salsa Tatemada," roast the fresh tomatoes, onion, garlic, and jalapeños under a broiler for 5 minutes until charred before adding them to the blender.
  • Storage: This salsa keeps well in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. It actually tastes better on day 2!
  • Heat Level: For mild salsa, use 1 jalapeño and scrape out the white membrane and seeds. For hot salsa, use 2 serrano peppers instead of jalapeños.
