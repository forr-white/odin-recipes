Golden Sweet Cornbread

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

bread • Southern American

There is a great debate in the South: sweet vs. savory cornbread. This recipe falls firmly in the "sweet" camp. It is cake-like, moist, and tender—the perfect contrast to spicy chili or salty collard greens. It is almost like a dessert, but socially acceptable to eat with dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • ⅔ cup white sugar
  • 3½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil (or melted butter)
  • 1 large egg

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Lightly grease a 9-inch round cake pan or a cast-iron skillet with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Dry Mix: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder, and salt until evenly combined and no lumps remain.
  3. Wet Mix: Create a small well in the center of the flour mixture. Pour in the milk, vegetable oil, and egg.
  4. Combine: Whisk gently until just combined. It is okay if there are a few small lumps; do not overmix, or the bread will be tough.
  5. Bake: Pour the batter into your prepared pan. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes. The top should be golden brown, and a toothpick inserted into the center should come out clean.
  6. Cool: Let the cornbread cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before slicing. This helps it set and prevents it from crumbling when cut.

Notes

  • Crispy Edges: For a restaurant-style crust, put the butter/oil in your cast iron skillet and place it in the preheating oven for 5 minutes. Pour the batter into the sizzling hot pan before baking.
  • Spicy Variation: Add ½ cup of shredded cheddar cheese and ¼ cup of diced jalapeños (fresh or pickled) to the batter for a savory kick.
  • Honey Butter: This cornbread is best served warm with a mix of softened butter and honey.
  • Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
