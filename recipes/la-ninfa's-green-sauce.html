La Ninfa's Green Sauce

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Mexican

Famous across Texas and beyond, Ninfa's Green Sauce is more than just a salsa—it's an institution. Unlike standard tomatillo salsas, this version uses ripe avocados and sour cream to create a luxuriously smooth, dip-able texture. It is the perfect balance of tangy tomatillos and creamy fats.

  • Chef's Knife (For prepping the avocados and peppers)

Ingredients

  • 3 medium-sized green tomatoes, coarsely chopped
  • 4 tomatillos, husks removed and chopped
  • 1 to 2 jalapeños, stemmed and coarsely chopped (keep seeds for heat)
  • 3 small garlic cloves, peeled
  • 3 medium-sized ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 4 sprigs fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1½ cups sour cream

Directions

  1. Simmer: Combine the chopped green tomatoes, tomatillos, jalapeños, and garlic in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil over medium heat (the tomatoes will release enough liquid to prevent sticking). Reduce heat and simmer for 10–15 minutes until the vegetables are soft.
  2. Cool: Remove the saucepan from the heat. Let the mixture cool for at least 15–20 minutes. Note: Blending hot liquids with dairy can cause the sour cream to curdle.
  3. Blend: Transfer the cooked mixture (including the liquid in the pan) into a blender or food processor. Add the avocados, cilantro, and salt. Blend until completely smooth and vibrant green.
  4. Incorporate Dairy: Pour the blended mixture into a large bowl. Gently stir in the sour cream until fully incorporated and the color is uniform.
  5. Chill: For the best flavor, refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving. Serve chilled with salty tortilla chips.

Notes

  • Substitutions: If you cannot find true green tomatoes, you can substitute them with 6 additional tomatillos. The flavor will be slightly tangier but still delicious.
  • Heat Level: For a milder sauce, remove the seeds and white pith from the jalapeños before simmering.
  • Storage: This sauce keeps well in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. The lime/acid in the tomatillos helps keep the avocado from browning.
