"Mega-Pot" Spinach Artichoke Nacho Dip

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

appetizers • Tex-Mex Fusion • Party Food

This is not your standard white restaurant dip—this is "Stadium Comfort" in a bowl. Designed for massive parties on a budget, this recipe utilizes a commercial can of Rico's Gourmet Nacho Cheese as the base. By fortifying that salty, movie-theater liquid gold with real cream cheese, stringy Monterey Jack, and savory vegetables, we create a hybrid dip that yields nearly 2 gallons of savory perfection.

  • 7-Quart Slow Cooker (This recipe fills a 6-quart pot to the very brim; 7-quart or Roaster Oven recommended)
  • Large Colander (Essential for rinsing the brine off the artichokes)
  • Sturdy Ladle (For serving)

Ingredients

The "Liquid Gold" Base

  • 1 can (124 oz) Rico’s Gourmet Nacho Cheese Sauce
  • 2 blocks (8 oz each) cream cheese, cubed
  • 1 lb Monterey Jack cheese, shredded

The Mix-Ins

  • 5 cans (14.65 oz each) quartered artichoke hearts
  • 1 bag (10 oz) frozen chopped spinach, thawed
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper (optional)

For Serving

  • 6–7 lbs tortilla chips (Choose "Restaurant Style" or sturdy chips)

Directions

  1. Prep the Veggies (Crucial Salt Control): Thaw the frozen spinach and squeeze it bone dry with a towel. Fluff it with a fork. Open all 5 cans of artichokes, dump them into a colander, and rinse thoroughly with cold water.
    Note: Rico's cheese is salty; if you don't rinse the brine off the artichokes, the dip will be too salty.
  2. Layer the Pot: This recipe creates a high volume, so layer it carefully to prevent overflow. Pour about half of the Rico’s Cheese into the slow cooker. Add the cubed cream cheese, shredded Monterey Jack, spinach, and chopped artichokes in the middle. Pour the remaining Rico’s Cheese over the top.
  3. The Melt: Cover and cook on HIGH for 1.5 to 2 hours.
  4. Do Not Stir Yet: Do not stir for the first hour. Let the heat penetrate and the vegetables wilt down slightly to create room in the pot.
  5. The Blend: After 1.5 hours, remove the lid. Stir very slowly and carefully to blend the cream cheese and veggies into the yellow sauce.
  6. Serve: Once uniform and hot, turn the slow cooker to LOW or WARM.

Notes

  • Wet Spinach Warning: Frozen spinach is full of water. You must wring it out in a clean towel until it stops dripping. If you skip this step, the excess water will seep out during cooking and make your cheese sauce soupy and thin.
  • Transport Safety: If your slow cooker has a latching lid, only latch it for transport. Never cook with the lid latched, as pressure can build up.
  • The "Skin" Factor: Nacho cheese develops a film on top quickly. Stir the dip every 20–30 minutes during the party to keep it looking fresh.
  • Color: Because the base is yellow/orange nacho cheese, the final dip will have a golden hue rather than the traditional white color. The flavor is fantastic, but the look is different!
