Lucky Smoked Ham & Black-Eyed Peas

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

stew • Southern American

In the American South, eating black-eyed peas on New Year's Day is said to bring a year of good luck and prosperity. This recipe relies on a slow simmer with a smoked ham hock to create a rich "pot liquor"—the flavorful broth that develops as the bean starches and collagen from the meat meld together.

Ingredients

The Base

  • 1 lb dried black-eyed peas
  • 1 smoked ham hock
  • 1 cup diced smoked ham (optional, for extra meatiness)
  • 6 cups low-sodium chicken stock
  • 2 cups water

Aromatics & Spices

  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions

  1. The Soak: Place beans in a large bowl. Sort through them to remove any small stones or shriveled beans. Cover with water by 3 inches. Let soak overnight (6–8 hours), then drain and rinse.
  2. Sear Meat: In a Dutch oven, heat a splash of oil over medium-high heat. Sear the ham hock on all sides until browned. This "wakes up" the smoky flavor. Remove hock and set aside.
  3. Sauté: In the same pot, add onion, bell pepper, and celery. Sauté for 5–7 minutes until softened. Add garlic, thyme, paprika, and red pepper flakes; cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Combine: Add the stock, water, bay leaf, seared ham hock, and drained beans. Bring to a boil, then immediately reduce heat to low.
  5. Simmer: Cover with the lid slightly cracked. Simmer gently for 1.5 to 2 hours. The beans are ready when they are tender and creamy, but have not yet burst into mush.
  6. Finish: Remove the hock. Shred any meat off the bone and return it to the pot. Stir in the optional diced ham. Taste now and season with salt and pepper (adding salt too early can toughen bean skins).

Notes

  • Quick Soak: If you're short on time, bring beans and water to a boil for 2 minutes. Turn off heat, cover tightly, and let sit for 1 hour. Drain and proceed to Step 2.
  • Pot Liquor: Don't toss the broth! It is packed with nutrients and flavor. Serve the peas with a side of cornbread to soak it up.
  • Acid: A teaspoon of apple cider vinegar stirred in at the very end cuts through the richness of the ham and brightens the whole dish.
