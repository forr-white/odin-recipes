Lucky Smoked Ham & Black-Eyed Peas

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

stew • Southern American

Ingredients

  • 1 lb dried black-eyed peas (sorted and rinsed)
  • 1 smoked ham hock
  • 1 cup diced smoked ham (optional)
  • 6 cups chicken stock (low sodium)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • to taste salt & black pepper

Directions

  1. The Soak: Place beans in a large pot and cover with water by 3 inches. Let soak overnight (6-8 hours), then drain and rinse. (See Notes for "Quick Soak" method).
  2. Sear the Meat: In a heavy soup pot or Dutch oven, heat a splash of oil over medium-high heat. Sear the ham hock on all sides until browned to wake up the flavor, then set aside.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, add the onion, bell pepper, and celery. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until soft. Add garlic, thyme, paprika, and red pepper flakes; cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Combine & Simmer: Add the chicken stock, water, bay leaf, seared ham hock, and drained beans. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover with the lid slightly cracked.
  5. The Wait: Simmer gently for 1.5 to 2 hours. Check at the 1-hour mark to ensure there is enough liquid. The beans are done when creamy and tender, but not mushy.
  6. Finish: Remove the hock, shred the meat off the bone, and return meat to the pot. Stir in the diced ham (if using). Taste and season with salt and pepper now (not earlier).

Notes

  • Quick Soak Method: Forgot to soak overnight? Boil beans in water for 2 minutes, turn off heat, cover tightly, and let sit for 1 hour. Drain and rinse before cooking.
  • Creamy Texture: For a thicker "stew" consistency, remove a ladle full of beans at the end, mash them with a fork, and stir them back into the pot.
  • The Acid Trick: Right before serving, stir in 1 teaspoon of Apple Cider Vinegar. It brightens the heavy, smoky flavor instantly.
  • Storage: Flavors improve the next day. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days or freeze for up to 3 months.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 1.5 cups (approx. 8 servings total)

Fat: 8 g

Carbs: 35 g
