Mongolian Beef

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 lb Flank steak, sliced
  • Vegetable Oil
  • 1/4 cup Cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • Pinch of salt
  • Bunch of green onions, cut into 2 inch slices
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 red Fresno chiles, thinly sliced
  • 2 teaspoons gochugaru
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup dark soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Splash rice vinegar
  • Thai Basil
  • 5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • Green onions, thinly sliced, for serving
  • Broccoli florets, optional

Directions

  1. Prepare 2 cups of Rice
  2. Slice the flank steak into 1/2 inch slices. Add slices to a medium bowl.
  3. Season a wok with 3-4 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium high heat, swirling
    occasionally, until the oil begins to smoke. Discard that oil and wipe it out with a paper towel.
  4. Add another 3-4 tablespoons of oil and heat again over medium high heat.
  5. While the oil heats, toss the beef together with cornstarch, ground white pepper, and a pinch of
    salt
  6. Once the oil is hot, shake off the excess starch from the beef and fry each piece of beef in
    batches for 2-3 minutes per side. Place to the side as the finish browning.
  7. Add in the green onions and sear for 30 seconds, toss and sear again until well browned.
  8. Add in ginger, chiles, and gochugaru. Stir fry for 45 seconds.
  9. Add brown sugar, stir fry that in then add dark soy sauce, regular soy sauce, water, and a splash
    ofrice vinegar. Let boil and reduce for 3 minutes.
  10. Add in the beef, toss and stir to coat thoroughly
  11. Turn off the heat and add in thai basil and finely chopped garlic. Stir to combine.
  12. Into a bowl, heavily add the steamed rice.
  13. Top the rice with the cooked beef with extra sauce.
  14. Top with thinly sliced green onions with a side of broccoli and serve.
