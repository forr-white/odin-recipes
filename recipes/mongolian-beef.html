Mongolian Beef

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Chinese American

This recipe delivers that signature restaurant-quality texture: beef that is crispy on the edges but tender inside, coated in a glossy, savory-sweet sauce. This version gets a unique aromatic lift from the addition of Thai basil and a spicy kick from Gochugaru and fresh chiles.

Ingredients

The Beef & Coating

  • 1 lb Flank steak, sliced into ½-inch strips
  • ¼ cup Cornstarch
  • ½ teaspoon ground white pepper
  • Pinch of salt
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

The Sauce Base

  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup dark soy sauce (for color)
  • ¼ cup regular soy sauce (for salt)
  • ¼ cup water
  • Splash of rice vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons Gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)

Aromatics & Finish

  • 1 bunch green onions, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 red Fresno chiles, thinly sliced
  • 5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • Handful of Thai Basil leaves

Serving

  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • Steamed broccoli florets (optional)
  • Extra thinly sliced green onions

Directions

  1. Prep Beef: Slice the flank steak against the grain into ½-inch thick strips. In a medium bowl, toss the beef with the cornstarch, white pepper, and a pinch of salt until every piece is evenly coated and dry.
  2. Heat Oil: Heat about ½ cup of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. (Tip: If using a carbon steel wok, swirl oil to coat the sides first).
  3. Flash Fry: Once the oil is shimmering hot, shake excess starch off the beef. Fry the beef in batches (do not overcrowd) for 2–3 minutes per side until crispy and browned. Remove beef and set aside on a plate.
  4. Sear Aromatics: Pour out most of the oil, leaving about 1 tablespoon in the wok. Add the cut green onions (2-inch pieces) and sear for 30 seconds until blistered. Add the chopped ginger, sliced Fresno chiles, and Gochugaru. Stir-fry for 45 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Simmer Sauce: Add the brown sugar, dark soy sauce, regular soy sauce, water, and rice vinegar. Bring to a boil and let it bubble for about 3 minutes until the sugar dissolves and the sauce thickens slightly.
  6. Combine: Toss the fried beef back into the wok. Stir vigorously to coat every piece in the sticky sauce.
  7. Finish: Turn off the heat. Immediately stir in the Thai basil and the raw chopped garlic. (Adding the garlic off the heat keeps the flavor punchy and fresh).
  8. Serve: Scoop steamed rice into bowls. Top with the beef and plenty of sauce. Garnish with fresh green onions and serve with broccoli on the side.

Notes

  • Slicing Tip: Freezing the flank steak for 20 minutes before slicing makes it much easier to cut thin, uniform strips. Always cut perpendicular to the muscle fibers (against the grain) for tenderness.
  • Gochugaru Substitute: If you don't have Korean chili flakes, you can use 1 teaspoon of standard red pepper flakes, though Gochugaru offers a distinct smoky sweetness.
  • Dark Soy Sauce: This ingredient is thicker and less salty than regular soy sauce; it is primarily used to give the dish its signature deep mahogany color.
