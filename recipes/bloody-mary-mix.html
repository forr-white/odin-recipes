Bloody Mary Mix

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • American

Store-bought mixes simply can't compare to fresh, homemade Bloody Mary Mix. This recipe hits all the right notes: savory tomato, tangy Worcestershire, and a kick of horseradish and spice. It keeps well in the fridge, making it perfect for hosting brunch or curing a holiday hangover.

Ingredients

  • 1 (48-ounce) can tomato juice (about 6 cups)
  • 3 tablespoons prepared creamy hot horseradish
  • 3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons celery salt
  • 3 teaspoons garlic salt
  • Tabasco sauce (10–15 shakes, or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Directions

  1. Combine: In a large pitcher, combine the tomato juice, horseradish, Worcestershire sauce, celery salt, garlic salt, and black pepper.
  2. Season: Season with 10–15 shakes of Tabasco sauce, or adjust to your preferred spice level. Stir vigorously to combine.
  3. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Serve: Stir well before pouring. To make a cocktail, pour over ice and add 1.5 to 2 ounces of vodka per glass.

Notes

  • Let it sit: While you can drink this immediately, it tastes much better after sitting in the fridge for an hour or even overnight.
  • To make a drink: Fill a tall glass with ice. Add 2 oz of vodka (or tequila for a Bloody Maria). Top with the mix and stir.
  • Garnish Ideas: Go crazy with toppings! Celery stalks, lemon/lime wedges, olives, pickled okra, or even a slice of crispy bacon.
  • Storage: Keep refrigerated in a sealed pitcher or jar for up to 1 week. Shake or stir before using as spices will settle.
