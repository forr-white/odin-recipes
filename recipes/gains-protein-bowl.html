Gains Protein Bowl

dinner • Mexican Fusion

A bold, high-protein Mexican-fusion bowl loaded with seasoned beef, rice, beans, corn, and melty cheese. Perfect for meal prep, post-workout meals, or a weeknight dinner that hits every craving.

Ingredients

Beef & Sauce

  • 3 lb ground beef (90/10 or leaner)
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (about 1 ounce)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp salt (or to taste)
  • ¾ cup taco sauce
  • 2 cups pico de gallo, divided
  • ½ cup cream cheese (softened)
  • ½ cup light sour cream

Rice & Mix-Ins

  • 2 cups dry long-grain rice (white, jasmine, or brown)
  • 1 can corn, drained
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 bag Mexican 4-cheese blend

Optional Finishing Touches

  • Fresh lime wedges
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Extra pico de gallo for topping

Directions

  1. Cook the rice: Prepare 2 cups of rice according to package directions. Fluff with a fork and set aside.
  2. Brown & season the beef: Add the ground beef to a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook until mostly browned. Drain excess grease if needed. Add taco seasoning, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt. Cook 2–3 minutes to bloom the spices.
  3. Create the creamy taco mixture: Lower heat to medium-low. Stir in the taco sauce, cream cheese, and light sour cream. Mix until smooth and creamy. Fold in **half of the pico de gallo**.
  4. Warm the beans and corn: In a separate pan, warm the corn and black beans for 3–5 minutes. Season lightly if desired.
  5. Assemble your bowls: Layer rice → beans & corn → creamy taco beef → shredded cheese. Let the cheese melt slightly.
  6. Finish & serve: Top with more pico de gallo, lime juice, or cilantro. Serve hot.

Notes

  • For spicier bowls, add jalapeños or a pinch of cayenne.
  • Swap in ground turkey or chicken for a lighter option.
  • Stir cheese directly into the hot beef mixture for gooier, fully incorporated bowls.
  • Great for meal prep — reheats well with a splash of water.
  • Brown rice or cauliflower rice both work as lower-carb alternatives.

Nutrition
