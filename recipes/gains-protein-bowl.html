Gains Protein Bowl

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican Fusion

A bold, high-protein Mexican-fusion bowl loaded with seasoned beef, rice, beans, corn, and melty cheese. It gets its name because it is packed with protein (the "gains"). It is the perfect recipe for meal prep, post-workout meals, or a weeknight dinner that hits every craving.

Ingredients

Beef & Sauce

  • 3 lb ground beef (90/10 or leaner)
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (about 1 oz)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp salt (or to taste)
  • ¾ cup taco sauce
  • 2 cups pico de gallo, divided
  • ½ cup cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup light sour cream

Rice & Mix-Ins

  • 2 cups dry long-grain rice (white, jasmine, or brown)
  • 1 can corn, drained
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 bag (8 oz) Mexican 4-cheese blend

Optional Finishing Touches

  • Fresh lime wedges
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Extra pico de gallo for topping

Directions

  1. Cook Rice: Prepare 2 cups of dry rice according to package directions (or use a rice cooker). Fluff with a fork and set aside.
  2. Brown Beef: Add the ground beef to a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook until mostly browned, breaking it up with a spoon. Drain excess grease if needed.
  3. Season: Add the taco seasoning packet, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt. Stir and cook for 2–3 minutes to bloom the spices.
  4. Make Sauce: Lower heat to medium-low. Stir in the taco sauce, softened cream cheese, and light sour cream directly into the beef. Mix until smooth and creamy. Fold in half of the pico de gallo.
  5. Warm Veggies: In a separate pan (or microwave), warm the drained corn and black beans for 3–5 minutes.
  6. Assemble: Build your bowls by layering rice first, then the bean/corn mixture, followed by a generous scoop of the creamy taco beef. Top immediately with shredded cheese so it melts from the residual heat.
  7. Serve: Top with the remaining pico de gallo, a squeeze of fresh lime juice, and cilantro.

Notes

  • Spicier: Add diced jalapeños to the beef mixture or a pinch of cayenne pepper with the spices.
  • Lighter Option: Swap the beef for ground turkey or chicken. The creamy sauce keeps lean meats moist.
  • One Pot Style: For a messier but tastier version, stir the cheese directly into the hot beef mixture in the skillet before serving.
  • Carb Swap: Brown rice or cauliflower rice work perfectly as lower-glycemic alternatives.
