Chicken Tikka Masala

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Indian

This is one of the most popular curries in the world for a reason. Chunks of tender chicken are marinated in spiced yogurt, seared until charred, and then finished in a luxurious, creamy tomato sauce packed with warming spices like garam masala, cumin, and coriander. It rivals anything you can get at a restaurant.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade

  • 1 cup plain yogurt or Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1.5 teaspoons cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon red chili powder (not cayenne pepper)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons grated fresh ginger
  • 2 teaspoons finely minced garlic
  • 1.5 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into small bite-sized pieces

For the Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large sweet onion, finely diced
  • 2 teaspoons grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 (8 oz) can tomato sauce (unseasoned)
  • 1¼ cups heavy cream
  • ¼ cup water (only if needed to thin)

For Serving

  • Chopped cilantro, for garnish
  • Naan bread
  • Cooked Basmati rice

Directions

  1. Marinate: In a bowl, whisk together all the ingredients for the marinade. Add the chicken and toss to coat evenly. Let marinate for at least 1 hour (ideally overnight in the fridge for the most tender results).
  2. Sear Chicken: Warm oil in a large skillet (preferably cast iron) over medium-high heat. Once shimmering, add chicken pieces in batches, making sure not to crowd the pan. Let them fry, untouched, for 1 minute to develop a crust, then sauté until browned, about 5 more minutes. (The chicken does not need to be cooked through yet). Set aside and keep warm.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Melt the butter in the same pan. Sauté the onions until soft (about 3 minutes) while scraping up any browned bits stuck on the bottom of the pan. Add ginger and garlic; sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Bloom Spices: Add in the brown sugar, coriander, cumin, paprika, garam masala, and salt. Stir constantly for about 15 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Simmer Base: Pour in the tomato sauce; let simmer gently for about 3–5 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens and becomes deeper in color.
  6. Add Cream: Stir in the heavy cream until fully incorporated and smooth.
  7. Finish Chicken: Add the seared chicken along with its juices back to the pan. Cook for an additional 8–10 minutes until chicken is cooked through and the sauce is thick and bubbling. Pour in the water to thin out the sauce only if it becomes too thick.
  8. Serve: Serve hot over cooked Basmati rice with Naan bread and garnish with chopped cilantro.

Notes

  • Marinating Time: Don't rush the marinade! The yogurt enzymes tenderize the chicken. Overnight is best, but even 30 minutes is better than nothing.
  • Chili Powder: Indian Red Chili Powder is different from American Chili Powder (which usually contains cumin and garlic). If you can't find Indian chili powder, use half the amount of cayenne pepper mixed with sweet paprika.
  • Chicken Thighs: For even juicier meat, swap the chicken breasts for boneless, skinless chicken thighs.
  • Vegetarian Option: This sauce is incredible with paneer cheese or firm tofu instead of chicken.
