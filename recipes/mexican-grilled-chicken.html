Mexican Grilled Chicken

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Mexican

This isn't your average lime-and-cilantro marinade. The secret weapon here is **pomegranate or tart cherry juice**. The natural acidity and tannins in these juices act as a powerful meat tenderizer while providing a deep, complex base for the smoky paprika and cumin. It results in a beautifully charred crust and an incredibly juicy interior.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil or avocado oil
  • 3 tablespoons pomegranate juice (unsweetened) or tart cherry juice
  • 3 large garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¾ teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt

Directions

  1. Prep Chicken: If using chicken breasts, place them between two sheets of plastic wrap and pound with a meat mallet to an even ½-inch thickness. This ensures they cook at the same rate and stay juicy.
  2. Marinate: In a gallon-sized resealable bag, combine the oil, juice, garlic, cumin, paprika, chili powder, and salt. Mix well. Add the chicken, seal the bag while removing excess air, and massage to coat. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or up to 12 hours.
  3. Heat Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (approx. 400°F–450°F).
  4. Oil the Grates: Dip a folded paper towel in a little oil. Using tongs, rub the oiled towel over the grill grates. This prevents sticking and creates better sear marks.
  5. Grill: Place chicken on the grill and close the lid. Cook for 5–7 minutes. Flip the chicken only when it releases easily from the grates. Cook the second side for another 5–7 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  6. Rest: Transfer the chicken to a clean platter and tent loosely with foil. Let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute so the meat stays moist.

Notes

  • Juice Substitution: If you can't find pomegranate or cherry juice, fresh orange juice is a good secondary substitute, though the flavor will be more citrus-forward.
  • Thighs vs. Breasts: If using boneless chicken thighs, they are much more forgiving. You can cook them to 175°F for a more tender, "fall-apart" texture.
  • Serving Idea: Slice and serve in warm corn tortillas with our Homemade Guacamole and fresh cilantro.
