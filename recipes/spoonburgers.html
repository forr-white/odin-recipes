Spoonburgers

dinner • American

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (85/15 or 90/10)
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped green pepper
  • 1–2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup finely grated carrot
  • 1 (8 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (or 2 tsp maple syrup)
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp yellow or Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: pinch red pepper flakes or 1–2 tsp hot sauce
  • To serve: toasted buns, rice, baked potatoes, or garlic toast

Directions

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until well-browned with some crispy bits. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Add the chopped onion, green pepper, garlic, and grated carrot. Cook for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables soften and begin to caramelize.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1–2 minutes to deepen the flavor and cook off the raw taste.
  4. Add the tomato sauce, ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar (or maple syrup), Worcestershire sauce, mustard, smoked paprika, and any optional red pepper flakes or hot sauce. Stir well to combine and taste for salt and pepper.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is thick, rich, and spoonable.
  6. Serve hot, spooned over toasted buns, rice, baked potatoes, or garlic toast.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 1 portion (about 1 cup meat mixture)

Calories: 320

Fat: 18 g

Carbs: 17 g

Protein: 22 g
