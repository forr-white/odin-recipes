Spoonburgers

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • American

Forget the canned stuff. A "Spoonburger" is a homemade take on the classic Sloppy Joe, but with much more depth of flavor. We build a savory base with onions, peppers, and a secret ingredient—finely grated carrots—which add natural sweetness to balance the tangy vinegar and tomato sauce. It's messy, delicious, and perfect for a weeknight meal.

Ingredients

The Meat & Veggie Base

  • 1 lb ground beef (85/15 is flavorful, but 90/10 works for a leaner version)
  • ½ cup onion, chopped
  • ½ cup green bell pepper, chopped
  • ½ cup carrot, peeled and finely grated
  • 1–2 cloves garlic, minced

The Sauce

  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (or 2 tsp maple syrup)
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp yellow or Dijon mustard
  • ¼ tsp smoked paprika
  • Optional: Pinch of red pepper flakes or hot sauce for heat
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

To Serve

  • Hamburger buns (toasted)

Directions

  1. Brown Beef: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it up with a wooden spoon, until well-browned with some crispy bits. Season lightly with salt and pepper. (Tip: If there is excessive grease in the pan, drain most of it off now, leaving about 1 tablespoon).
  2. Sauté Veggies: Add the chopped onion, green pepper, minced garlic, and grated carrot to the beef. Cook for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables soften and the onions become translucent.
  3. Toast Paste: Stir in the tomato paste. Cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly. This "toasts" the paste, removing the raw metallic taste and deepening the flavor.
  4. Simmer: Add the tomato sauce, ketchup, vinegar, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, mustard, smoked paprika, and optional hot sauce. Stir well to combine.
  5. Thicken: Reduce heat to Low. Simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally. You want the mixture to reduce until it is thick, rich, and "spoonable" (not watery).
  6. Serve: Taste and adjust salt and pepper if needed. Spoon hot mixture onto toasted buns.

Notes

  • The Bun Matters: Because the filling is wet, you must toast your buns. Butter the cut sides and toast them in a skillet or broiler until golden. This prevents the bread from getting soggy immediately.
  • Serving Variations: While a bun is classic, this mixture is also delicious served over a baked potato, rice, or on thick slices of garlic toast.
  • Make Ahead: Like chili, this tastes even better the next day. It keeps in the fridge for 3–4 days and freezes perfectly.
