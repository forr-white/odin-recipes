Greek Chicken Meatballs & Lemon Orzo

Submitted by mychillrecipes.com & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • Greek

This is a complete, well-balanced dinner made in a single skillet. The chicken meatballs are kept moist with grated onion and feta, while the orzo cooks directly in the broth, absorbing all the savory flavors and releasing starch to create a naturally creamy, lemon-scented sauce. It’s bright, comforting, and packed with Mediterranean flavors.

Ingredients

The Meatballs

  • 1 lb ground chicken
  • ½ cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup yellow onion, grated (squeeze out excess liquid)
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1¼ tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)

The Orzo & Broth

  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for searing)
  • 1½ cups dry orzo pasta
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Finishing

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp additional lemon zest
  • 3 cups baby spinach, lightly packed
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Extra crumbled feta and lemon wedges for serving

Directions

  1. Mix Meatballs: In a large bowl, combine the ground chicken, panko, egg, minced garlic, grated onion, lemon zest, dill, oregano, salt, pepper, red pepper flakes, and the ½ cup of feta. Mix gently with your hands until just combined. Do not overwork the meat.
  2. Form: Scoop and roll the mixture into golf-ball-sized meatballs (you should get about 16). Tip: Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking. Chill them in the refrigerator for 10 minutes to firm up.
  3. Sear: Heat the olive oil in a large deep skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the meatballs in batches until browned on two sides, about 4 to 5 minutes total. Transfer them to a plate (they will finish cooking later).
  4. Toast Orzo: Reduce heat to medium. In the same skillet (don't wipe it out!), add the remaining minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds. Stir in the dry orzo and toast for 1 minute, coating it in the flavorful pan drippings.
  5. Simmer: Pour in the chicken broth, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon.
  6. Cook Together: Nestle the browned meatballs back into the skillet with the orzo. Cover and simmer on medium-low heat. Stir occasionally to keep the orzo from sticking to the bottom. Cook until the orzo is al dente and meatballs register 165°F (74°C), about 10 to 12 minutes.
  7. Finish: Turn off the heat. Stir in the butter, lemon juice, and extra lemon zest until the sauce looks glossy. Fold in the spinach until wilted, then add the parsley.
  8. Serve: Sprinkle extra feta over the top. Cover and let it rest for 2 to 3 minutes to thicken slightly. Serve warm with lemon wedges.

Notes

  • Grated Onion: Grating the onion keeps the meatballs incredibly moist, but be sure to grate it directly into the bowl or catch the juices to include them.
  • Sticking: Orzo loves to stick to the bottom of the pan. Make sure to stir gently around the meatballs a few times during the covered simmering stage.
  • Consistency: If the orzo absorbs too much liquid while sitting, splash in a little extra warm broth or water to loosen the sauce before serving.
