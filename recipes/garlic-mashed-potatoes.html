Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sides • American

The secret to these mashed potatoes isn't just the butter—it's the garlic technique. Instead of roasting garlic separately or adding raw garlic (which can be harsh), we boil the cloves right along with the potatoes. This mellows the garlic into a sweet, savory flavor that mashes seamlessly into the creamy Yukon Golds.

Ingredients

  • 5 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cubed into 1-inch pieces
  • 10 whole garlic cloves, peeled
  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • ¼–½ cup whole milk (warmed)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives or parsley for garnish (optional)

Directions

  1. Boil: Place the cubed potatoes and the whole peeled garlic cloves in a large pot. Cover with cold water by at least an inch. Add a generous pinch of salt. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  2. Cook: Reduce heat slightly and boil until the potatoes are fork-tender, about 15–20 minutes. (The garlic will be soft and ready to mash by then too).
  3. Drain & Dry: Drain the potatoes and garlic in a colander. Return them to the hot pot for 1 minute on low heat to steam off excess moisture. This ensures the potatoes absorb the butter instead of being watery.
  4. Rice: Working in batches, place the potatoes and garlic cloves into a potato ricer and squeeze them into a large bowl (or back into the pot). The ricer breaks down the garlic and mixes it perfectly with the potato.
  5. Mash: Gently fold in the butter and warm milk. Stir until just combined and creamy. Do not overmix, or they will become gluey.
  6. Season: Taste and add salt and pepper as desired. Serve hot topped with an extra pat of butter.

Notes

  • Warm the Dairy: Adding cold milk to hot potatoes cools them down and can make the texture seizing/gummy. Heat your milk and butter in the microwave for 30 seconds before adding.
  • Yukon Gold vs. Russet: Yukon Golds are naturally creamy and buttery. Russets are starchier and fluffier. You can use a 50/50 mix for the best of both worlds.
  • The Ricer: If you don't have a ricer, use a hand masher, but mash the garlic cloves with a fork first to ensure you don't get large chunks of garlic. Avoid using a hand mixer or blender, which turns potatoes into glue.
