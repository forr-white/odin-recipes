Buttery Flour Tortillas

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

bread • Mexican

Once you taste a fresh, warm homemade tortilla, you will never want to go back to the store-bought variety. These tortillas are soft, pillowy, and rich with buttery flavor. They are perfect for tacos, burritos, or just slathered with a little extra butter right off the pan.

Recommended Tools

  • Comal or Cast Iron Skillet (Essential for even heat and those signature brown spots)
  • Rolling Pin (For getting the tortillas thin and even)
  • Mixing Bowl (Large enough to knead the dough comfortably)
  • Tortilla Warmer (Keeps them soft and pliable while you cook the rest)

Ingredients

  • 3 cups All-Purpose Flour
  • 1 Tablespoon Baking Powder
  • 2 Teaspoons Salt
  • ½ Cup Unsalted Butter
  • 1 Cup Water

Directions

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Prepare Liquids: Melt the butter. Combine the melted butter with the warm water and stir.
  3. Combine: Pour the butter/water mixture into the dry ingredients. Mix until combined, then knead gently until you form a smooth, soft ball of dough.
  4. Rest: Cover the dough ball with plastic wrap or a damp towel and let it rest for 10–15 minutes. (This relaxes the gluten, making it easier to roll).
  5. Portion: Divide the dough into 8–10 even-sized pieces and roll them into small balls.
  6. Second Rest: Transfer the dough balls back into the bowl or keep them on the counter, covered, for another 10–15 minutes.
  7. Roll: On a lightly floured work surface, use a rolling pin to roll out each ball until you have a thin, 10–12 inch round.
  8. Cook: Preheat a comal, cast iron skillet, or non-stick pan over medium heat. Place a raw tortilla in the pan.
  9. Flip: Cook until bubbles start to form on the surface and the bottom has golden spots (about 45–60 seconds). Flip and cook for another 30 seconds on the other side. Remove and keep warm in a clean towel or tortilla warmer.

Notes

  • Don't Skip the Rest: The resting periods are crucial. If the dough springs back when you try to roll it, let it rest for another 5 minutes.
  • Heat Management: If your tortillas are getting stiff or "cracker-like," your heat is likely too low. You want a high enough heat to cook them quickly so they stay soft.
  • Storage: Store cooled tortillas in an airtight bag in the refrigerator for up to a week. Reheat on a hot pan or in the microwave.
