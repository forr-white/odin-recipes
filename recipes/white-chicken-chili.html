Creamy White Chicken Chili

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • American

If you want comfort food without the heaviness of a beef-and-tomato chili, this is the answer. It gets its richness from cream cheese melted into the broth, which pairs perfectly with the slight heat from the green chiles and taco seasoning. It's hearty enough for a winter dinner but light enough for a weeknight meal prep.

Ingredients

The Chili Base

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts (raw)
  • 1 sweet onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tablespoons taco seasoning
  • 1 can (4 oz) diced green chiles (mild)
  • 1 can (14 oz) fire-roasted diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)

The Creamy Finish

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened to room temperature
  • 1 can (14 oz) pinto beans or Great Northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (frozen or fresh)

Toppings (Optional)

  • Avocado, cilantro, tortilla strips, shredded cheese, lime wedges

Directions

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the butter. Once melted, add the diced onion and sauté until translucent (about 5 minutes). Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  2. Season: Stir in the taco seasoning and cook for 30 seconds to "bloom" the spices. This enhances their flavor significantly.
  3. Simmer Chicken: Add the green chiles, fire-roasted tomatoes (juices included), raw chicken breasts, and chicken broth. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20–25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through (internal temp 165°F).
  4. Shred: Remove the cooked chicken from the pot. Place it in a bowl and shred it using two forks (or a stand mixer paddle attachment). Set aside.
  5. Temper the Cheese (Key Step): In a small bowl, place the softened cream cheese. Ladle about ½ cup of the hot broth from the pot into the bowl. Whisk vigorously until the cream cheese is smooth and liquidy. (Adding the block directly to the pot often results in little white clumps that never melt).
  6. Finish: Pour the smooth cream cheese mixture back into the main pot. Stir well. Add the shredded chicken back in, along with the drained beans and corn.
  7. Final Simmer: Simmer uncovered for 10 minutes to let the chili thicken and flavors meld.
  8. Serve: Ladle into bowls and top with avocado, lime juice, and cilantro.

Notes

  • Rotisserie Hack: Short on time? Skip step 3. Sauté the veggies, add the liquids and cooked rotisserie chicken, and simmer for just 10 minutes before adding the cream cheese.
  • Bean Choice: Pinto beans are traditional here, but white beans (Cannellini or Great Northern) make the chili look "whiter" and creamier.
  • Heat Level: This recipe is mild. To make it spicy, add a diced jalapeño in step 1 or a dash of cayenne pepper.
