Copycat Chuy's Creamy Jalapeño Ranch Dip

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • Tex-Mex

If you have ever been to Chuy's, you know the green sauce is legendary. It’s not quite guacamole, and it’s not quite standard ranch. It is a creamy, addictive, cilantro-lime-jalapeño concoction that people order by the bowlful. This copycat recipe hits all the right notes: tangy, spicy, and incredibly smooth.

  • Food Processor (Essential for breaking down the cilantro and peppers smoothly)
  • Rubber Spatula (To scrape down the sides of the processor bowl)
  • Citrus Juicer (Fresh lime juice makes a huge difference)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup loosely packed fresh cilantro leaves and tender stems
  • ½ cup hot pickled jalapeño chiles, drained (reserve juice)
  • 2 tablespoons pickled jalapeño liquid (brine from the jar)
  • 1 (8 oz) package cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • 3 tablespoons buttermilk ranch seasoning (powdered mix, usually about 1 packet)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, grated or minced
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • For serving: Tortilla chips, fresh jalapeño slices, pickled red onions

Directions

  1. Blitz the Base: Combine cilantro, pickled jalapeños, and the liquid from the jar in a food processor. Pulse until finely chopped, about 10 pulses, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. You want a fine green paste.
  2. Make it Creamy: Add the softened cream cheese, mayonnaise, sour cream, ranch seasoning, lime juice, garlic, and salt.
  3. Blend: Pulse until completely smooth and pale green, about 10–15 pulses.
  4. Chill (Recommended): Pour into a serving bowl. While you can eat it immediately, the flavors meld and the dip thickens slightly if you refrigerate it for an hour first.
  5. Serve: Serve with plenty of tortilla chips.

Notes

  • Spiciness: The heat level depends entirely on your pickled jalapeños. Use "Hot" for the authentic kick, or "Tamed/Mild" for a family-friendly version.
  • Ranch Mix: Make sure to use the dry powder seasoning mix (like Hidden Valley), not prepared bottled dressing.
  • Storage: This dip keeps well in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. It actually tastes better the next day!
