Paloma

Submitted by Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • Mexican

While the Margarita may be the most famous Mexican export, the Paloma is often the preferred choice in Mexico itself. It is simpler, brighter, and more thirst-quenching. The combination of earthy tequila, bitter grapefruit, and tart lime creates a perfectly balanced highball that is dangerous easy to drink.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Blanco (Silver) Tequila
  • 2 oz fresh grapefruit juice (Ruby Red is best)
  • ½ oz fresh lime juice
  • ¼ oz agave nectar (or simple syrup)
  • 2 oz sparkling water (or club soda)
  • Coarse sea salt (for the rim)
  • Ice cubes
  • Grapefruit wedge or lime wheel (for garnish)

Directions

  1. Rim the Glass: Pour coarse salt onto a small plate. Rub a grapefruit or lime wedge around the rim of a highball glass. Dip the wet rim into the salt to coat.
  2. Dissolve Sweetener: In the bottom of the glass (or a separate shaker), combine the grapefruit juice, lime juice, and agave nectar. Stir well until the agave is fully dissolved. (Note: Agave is hard to mix once ice is added).
  3. Build the Drink: Fill the glass with ice. Pour in the tequila.
  4. Top & Finish: Top with the sparkling water. Give it one gentle stir to combine ingredients without killing the carbonation.
  5. Garnish: Add a wedge of fresh grapefruit or lime and serve immediately.

Notes

  • The "Quick" Version: In many Mexican households, this is made simply by mixing tequila, a squeeze of lime, and a grapefruit soda (like Squirt or Jarritos Toronja). If doing this, skip the agave and sparkling water.
  • Tajín Rim: For a spicy kick, mix the rimming salt with a little Tajín or chili powder.
  • Tequila Choice: Use a Blanco (Silver) tequila for a crisp, clean taste. A Reposado will add vanilla/oak notes that make the drink heavier.
