Bulk High-Protein Vegan Potato Soup

Submitted by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • American

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup vegetable oil
  • 6 large yellow onions, diced (about 6 lbs)
  • 8–9 medium carrots, diced (about 3 lbs)
  • 2 bunches celery, diced (about 3 lbs)
  • 12 cloves garlic, minced
  • 18 lbs russet or Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 6 cups dried red lentils, rinsed
  • 22 quarts (5½ gallons) water or vegetable broth (or mix of both)
  • 2 tbsp dried thyme
  • 2 tbsp dried parsley
  • 2 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 8 bay leaves
  • 3 tbsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 8 cups unsweetened oat milk (or soy milk for extra protein)
  • Optional thickener: 1½ cups blended rolled oats or 1½ cups instant mashed potato flakes
  • Optional garnish: chopped parsley, green onion, or nutritional yeast sprinkle

Directions

  1. Prep: Wash, peel, and dice all vegetables before starting. Rinse lentils well.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In large stockpots, heat oil over medium heat. Divide onions, carrots, and celery evenly between pots. Cook until softened, about 10 minutes.
  3. Season: Add garlic, thyme, parsley, and smoked paprika. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add Base: Stir in potatoes, lentils, bay leaves, and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to simmer. Cook 30–35 minutes, stirring occasionally, until potatoes and lentils are tender.
  5. Blend: Remove bay leaves. Use an immersion blender to purée about half of each pot (or mash with a potato masher). This gives a creamy, hearty texture.
  6. Finish: Stir in plant milk and simmer 5 more minutes. Taste and adjust salt and pepper.
  7. Serve: Garnish with parsley or a drizzle of oil. Keep warm for serving.

Notes

  • Affordability: Entire batch costs roughly $25–$30 using bulk produce and dry goods.
  • Texture: Soft and easy to eat — ideal for mixed ages or dental needs.
  • Storage: Holds 4–5 days in the fridge; freezes up to 3 months. Add water or broth when reheating to loosen.
  • Serving Tip: Pair with bread, rolls, or rice for a balanced, filling meal.

Nutritional Information

Serving Size: 50 servings, 1.5 cups each

Calories: 205

Fat: 5 g

Carbohydrates: 30 g

Protein: 10 g
