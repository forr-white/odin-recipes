Baked Jalapeño Poppers

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

appetizers • American

These Baked Jalapeño Poppers are the ultimate crowd-pleaser for game days or parties. Unlike the fried version, these are baked until the peppers are tender and the cheese filling is hot and bubbly. The crispy buttery panko topping gives you that satisfying crunch without the mess of deep-frying.

Recommended Tools

  • Rimmed Baking Sheet (essential for catching any cheese overflow)
  • Disposable Gloves (highly recommended when cutting hot peppers!)
  • Small Mixing Bowls (for the filling and the topping)
  • Paring Knife (for seeding the jalapeños)

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or baking spray
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, room temperature
  • 4 pieces cooked thick-cut bacon, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh chives
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • 10 jalapeños, halved and seeded
  • 2 tablespoons salted butter, melted
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs

Directions

  1. Prep the oven: Preheat the oven to 350°F with a rack in the center position. Grease a rimmed sheet pan with the oil or baking spray.
  2. Make the filling: In a small bowl, combine the Parmesan, cream cheese, bacon, chives, garlic, salt, and pepper. Stir to mix well.
  3. Stuff the peppers: Divide the cream cheese mixture among the jalapeño halves, filling each evenly.
  4. Add the crunch: In a second small bowl, combine the panko and melted butter. Sprinkle the panko mixture over the stuffed jalapeños.
  5. Bake: Place the stuffed jalapeños on the prepared sheet pan, filled sides up. Bake until the filling is bubbling, jalapeños have softened, and panko is golden, about 30 minutes. Serve hot.

Notes

  • Safety First: The oils from jalapeño seeds can burn your skin and eyes. Wear disposable gloves when slicing and seeding them, and do not touch your face!
  • Control the Heat: The heat lives in the seeds and the white membrane inside the pepper. Scrape these out completely for a milder popper, or leave a little bit in for a kick.
  • Make Ahead: You can stuff the peppers earlier in the day and keep them in the fridge. Add the panko topping right before baking.
