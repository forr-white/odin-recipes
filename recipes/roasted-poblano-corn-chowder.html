Roasted Poblano Corn Chowder

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • Mexican

This chowder hits the perfect balance between sweet, savory, and smoky. By roasting the poblano peppers until blistered, we unlock a deep, earthy flavor that pairs perfectly with sweet corn and sharp cheddar. The secret to the texture is blending a portion of the potatoes and broth, creating a thick base without needing a flour roux.

Ingredients

The Peppers

  • 6 large poblano peppers

The Chowder Base

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 medium onions, diced
  • 2 ribs celery, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lb Yukon Gold potatoes, scrubbed and diced into ½-inch chunks
  • 1 bag (16 oz) frozen corn (approx. 3½ cups)
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth

Seasoning

  • 2½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon dried Mexican oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves

The Finish

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded mild or sharp cheddar cheese
  • Garnish: Fresh lime wedges and chopped cilantro

Directions

  1. Roast the Poblanos: Line a baking sheet with foil. Place poblanos on top. Set your oven rack directly underneath the broiler and turn the broiler to high. Broil peppers for 5 minutes, or until skins are blackened and blistered. Flip and broil for another 5 minutes until blackened on all sides.
  2. Steam & Peel: Remove from oven. Immediately cover the baking sheet with foil or plastic wrap to trap the steam. Let sit for 5 minutes (this loosens the skins). Rub off the charred skins, slice open, discard seeds/stems, and dice the peppers. Set aside.
  3. Sauté Vegetables: Melt butter in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add onions, celery, and carrot. Cook for 8 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Simmer: Add potatoes, broth, salt, bay leaves, cumin, coriander, oregano, and black pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer for 10 minutes, or until potatoes are tender.
  5. Blend (The Thickener): Discard bay leaves. Carefully scoop out about 4 cups of the soup (broth and potatoes) and transfer to a blender. (Caution: When blending hot liquids, remove the center cap of the lid and cover with a towel to vent steam). Pulse until smooth.
  6. Combine: Pour the blended mixture back into the pot. Stir in the frozen corn and the diced roasted poblanos. Bring to a simmer.
  7. Finish: Stir in the heavy cream and cheddar cheese. Stir until the cheese has melted and the soup is hot. Simmer for 5 more minutes.
  8. Serve: Ladle into bowls and serve with a squeeze of fresh lime juice and cilantro.

Notes

  • Heat Level: Poblano peppers are generally mild (like a bell pepper with a kick), but occasionally you get a hot one. Removing the seeds and white ribs inside reduces the heat significantly.
  • Potatoes: Yukon Golds are preferred here because they have a buttery texture and thin skins (no peeling required), but Russets will work if you peel them first.
  • Storage: This soup keeps well in the fridge for 3–4 days. Reheat gently on the stove; do not boil rapidly or the dairy may separate.
