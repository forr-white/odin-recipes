Homemade Rolls

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

bread • American

Nothing completes a family dinner like warm, soft yeast rolls. This recipe produces a classic American dinner roll: slightly sweet, incredibly fluffy, and finished with a generous brush of melted butter. While they take some time for the rise, the active work is minimal and the result is far superior to anything in a bag.

Recommended Tools

  • Stand Mixer (Using the dough hook saves your arms from manual kneading)
  • Baking Sheet Pan (A large 13x18 half-sheet is ideal)
  • Silicone Pastry Brush (For that perfect butter finish)

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups whole milk
  • ½ cup + 1 teaspoon white sugar, divided
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1½ teaspoons salt
  • ¼ cup warm water (approx. 110°F)
  • 4½ teaspoons (2 packets) active dry yeast
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour (approximate)
  • 2 large eggs, slightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons salted butter, melted (for brushing)

Directions

  1. Scald Milk: In a medium saucepan over medium-low heat, combine milk, ½ cup sugar, 5 tablespoons butter, and salt. Heat until the butter melts and small bubbles appear around the edges. Remove from heat and let cool to lukewarm. (Crucial: if it is too hot, it will kill the yeast later).
  2. Activate Yeast: While the milk cools, combine the warm water, 1 teaspoon of sugar, and yeast in a small bowl. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until bubbly and frothy. If it doesn't foam, your yeast is dead; start over.
  3. Mix: Add 3 cups of flour to the bowl of a stand mixer. Using the dough hook, pour in the lukewarm milk mixture and mix until combined. Add the yeast mixture and beaten eggs. Mix until incorporated.
  4. Knead: Gradually add the remaining 2 cups of flour, ½ cup at a time, until the dough starts to pull away from the sides of the bowl. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable. Note: You may not need the full 5 cups. Don't over-flour or the rolls will be dry.
  5. First Rise: Cover the bowl with a clean towel and let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free spot until doubled in size (about 1 to 1.5 hours).
  6. Shape: Punch the dough down to release air. Form the dough into 16 uniform balls (about the size of a billiard ball). If the dough is sticky, spray your hands with cooking spray. Place on a large greased baking sheet, spaced 2–3 inches apart.
  7. Second Rise: Cover again and let rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  8. Bake: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Bake for 12–13 minutes. Important: These brown quickly; tent the rolls loosely with foil for the last 5 minutes of baking.
  9. Finish: Immediately after removing from the oven, brush the tops with the 2 tablespoons of melted salted butter.

Notes

  • Flour Tip: Always measure flour by "spooning and leveling" into the cup rather than scooping directly, which packs the flour and makes the rolls heavy.
  • Warmth: If your house is cold, let the dough rise inside the oven (turned OFF) with the oven light on. The light provides just enough warmth.
  • Make Ahead: You can shape the rolls, place them on the pan, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight. Let them sit at room temperature for 1–2 hours the next day before baking.
