Homemade Chili

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

soups • American

This is the quintessential American chili recipe—the kind you want on a cold night or for game day. It hits the perfect balance of savory, sweet, and spicy. We use a touch of sugar to cut through the acidity of the tomatoes, creating a rich, mellow flavor profile that tastes like it simmered all day.

Ingredients

The Base

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 lb ground beef (85/15 recommended)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste

The Spice Blend

  • 2½ tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (white or brown)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1½ teaspoons salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

Liquids & Beans

  • 1½ cups beef broth
  • 1 (15oz) can petite diced tomatoes (undrained)
  • 1 (16oz) can red kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 (8oz) can tomato sauce

Directions

  1. Sauté Onion: Add the olive oil to a large soup pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent.
  2. Brown Beef: Add the ground beef to the pot. Break it apart with a wooden spoon as it cooks. Cook for 6–7 minutes until the beef is fully browned and no pink remains. Drain excess grease if desired.
  3. Bloom Spices: This step is key for flavor. Add the chili powder, cumin, sugar, tomato paste, garlic powder, salt, pepper, and cayenne. Stir well and cook for 1–2 minutes. This "blooms" the spices and caramelizes the tomato paste.
  4. Simmer: Pour in the beef broth, diced tomatoes (with their juice), drained kidney beans, and tomato sauce. Stir well to combine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  5. Cook: Bring the liquid to a low boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer, uncovered, for 20–25 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. The chili will thicken as it simmers.
  6. Rest: Remove the pot from the heat. Let the chili rest for 5–10 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to settle and the texture to thicken further.

Notes

  • Why Sugar? The acidity in canned tomatoes can be harsh. The sugar neutralizes that acid, making the chili taste rounder and savory without actually tasting "sweet."
  • Next-Day Magic: Chili is notoriously better the next day. If you have time, make it a day ahead and reheat it gently.
  • Toppings: Serve with shredded cheddar cheese, sour cream, green onions, and corn chips or cornbread.
  • Bean Variations: Feel free to swap kidney beans for black beans or pinto beans depending on your preference.
