Crockpot Hot Chocolate

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

drinks • American

Forget the powdered packets. This Crockpot Hot Chocolate is incredibly rich, creamy, and decadent. Made with real chocolate bars, sweetened condensed milk, and heavy cream, it’s closer to a drinkable dessert than a standard cocoa. Because it stays warm in the slow cooker, it is absolutely perfect for holiday parties, movie nights, or warming up after playing in the snow.

Ingredients

  • 1 giant Hershey’s milk chocolate bar (approx. 7 oz), broken into pieces
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1.5 cups heavy cream
  • 6 cups whole milk
  • 1–2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 scoops (approx. 2 tbsp) Hershey’s cocoa powder
  • Marshmallows, for topping

Directions

  1. Combine: Add the broken chocolate bar pieces, sweetened condensed milk, heavy cream, whole milk, vanilla extract, and cocoa powder to the crockpot.
  2. Whisk: Whisk well to combine. (The chocolate bar won't be melted yet, so just mix the liquids and powder as best you can).
  3. Cook: Cover and cook on LOW for about 2 hours. Stir occasionally (every 30 minutes or so) to ensure the chocolate melts evenly and doesn't scorch on the bottom.
  4. Serve: Once the mixture is hot, smooth, and creamy, whisk one last time. Ladle into mugs and top with marshmallows.
  5. Keep Warm: Switch the slow cooker to the WARM setting for serving during parties.

Notes

  • Extra Richness: If you love dark chocolate, swap the milk chocolate bar for semi-sweet, or add a handful of chocolate chips.
  • Smooth Texture: If you notice any small clumps of cocoa powder, use an immersion blender right in the pot for 10 seconds to make it silky smooth.
  • Toppings Bar: Set out bowls of whipped cream, crushed peppermint, caramel sauce, sprinkles, and cinnamon sticks for guests to customize their cups.
  • Lighter Version: You can substitute half-and-half for the heavy cream, but do not use skim milk or it will be too thin.
