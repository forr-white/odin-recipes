Chipotle Ranch

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

sauce • American

If you love the "southwest" dressing at your favorite chain restaurant, this is it. It combines the cool, creamy herb profile of classic ranch with the smoky, deep heat of chipotle peppers in adobo. It is incredibly versatile—use it as a salad dressing, a dip for quesadillas, or even a sauce for fish tacos.

  • Rubber Spatula (To get every drop out of the blender)

Ingredients

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • 3 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce (from a can)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • ¾ teaspoon dried parsley
  • ¾ teaspoon dried dill
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 3–6 tablespoons water, depending on your preferred consistency

Directions

  1. Blend: Add the mayonnaise, sour cream, chipotle peppers, lime juice, spices, and herbs to a food processor or blender. Blend on high until the peppers are completely pulverized and the mixture is smooth and pale orange.
  2. Adjust Consistency: Add water, one tablespoon at a time, blending in between additions. For a dip, you may only need 1–2 tablespoons. For a pourable salad dressing, use 3–6 tablespoons.
  3. Season: Taste and add more salt if needed. If you want more heat, blend in another pepper or a teaspoon of the adobo sauce from the can.
  4. Store: Serve immediately or transfer to a bottle or jar and refrigerate. The flavors will deepen after sitting for an hour.

Notes

  • Heat Control: Chipotle peppers can vary in heat. Start with 1 or 2 peppers if you are sensitive to spice, then add more to taste.
  • Adobo Sauce: Don't rinse the peppers! The sauce they are canned in provides a huge amount of flavor.
  • Storage: Keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 10 days. Shake well before using if separation occurs.
