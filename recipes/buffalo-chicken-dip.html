Buffalo Chicken Dip

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

appetizers • American

This Buffalo Chicken Dip is the undisputed king of football parties. It takes all the tangy, spicy satisfaction of hot wings and combines it with cream cheese and cheddar for a scoopable, mess-free snack. It’s creamy, cheesy, and packs just the right amount of heat.

  • Mixing Bowl (large enough to mix the cream cheese and chicken)
  • Rubber Spatula (for spreading the dip evenly)

Ingredients

  • 2 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 2 cups blue cheese crumbles
  • 2 chicken breasts, poached and shredded (or rotisserie chicken)
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese (divided)
  • Frank's RedHot Buffalo Wings Sauce (approx. ½ to ¾ cup, or to taste)

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Soften: Place the cream cheese in a large microwave-safe bowl. Heat in the microwave for 30–60 seconds until soft and easily spreadable.
  3. Mix: Blend in the blue cheese, shredded chicken, 1 cup of the shredded cheddar, and the Buffalo wing sauce. Stir until thoroughly combined.
  4. Assemble: Spread the mixture into a baking dish. Sprinkle the remaining cup of cheddar cheese evenly on top.
  5. Bake: Bake for 30–45 minutes, until the dip is bubbly and the cheese on top is browned.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with celery sticks, carrot sticks, and tortilla chips.

Notes

  • Shortcut: To save time, use a store-bought rotisserie chicken instead of poaching your own breasts. You'll need about 2–3 cups of shredded meat.
  • Blue Cheese Substitute: If you aren't a fan of blue cheese, you can substitute it with Ranch dressing or more cheddar/Monterey Jack cheese, though the blue cheese gives it that authentic "wing" flavor.
  • Sauce: Make sure to use "Buffalo Wing Sauce" and not just plain "Hot Sauce" for the creamier consistency and flavor.
