Ham And Cheese Frittata

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

breakfast • Italian

Think of a frittata as a crustless quiche or a giant, fluffy open-faced omelet. It starts on the stove to sauté the fillings and finishes in the oven for a perfectly set center. It is an excellent way to use up leftover holiday ham and feeds a crowd easily.

Recommended Tools

  • 12-inch Oven-Safe Skillet (Cast iron or high-quality stainless steel works best. Do not use a pan with a plastic handle!)
  • Large Mixing Bowl (You need plenty of room to whisk 12 eggs)
  • Chef's Knife (For dicing the ham and onion)

Ingredients

  • 12 large eggs
  • ½ cup milk (whole milk or half-and-half works best)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cups smoked ham, diced
  • Chopped fresh parsley or green onion (optional garnish)

Directions

  1. Prep: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Dice the onion and smoked ham into uniform pieces.
  2. Sauté: Melt the butter in a 12-inch oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and sauté until they begin to soften and become translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add Ham: Add the diced ham to the skillet and cook for an additional 2–3 minutes to warm it through and get a little color on the edges.
  4. Whisk Eggs: In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper until the yolks and whites are combined. Note: Do not over-beat; just mix until combined.
  5. Combine: Pour the egg mixture over the onions and ham in the skillet (keep the heat on medium). Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese evenly over the top.
  6. Set Edges: Cook on the stovetop for about 5 minutes without stirring. You want the edges to begin to cook and pull away slightly from the sides of the pan.
  7. Bake: Carefully transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Bake for 18–20 minutes. The center should be set (not jiggly) and the top slightly puffed and golden.
  8. Serve: Remove from the oven (handle is hot!). Garnish with fresh parsley or green onions. Slice into wedges and serve warm.

Notes

  • Pan Size Matters: This recipe uses 12 eggs, which requires a 12-inch skillet. If you only have a 10-inch skillet, reduce the recipe to 8–9 eggs, or it will overflow and take much longer to bake.
  • Texture Tip: Do not over-whisk the eggs. Introducing too much air can cause the frittata to puff up rapidly in the oven and then collapse into a dense texture when cooling.
  • Variations: This is a blank canvas. Swap ham for cooked bacon or sausage, or swap cheddar for feta, goat cheese, or Swiss.
