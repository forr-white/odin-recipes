Braised Beef Short Ribs

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

dinner • French

This is the kind of meal that makes your whole house smell incredible. Bone-in beef short ribs are seared to lock in flavor, then slowly braised in a rich Cabernet Sauvignon and herb broth until they are fall-off-the-bone tender. It is an elegant dish that feels restaurant-quality but is actually quite simple to make at home.

  • 6-Qt Dutch Oven (Essential for searing and braising in the same pot)
  • Tongs (For handling the heavy ribs during searing)
  • Fine Mesh Strainer (For creating a silky smooth sauce)
  • Chef's Knife (For dicing the mirepoix vegetables)

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3–4 lbs bone-in short ribs (approx. 8–10 pieces)
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
  • 1 Large Yellow Onion, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups Cabernet Sauvignon (or other dry red wine)
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 1 sprig oregano
  • Optional: 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (for thickening)

Directions

  1. Prep: To start, preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Generously season the meat on all sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Sear: Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a 6-Qt Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the meat in two batches for 5–6 minutes on each side, then transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. Sauté Veggies: Add 1 diced onion to the pot (don't clean it out!) and cook for 8–10 minutes or until translucent and softened. Then add 2 stalks diced celery and 2 diced carrots, and cook for 3–5 minutes more.
  4. Aromatics: Add 3 cloves smashed garlic and 2 tbsp tomato paste. Cook for a few more minutes, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  5. Deglaze: Stir in 2 cups wine, bring to a boil, lower the heat to medium and simmer until it reduces by half, about 15–20 minutes.
  6. Combine: Add 3 cups beef broth and stir. Transfer the meat back to the pot, nestling it into the liquid. Add bay leaves, thyme, and oregano.
  7. Braise: Cover with the lid and place the Dutch oven in the oven. Cook for 2.5–3 hours, or until the ribs are very tender. You can start checking for doneness around 2 hours.
  8. Finish the Sauce: Transfer the meat to a plate and cover loosely with foil to keep warm. Strain the sauce through a fine mesh sieve and discard the cooked vegetables. Pour the liquid back into the pot and simmer on the stove until it thickens.
  9. Thicken (Optional): If you prefer a thicker gravy, you may add a cornstarch slurry (1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water) and simmer for another minute. Serve sauce over ribs.

Notes

  • Make Ahead: Short ribs taste even better the next day! Making them a day ahead also allows the fat to solidify in the fridge, making it easy to skim off before reheating.
  • Serving Suggestions: Serve over creamy mashed potatoes, polenta, or egg noodles to soak up the rich sauce.
  • Wine Choice: Use a wine you would enjoy drinking. Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, or Pinot Noir all work well.
