Soft Cut-Out Sugar Cookies

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

desserts • American

These aren't your average hard, crunchy store-bought cookies. This recipe produces a bakery-style cookie with a soft, tender center and crisp edges. The secret to the flavor is the combination of vanilla and a hint of almond extract. Best of all, this dough holds its shape beautifully in the oven, so your stars don't turn into blobs!

Ingredients

  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, room temperature (softened)
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ¼ to ½ teaspoon almond extract (optional, but highly recommended)
  • Topping: Royal Icing or buttercream

Directions

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: Whisk the flour, baking powder, and salt together in a medium bowl. Set aside.
  2. Cream Butter & Sugar: In a large bowl using a hand or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the softened butter and sugar together on high speed until completely smooth and creamy (about 2 minutes).
  3. Add Wet Ingredients: Add the egg, vanilla, and almond extract. Beat on high speed until combined, about 1 minute. Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl to ensure everything is incorporated.
  4. Combine: Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients. Mix on low speed just until combined. The dough will be relatively soft. (Tip: If the dough seems excessively sticky, add 1 more tablespoon of flour, but try to avoid adding too much or cookies will be tough).
  5. Roll THEN Chill (The Secret): Divide the dough into 2 equal parts. Place each portion onto a piece of lightly floured parchment paper. Dust the top of the dough with flour, place another piece of parchment on top, and roll the dough out to ¼-inch thickness.
    By rolling the dough while it is soft, you avoid having to struggle with a hard disk of cold dough later!
  6. Chill: Stack the rolled-out dough sheets (still in their parchment sandwiches) on a baking sheet. Refrigerate for at least 1–2 hours (or up to 2 days).
  7. Cut: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with fresh parchment paper or silicone mats. Remove one sheet of dough from the fridge. Peel off the top parchment paper. Using cookie cutters, cut shapes and transfer them to the baking sheet. Re-roll scraps and repeat.
  8. Bake: Arrange cookies 3 inches apart. Bake for 11–12 minutes. You want them to look set, but only slightly browned around the very edges. Do not overbake.
  9. Cool: Allow cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes (they are fragile when hot), then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before icing.

Notes

  • Room Temperature: It is vital that your butter and egg are at room temperature. If the butter is too cold, it won't cream with the sugar properly. If it's melted, the cookies will spread.
  • Thickness: Keep the dough at a consistent ¼-inch thickness. If you roll them too thin, they will be crunchy crackers instead of soft cookies.
  • The Flavor: Don't skip the almond extract! Even a tiny amount gives these cookies that signature "wedding cake" flavor found in high-end bakeries.
