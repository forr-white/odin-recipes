Skillet Potatoes

sides • American

Ingredients

  • 2 large russet potatoes, scrubbed and cleaned
  • ½ cup neutral oil, avocado, coconut, or olive oil
  • Salt

Directions

  1. Cut the potatoes into small bite-sized pieces, about ½” thick. Once all the potatoes are cut, use a clean towel to pat the potato pieces completely dry. You don’t want the potatoes to have any excess moisture.
  2. Heat a 10 – 12 inch cast iron skillet over medium heat and add the oil. Let the pan and oil heat up for about 3 – 5 minutes. Place a piece of potato into the skillet and if it starts sizzling it’s ready.
  3. Working in batches, add a handful of the cut potatoes into the hot skillet. Spread evenly so they all have space to cook. Do not overcrowd the skillet with too many potatoes.
  4. Cook for about 7 minutes on the first side, then flip and cook for another 3 – 4 minutes or until the second side is golden brown and the potato is tender.
  5. Remove the crispy potatoes from the pan and place onto a paper towel lined plate or baking sheet to drain the excess oil, and immediately hit with a pinch of salt.
  6. Repeat with the remaining potatoes. This may be about 3 – 4 batches.

Notes

  1. Don’t overcrowd the pan with too many potatoes. If you do it will create steam and not get a crispy potato.
  2. Do not cover the pan with a lid as this creates steam.
  3. Keep the potatoes about ½” thick (not too small, but not too large) so that they will cook up crispy.
