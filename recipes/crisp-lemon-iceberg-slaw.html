Crisp Lemon Iceberg Slaw

Developed by Forrest White & Cook Anything Kitchen

salads • American

Sometimes simple is best. This slaw skips the heavy mayonnaise for a bright, acidic lemon vinaigrette. The crunch of the iceberg lettuce makes it the perfect palate cleanser alongside rich foods like barbecue, fried fish, or heavy tacos. It is light, crisp, and ready in 5 minutes.

  • Chef's Knife (The best tool for fine shredding)
  • Citrus Juicer (Gets the most juice out of your lemon)
  • Large Mixing Bowl (You need plenty of room to toss without spilling)

Ingredients

  • 1 head iceberg lettuce, shredded
  • Juice of 1 lemon (approx. 2–3 tbsp)
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1–2 tbsp white vinegar
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Directions

  1. Prep: Remove any wilted or damaged outer leaves from the iceberg lettuce. Cut the head in half and remove the core.
  2. Shred: Place the lettuce cut-side down and slice it very thinly to create fine shreds.
  3. Combine: Transfer the shredded lettuce to a large mixing bowl.
  4. Make Dressing: In a separate small bowl or jar, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, white vinegar, salt, and pepper until emulsified.
  5. Toss: Pour the dressing over the shredded lettuce just before you are ready to eat. Toss gently until the slaw is evenly coated.
  6. Serve: Taste and add more salt or lemon if needed. Serve immediately for maximum crunch.

Notes

  • Serve Immediately: Iceberg lettuce wilts quickly once dressed. Do not dress the salad until the very last minute.
  • Extra Crunch: Add thinly sliced green onions, shaved radishes, or julienned cucumbers for more texture.
  • More Tang: If you love acid, increase the vinegar or add a small splash of apple cider vinegar.
  • Herb Upgrade: Add fresh chopped dill, parsley, or chives for brightness.
