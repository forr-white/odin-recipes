Homemade Corn Tortillas

Developed by Cook Anything Kitchen

bread • Mexican

Fresh corn tortillas are a revelation compared to the dry, crumbly versions found at the grocery store. Using "Maseca" (instant corn masa flour), you can create soft, pliable tortillas that puff up beautifully on the griddle. The secret is the moisture level; the dough should feel like Play-Doh—smooth and not sticking to your hands.

Recommended Tools

  • Cast Iron Tortilla Press (The easiest way to get perfectly thin, uniform tortillas)
  • Comal or Cast Iron Skillet (Provides the steady, high heat needed for the characteristic "char")
  • Tortilla Warmer (Or a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft with steam)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Maseca (Instant Corn Masa Flour)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1⅓ cups warm water

Directions

  1. Mix Masa: In a large bowl, combine the Maseca and salt. Slowly add the warm water while mixing with your hand. Knead for a minute or two until the dough comes together. It should feel like modeling clay—smooth and not sticking to your fingers.
  2. Rest: Wrap the dough ball tightly in plastic wrap or cover with a damp towel. Allow it to rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes (up to 2 hours). This hydrates the flour and prevents cracking.
  3. Prep Press: Cut a plastic zip-top bag into two squares (or use parchment paper) to line the tortilla press. This is vital so the dough doesn't stick to the metal.
  4. Shape: Pinch off pieces of dough and roll them into 1-inch balls (about the size of a golf ball).
  5. Press: Place a dough ball in the center of the lined press. Press down firmly, rotate the tortilla 180 degrees, and press gently again to ensure even thickness.
  6. Cook: Heat a skillet or comal over medium-high heat. Carefully peel the tortilla off the plastic and lay it on the hot surface.
  7. The Three-Flip Rule:
    • Cook for 30 seconds (until edges look slightly dry); flip.
    • Cook for 60 seconds (until brown spots appear); flip.
    • Cook for 30 more seconds while gently pressing the center with a spatula—it should puff up with steam!
  8. Steam: Place finished tortillas immediately into a warmer or wrap in a clean towel. The steam is what makes them truly soft and pliable.

Notes

  • Troubleshooting: If the tortillas are cracking at the edges when you press them, the dough is too dry; knead in 1 more tablespoon of water. If they stick to the plastic, the dough is too wet; add a little more Maseca.
  • Warm Water: Using warm (not cold) water helps the starch in the corn flour absorb more effectively.
  • Reheating: If they get dry, splash them with a tiny bit of water and reheat on a hot skillet for 15 seconds per side.
